In Year Two of A2Zero implementation, Ann Arbor passed Michigan's first commercial benchmarking ordinance, requiring large commercial and multifamily buildings to track and report on building energy and water performance ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The first reporting deadline was June 1, and benchmarking transparency is anticipated to yield average energy savings of 2–3% per year ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
