On November 4, 2019, the Ann Arbor City Council passed a resolution declaring a climate emergency and committing to take action as a result of that declaration ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The resolution directed the City Administrator to develop a draft '2030 Carbon Neutral Ann Arbor Plan' to be presented to Council no later than March 31, 2020, in support of an Earth Day 2020 presentation ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Council also directed the City Administrator to design and execute a community engagement process and to seek collaboration with the University of Michigan and its President's Commission on Carbon Neutrality ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The resolution was sponsored by Mayor Taylor and Councilmembers Bannister, Smith, Ramlawi, Grand, Ackerman, Eaton, Nelson, and Griswold ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
