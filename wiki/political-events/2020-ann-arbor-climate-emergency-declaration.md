The Ann Arbor City Council unanimously declared a climate emergency and directed the City to immediately begin crafting an aggressive and living strategy to achieve community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). This declaration was made in the context of recognized local climate impacts including increased precipitation, rising temperatures, greater weather volatility, and anticipated disruptions to public health, safety, and the local economy ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
