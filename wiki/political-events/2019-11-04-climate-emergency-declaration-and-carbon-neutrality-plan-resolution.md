  2030 Carbon Neutrality Plan (2019-11-04)

On November 4, 2019, the Ann Arbor City Council unanimously passed a resolution declaring a climate emergency, stating that climate change is one of the most important issues of our time and that responding to the climate crisis necessitates a mobilization on par with those activated during times of disaster ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The declaration was made in the context of recognized local climate impacts including increased precipitation, rising temperatures, greater weather volatility, and anticipated disruptions to public health, safety, and the local economy ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). In passing the resolution, the Council committed to charting a path for how the entire Ann Arbor community could achieve carbon neutrality by the year 2030, directing the City to immediately begin crafting an aggressive and living strategy to that end ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The resolution directed the City Administrator to develop a draft '2030 Carbon Neutral Ann Arbor Plan' to be presented to Council no later than March 31, 2020, in support of an Earth Day 2020 presentation ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Council also directed the City Administrator to design and execute a community engagement process and to seek collaboration with the [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]] and its President's Commission on Carbon Neutrality toward shared carbon neutrality goals ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The resolution was sponsored by Mayor Taylor and Councilmembers Bannister, Smith, Ramlawi, Grand, Ackerman, Eaton, Nelson, and Griswold ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
