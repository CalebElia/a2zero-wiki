The [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|Ann Arbor Housing Commission]] is a government body affiliated with the [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City of Ann Arbor]] responsible for public housing and managing affordable housing sites in the city ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The Commission hosted an A2Zero presentation and discussion at its meeting on January 15, 2020, and co-hosted a community outreach event at [[locations/bryant-community-center|Bryant Community Center]] on February 19, 2020, as part of the A2Zero equity engagement effort ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). In Year Two of A2Zero (2021–2022), the Commission worked with the City to secure $2.5 million in federal funding to support energy, comfort, and safety improvements and to transition numerous affordable housing sites to net zero energy, as well as to secure bids for solar installation at multiple sites ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
