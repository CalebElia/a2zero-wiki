The Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI) was created in FY19 to actualize the sustainability goals created and reaffirmed by Ann Arbor City Council ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). OSI developed a 5-year work plan aimed at achieving a 25% reduction in community-wide greenhouse gas emissions by 2025 relative to 2000 levels, and held funding in its FY2020 budget for the public engagement process that would lead to the A2Zero plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The office is responsible for leading implementation of the A2ZERO climate action plan, adopted by City Council in June 2020 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]).

In Year One of A2ZERO, OSI managed programs spanning renewable energy, building electrification, energy efficiency, transportation, materials and waste, resilience, and community engagement ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). OSI also engaged externally by participating in Michigan Public Service Commission working groups, serving on the University of Michigan President's Commission on Carbon Neutrality, joining committees developing the MI Healthy Climate Plan, and serving on the national SolSmart Advisory Committee ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]; [[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The office operates a public-facing presence including a Farmer's Market stall, YouTube channel, and Sustainability Forum series ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]).

By Year Three, the OSI team comprised 13 named staff members: Missy, Zach, Julie, Sean, Thea, Simi, Hannah, Joe, Sheronda, Bryce, Jennifer, Corissa, and Ryan ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The office secured a $229,000 AmeriCorps grant to bring 10 AmeriCorps members to the office to support sustainability engagement and education ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). OSI received CDP A-List recognition for the second consecutive year as one of 122 cities and counties globally taking bold leadership on environmental action, and earned the Michigan Green Communities Gold Level award ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). In Year Three, OSI advanced major renewable energy milestones including facilitating 4MW+ of rooftop solar through Solarize Ann Arbor, commissioning an Energy Options Analysis for the SEU, and submitting testimony in four MPSC utility rate cases ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). OSI staff were also recognized by the Great Lakes Renewable Energy Association with the Exemplary Partnership Award for their state-wide, regional, and national engagement around the Solarize Ann Arbor model ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). Three OSI staff graduated from Champions for Change in Year Three, bringing the total to five staff who have completed this racial equity program, and OSI staff joined the Southeast Michigan Council of Governments (SEMCOG) Green Task Force to advance sustainability across the region ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).

OSI serves as the primary caretaker of A2ZERO, conducting the annual community-wide greenhouse gas inventory and using it to guide program design and track progress toward the 2030 carbon neutrality goal ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). As of Year Three, community-wide GHG emissions had been reduced by over 22% relative to the baseline, reflecting the cumulative impact of OSI-led programs across all seven A2ZERO strategies ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
