The [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] (OSI) is the City of Ann Arbor government office responsible for launching and leading the A2Zero initiative ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). OSI designed A2Zero to be iterative, transparent, failure-positive, and grounded in justice and equity ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

The Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI) is the City of Ann Arbor government office responsible for leading the A2Zero planning process. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) OSI mobilized dozens of community partners and volunteers, thousands of members of the public, dozens of technical advisors, and peer communities to provide input into the A2Zero Carbon Neutrality Plan over a four-month period beginning November 13, 2019. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI) designed the A2Zero planning process around five pillars: public engagement, technical advisory committees, partner support, staff expertise, and peer learning and literature review ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). OSI staff hosted 68 public events between November 13, 2019 and March 12, 2020 as part of the public engagement pillar ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). OSI also created the A2Zero website, a listserv, a meeting-in-a-box toolkit, and hired a communications firm to brand the City's carbon neutrality work ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

The Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI) is the party responsible for implementing all four actions under Strategy 1, including Community Choice Aggregation, the Onsite Renewables Bulk Buy, the Community Solar Program, and the Landfill Solar Project. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) OSI's Solarize Coordinator leads the Onsite Renewables Bulk Buy with support from the broader OSI team. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) OSI also intends to bundle energy efficiency and electrification support with the bulk buy program so that households and businesses receive a comprehensive package rather than separate programmatic offerings. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])

The [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] (OSI) is the party responsible for implementing all four actions under the Other Actions strategy, including equity programs, the Sustaining Ann Arbor Together grant program, the internal carbon price, and greenhouse gas offsets. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) OSI is committed to ensuring climate protection and mitigation work improves the lives of Ann Arbor residents who have been historically underrepresented and under-resourced. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The Office of Sustainability and Innovations is the City of Ann Arbor government office responsible for creating the A2Zero Living Carbon Neutrality Plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Staff within the office developed the Plan as a foundation for achieving community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The office grounded the Plan in equity principles, with the goal of ensuring the most vulnerable residents can thrive as the community addresses climate change ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI) was created in FY19 to actualize the climate goals created and reaffirmed by Ann Arbor City Council ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). OSI developed a 5-year work plan aimed at achieving a 25% reduction in community-wide greenhouse gas emissions by 2025 relative to 2000 levels ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Funding for public engagement related to the 2030 carbon neutrality planning process was available in the FY2020 OSI budget ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
