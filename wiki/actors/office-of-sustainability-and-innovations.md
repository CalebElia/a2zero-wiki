The Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI) was created in FY19 to actualize the sustainability goals created and reaffirmed by Ann Arbor City Council ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). OSI developed a 5-year work plan aimed at achieving a 25% reduction in community-wide greenhouse gas emissions by 2025 relative to 2000 levels, and held funding in its FY2020 budget for the public engagement process that would lead to the A2Zero plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The office is responsible for leading implementation of the A2ZERO climate action plan, adopted by City Council in June 2020 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]).

In Year One of A2ZERO, OSI managed programs spanning renewable energy, building electrification, energy efficiency, transportation, materials and waste, resilience, and community engagement ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). OSI also engaged externally by participating in Michigan Public Service Commission working groups, serving on the University of Michigan President's Commission on Carbon Neutrality, and joining committees developing the MI Healthy Climate Plan ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). The office operates a public-facing presence including a Farmer's Market stall, YouTube channel, and Sustainability Forum series ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]).
