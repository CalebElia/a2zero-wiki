[[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] is the primary electric utility supplying electricity to Ann Arbor and surrounding communities, and serves as the existing utility provider under A2Zero Strategy 1 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). In 2022, over 54% of DTE Electric's fuel mix used to supply Ann Arbor was generated using coal, while 13% was generated from renewables and an additional 2% was purchased as renewable energy ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). Michigan state law requires DTE to meet a 15% renewable portfolio standard ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).

DTE is a named collaborator on both the Community Solar Program and the Landfill Solar Project ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Under the CCA model, DTE would continue to provide transmission and distribution services even as the City procures power from an alternative supplier ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). For the Landfill Solar Project, DTE is a co-developer alongside the City and Pittsfield Township, with grid upgrade costs at the point of intersection still to be determined ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The City of Ann Arbor has also intervened in multiple Michigan Public Service Commission cases involving DTE, including U-20836, to challenge rate increases and protect the interests of residents with solar installations ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
