The [[actors/ann-arbor-area-transit-authority|Ann Arbor Area Transit Authority]] (AAATA), also known as [[actors/ann-arbor-area-transit-authority|TheRide]], serves as the regional public transit provider serving Ann Arbor and surrounding communities and is the party responsible for implementing both the [[initiatives/expand-improve-local-transit|Expand and Improve Local Transit]] and [[initiatives/expand-improve-regional-transit|Expand and Improve Regional Transit]] initiatives under A2Zero Strategy 4 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). AAATA is tasked with quadrupling current bus ridership, building bus rapid transit along major corridors, and adding 2 new electric buses per year for 10 years ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The A2Zero plan identifies AAATA as a key partner for bus fleet electrification, route expansion, and rapid transit development along major corridors, and also proposes expanding the Go-Pass program area and improving bus stop infrastructure with solar-powered shelters as part of the authority's role in the city's carbon neutrality strategy ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The authority is also expected to work with the Regional Transit Authority of Southeast Michigan to establish regional transit connections to Detroit and surrounding municipalities by 2021 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

In Year 3 of A2Zero, the City supported an AAATA propulsion study to identify solutions for achieving carbon-free busing ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The community also passed a millage to support TheRide in improving and expanding public transit, effective 2024 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
