The Great Lakes Renewable Energy Association (GLREA) is a regional nonprofit organization focused on advancing renewable energy in the Great Lakes region ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). In Year Two of A2Zero (2021–2022), the association awarded the proposed [[initiatives/sustainable-energy-utility|Sustainable Energy Utility (SEU)]] its Project of the Year award, and City staff member [[actors/galen-hardy|Galen Hardy]] was appointed to its board ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The City also developed a Solarize Toolkit in collaboration with GLREA to guide replication of the Solarize model beyond Washtenaw County, and jointly launched the [[initiatives/ann-arbor-solar-stories|Ann Arbor Solar Stories]] initiative to amplify resident voices about the value of residential solar ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
