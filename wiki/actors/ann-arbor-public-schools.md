[[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]] was one of 67 organizations that signed up as an A2Zero partner organization by early March 2020 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] sent 11,000 backpack fliers to all K-5th grade students in Ann Arbor Public Schools as part of A2Zero outreach ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
[[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]] is listed as a collaborator and project co-designer for the Sustaining Ann Arbor Together Grant Program, which provides small grants to schools among other recipients. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
Ann Arbor Public Schools (AAPS) is identified in the A2Zero plan as a partner institution for incorporating plant-based diet programs and collaborating on food planning, purchasing, and waste reduction ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan also proposes composting at schools and local food at schools as community and education actions ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
