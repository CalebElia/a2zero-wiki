The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor-planning-department|City of Ann Arbor Planning Department]] plays a central role in advancing [[strategies/strategy-4-vmt-reduction|Strategy 4]] of the A2Zero plan by reforming land-use regulations to reduce car dependency ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The department is responsible for implementing both the [[initiatives/increase-diversity-of-housing|Increase Diversity of Housing]] and [[initiatives/develop-mixed-use-neighborhoods|Develop Mixed-Use Neighborhoods]] initiatives under A2Zero Strategy 4, with support from the Planning Commission and City Council ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The department is tasked with completing a Comprehensive Master Plan Update by 2021 and adopting corresponding zoning code amendments by 2024 to enable mixed-use development and increased housing density ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Planning staff are also expected to review the zoning code in 2028 and make suggestions to Council for further changes ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

During Year Two, the department collaborated with A2Zero to integrate climate goals into the Unified Development Code, co-developed a transportation carbon calculator for plan review, and removed parking minimums from plan review requirements ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
