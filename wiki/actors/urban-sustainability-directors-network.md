The Urban Sustainability Directors Network (USDN) is a peer learning network connecting sustainability directors across U.S. cities ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). It is identified as a collaborator and project co-designer for the [[initiatives/advance-equity-programs|Advance Equity Programs]] initiative, supporting the [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] in embedding equity across its climate programs ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). In Year Three, USDN provided two grants to the City of Ann Arbor: a $20,000 Emergent Learning Fund grant for equitable community engagement around the circular economy and a $15,200 mini-grant to support the Aging in Place Efficiently program ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
