The State of Michigan awarded $54,000 to the City of Ann Arbor to support the [[initiatives/utility-pole-ev-charging-pilot|Utility-Pole EV Charging Pilot]], secured during Year Two of A2Zero (2021–2022) ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). By Year 3, the funded chargers were reported as the City's first curbside EV chargers, described as the state's first utility pole EV charging program, with installation targeted for fall 2023 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). This single award is tracked across both annual reports — the Year 2 report captures the award itself, and the Year 3 report captures its installation progress under the broader [[initiatives/ev-charger-deployment|EV Charger Deployment]] initiative.
