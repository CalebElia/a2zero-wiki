The federal government awarded $2,500,000 to the [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|Ann Arbor Housing Commission]] to support energy, comfort, and safety improvements at Ann Arbor Housing Commission sites ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). This funding is intended to transition numerous affordable housing sites to net zero energy, improving comfort, affordability, and sustainability ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The funding was secured during Year Two of A2Zero (2021–2022), though the specific federal funder is not identified in the source document ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
