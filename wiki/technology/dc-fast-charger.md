Direct Current Fast Chargers (DCFCs) are planned for deployment in Ann Arbor starting in 2022 to serve ride hailing companies, car sharing services, and the general public ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The A2Zero plan assumes 2 DCFCs will be installed per year, both paid for through utility rebates, as part of the broader goal of equipping 2% of all public and private parking spaces with DCFC infrastructure ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). DCFCs complement the larger level 2 charger rollout of 1,250 units per year, with new chargers prioritized for low-income areas and multifamily housing residents who lack home charging access ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
