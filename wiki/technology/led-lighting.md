LED lighting is being deployed by the City of Ann Arbor to replace conventional streetlights and traffic signals, with energy savings of up to 70% for streetlights and 85% for traffic signals compared to conventional technology ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). A key barrier is that approximately two-thirds of Ann Arbor's streetlights are currently owned by [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]], requiring the City to negotiate and purchase these assets before conversion can proceed ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The initiative targets replacement of 4,467 streetlights and all traffic signals, with full conversion projected to be complete by 2029 at a total cost of $3,100,000 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The projected GHG reduction potential is 2,600 MTCO²e, representing 0.1% of community-wide emissions ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
