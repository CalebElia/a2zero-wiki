Community Choice Aggregation is a procurement mechanism that allows local governments to aggregate electricity demand and purchase power from alternative suppliers while the existing utility continues to provide transmission and distribution services ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). As an opt-out program, CCA achieves economies of scale that can result in cost parity with existing electricity rates, making it accessible to the entire community including renters and low-income residents who cannot install their own renewable systems ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Michigan does not currently have CCA enabling legislation, making state legislative action the primary barrier to implementation in Ann Arbor ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
