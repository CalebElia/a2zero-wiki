Solar-powered battery storage was deployed at the Northside Community Center as part of Ann Arbor's first A2ZERO resilience hub, providing up to three days of backup power in case of a grid outage ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). This deployment demonstrates the use of paired solar and storage systems to enhance community resilience in underserved neighborhoods ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]).

Battery storage systems are also proposed as a core component of Ann Arbor's [[initiatives/sustainable-energy-utility|Sustainable Energy Utility (SEU)]], which would pair local solar installations with battery storage on homes and businesses to deliver 100% clean, reliable electricity ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). As of Year 2, the SEU concept including battery storage was in the proposal stage ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
