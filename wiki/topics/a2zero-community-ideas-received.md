The A²ZERO plan was built from a community ideation process that generated far more ideas than the 44 that became formal actions. This page documents the complete unfiltered list from Appendix 5, organized by the original topic structure. Many items listed here did become formal plan actions; others were considered but not adopted due to feasibility, impact, or jurisdictional constraints. The full list is preserved here because ideas not adopted in 2020 may become viable as technology, policy, or political conditions change — a living-plan principle. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])

## Energy

### Energy Production
- Regional methane digester
- Solar energy on greenbelt
- County/city energy efficiency and renewable energy concierge service
- Create microgrids
- Community solar on non-city property (University of Michigan, municipal, AAPS)
- Bulk-buy purchasing for renewable energy, energy efficiency, and EVs
- Target high-energy "districts" with renewables, efficiency, electrification (residential, commercial)
- Change solar incentives
- Create volunteer solar opportunities

### Energy Efficiency
- New building requirements: energy efficiency, renewables
- Net zero affordable housing
- Reduce property taxes based on energy efficiency upgrades
- Develop carbon neutral districts (zero energy zones, 2030 districts)
- DTE Energy on-bill financing for energy efficiency improvements
- **Geothermal districts** *(not adopted in 2020; has since become a key emerging technology)*
- Require green/white roofs
- Energy disclosure policies (rental/single-family/commercial)

### Electrification
- Require all existing appliances and HVAC systems be electrified
- Increase grid capacity for electrification

### Financing
- Create financing opportunities/loan loss reserve
- Create carbon tax
- Create on-bill financing for renewable energy and energy efficiency projects

### Sequestration
- Increase carbon sequestration practices in Ann Arbor

### Offsets
- Offset programs

### Education, Outreach, and Zoning
- Youth education on carbon neutrality
- Information sessions on energy efficiency, electrification, renewables, and EVs for different sectors
- Spread information about DTE Energy's existing energy efficiency programs
- Increase awareness of existing programs
- Change zoning laws to allow for more density
- Expedite permitting for more efficient construction
- Create more zoning opportunities for solar

## Mobility

### Motor Vehicle Electrification
- Require new construction to include EV charging stations; expand existing charging
- Allow homeowners to make chargers available to others
- Encourage/support electrifying car-sharing; implement shared-use program (City)
- Incentivize/convert fleets to EV (City, car-sharing, ride-hailing, commercial delivery)
- Create a total cost of ownership target commitment for EVs
- Pair EV charging stations with solar power and storage
- Set up EV bulk buy program (individuals, institutions, employers)
- Subsidize EVs and charging for low-income households
- Organize buyback program for ICE vehicles
- Encourage pilot/autonomous vehicle programs and electric delivery options
- Reduce congestion at chargers via rotating system
- Battery recycle program (utility batteries → school and public buses)
- Hub deliveries to downtown (smaller green vehicles inside downtown)
- DTE Energy: Coordinate Workplace Charging Challenge; leverage DTE rebates
- Require/encourage electrifying lawn equipment

### Active Transportation
- E-bike and e-scooter program for short-distance travel
- Expand sidewalks and crosswalks in currently unwalkable areas
- Maintain and repair bike lanes preferentially (including gravel clearing, snow)
- More bike lanes; improved bike access to downtown; increased regional/connected bikeways
- Protected bike lanes or elevated bikeways; greenways
- Expand Common Cycle volunteer bike repair clinic
- Encourage bike banks/bike sharing
- City snow clearing of sidewalks
- Allow bikes inside buses (transit connection)
- Enforce codes protecting bikes and bike lanes
- Traffic signals that reflect active transport needs; safer crosswalk lighting
- Add skywalks between major buildings

### Transit and Paratransit
- Make public transportation free
- Expand Go-Pass area; reduce transit fees; annual pass deals
- Expand TheRide bus routes and connect routes
- Increase speed and efficiency of bus transit; rapid transit along major corridors
- Better technology to make bus system more accurate and on-time
- Solar-powered bus stop shelters (security lighting, cameras, info)
- Create bus and carpool lanes
- Student shuttles to reduce demand for student cars; UM transit collaboration
- Develop light rail (commuter, local); electrified regional public transit
- Electrify TheRide vehicles
- "Right size" transit vehicles
- Create "mobility hubs" (e-bikes, e-scooters, zipcars, bus stops, EV chargers, etc.)
- Improve public school transit
- Increase availability of Park and Ride lots for commuters
- Allow multiple modes of fare payment
- Encourage alternatives to flying (trains, etc.)

### Policies, Funding, and Education
- "Upzone" for density and affordability; mixed-use neighborhoods; transit-oriented planning
- Build affordable workforce housing near workplaces to reduce commuting
- Incentivize work-from-home, 4-day-work-week, and shorter work day
- Reduce parking availability; eliminate parking minimums; tax vacant lots/new parking
- Enact car-free (or petrol-free) zones; pedestrian-only downtown on weekends
- Implement odometer tax or congestion tax; City gasoline tax; tax large industries
- Enforce no idling laws
- Alter Act 51 to give locals more control over road infrastructure funding
- Lobby to eliminate state registration fees for EVs
- Increase education about biking and walking through middle and high school
- Certification/incentive program for auto dealers and maintenance shops selling/servicing EVs
- Leverage Forth Mobility for education and outreach
- Health-care systems educate patients about benefits of active transportation

### Transportation Strategies
- Adopt transportation pyramid (walking priority over single-occupancy driving)
- Permeable pavement
- One-way street design
- Encourage carpooling for larger companies using apps
- Spotlight organizations taking action; provide sample sites and working examples
- Help people understand carbon footprint related to transportation

## Resource Reduction

### Waste Reduction
- Conduct environmentally-extended input-output analysis (City, University, hospitals)
- Create a Zero Waste Plan
- Require all city-wide events be Zero Waste
- Discourage landfilled waste (extended pickup intervals, pay-by-amount model, container limits)
- Reduce single-use plastics (bans, taxes, bottle-filling stations, reusable container incentives)
- Electrify waste truck fleet
- Choose/require methane recovery at landfills in active use

### Recycling
- Renovate or develop city-owned Materials Recovery Facility
- Educate public and resolve recycling uncertainty
- Eliminate drop-off fees for recycling
- Multi-stream recycling pickup
- Neighborhood-level recycling rather than drop-off only
- Improve textile recycling
- Enforce recycling

### Organics
- Improve composting pickup (accessible to commercial, industrial, multi-family; year-round)
- Local composting (neighborhood-scale; return to local farmers; free compost to residents)
- Encourage/incentivize homeowner composting
- Address wastewater; methane recovery on wastewater

### Plant-Based Diets
- Scale pledge programs; Blue Zone Program
- Incorporate AAPS and institutions
- Cooking classes focused on plant-based, local, seasonal foods
- Education/ban on commodity beef/CAFO meat

### Local Food Production
- Scale community gardens and local food production
- Local food waste recovery (gardeners, farms, restaurants, grocery stores)
- Farmers Market expansion (year-round, more locations)
- Expanded food hubs/local food in grocery stores
- Protect SNAP at appropriate scales
- Pilot local food subscription meals with circular/reusable packaging
- Collaborate with AAPS, UM on food planning, purchasing, and waste

### Circular Economy
- Reskilling and skill-sharing opportunities
- Repair centers for bikes, appliances, etc.
- Toy and tool library/shed; shared play areas
- Support thrift stores, reuse centers, flea markets
- Create a time bank/local currency

### Sustainable Purchasing
- Charge a carbon price on embedded GHG of products
- Create local market for reusable goods
- Closed-loop reusable packaging
- Offer refillable bulk containers and takeout containers at point of sale
- Create/enforce purchasing policies for municipal operations, institutions, and individuals

### Construction and Development
- Require sustainable materials in new development

## Adaptation and Resilience

### Preparation
- Develop new/leverage existing infrastructure as resilience hubs
- Activate some homes as shelters with resources and energy
- Identify safe gathering points in neighborhoods during power outages, extreme heat/cold
- Conduct needs mapping of neighborhoods based on resident support requirements
- Generate neighborhood-based resiliency plans
- Activate neighborhood climate ambassadors/captains
- Enable/organize ongoing training via Red Cross/CERT/partners
- Coordinate across Red Cross/AmeriCorps/partners
- Develop redundant communication (e.g., Code Red) not dependent on internet
- Assist/organize preparing and distributing residential emergency kits
- Host a National Preparedness Month campaign
- Conduct risk assessments and incorporate into planning
- Strengthen relationships with other local governments

### Community Cohesion
- Activate strong neighborhood community networks
- Activate neighborhood welcome ambassadors
- Educate community on expected climate risks and how to prepare
- Increase local workshops and space for public input
- Youth participation through actions, videos, ambassadors, curriculum, training
- Develop program to recognize neighborhood-level efforts
- Enable/organize block parties for energy/resilience themes
- Provide emotional resilience and psychological support services
- Create shared childcare programs and communal kitchens

### Response
- Develop climate refugee support networks
- Develop program to use city buses for evacuation and power sources during disaster

### Urban Planning
- Implement sensors and data to monitor heat, air quality, waterways, and floods
- Urban planning to moderate heat via shade trees, lighter surfaces, rain gardens
- Design for increased urban density and walkability/bikeability; update zoning
- Street trees: shading analysis, inventory, master plan update, private tree planting incentives
- Improve parking lot design standards for shading, stormwater, heat island mitigation
- Incentivize white/green roofs
- Purchase repetitive-loss properties; redevelop vacant space as green space
- Prioritize affordable housing

### Energy (Resilience Context)
- Transition to underground power lines
- Create redundancy in energy systems (storage)
- Decentralize energy infrastructure; incentivize individual energy production
- Increase renewable energy systems, storage, microgrids, and community solar
- Plan for higher energy loads due to increasing temperatures

### Green Spaces
- Connect green spaces
- Increase natural area preservation and stewardship for carbon sequestration and stormwater
- Educate on native and invasive plants
- Increase land use for local food production
- Encourage fruit trees/foraging; allow food production on city lands in neighborhoods

### Water
- Enable/encourage water generation, reuse, and natural water/flood capture systems
- Design flexible water systems that adapt to conditions
- Remove dams/coordinate dam management
- Add vegetated buffers along waterways for stormwater management
- Stricter floodway/floodplain management and planning

### Transportation (Resilience Context)
- Design roads for flexibility, alternative transportation, stormwater management, and durability
- Leverage Common Cycle to keep bikes on the road
- Encourage/enable car sharing
- Install EV chargers at stores and apartments

### Residential/Commercial Support
- Define and educate on residential best management practices
- Create a resilience fund focused on equity
- Educate on DTE Energy support for energy efficiency upgrades
