The A2Zero Annual Report 2021–2022 is the second annual progress report on Ann Arbor's community-wide carbon neutrality plan, originally adopted by City Council in June 2020. Produced by the [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City of Ann Arbor]]'s [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI)]], the report covers the second year of plan implementation and is structured around the same seven strategies as the original Climate Action Plan. It functions simultaneously as an accountability document — cataloguing what was accomplished, what fell short, and what was learned — and as a forward-looking planning instrument, closing with a set of Year 3 priorities.

The report is organized strategy by strategy, with each section presenting a bulleted list of Year 2 accomplishments accompanied by one or more 'Dive Deeper' sidebars that provide narrative context on flagship initiatives. The seven strategies covered are: (1) powering the electric grid with 100% renewable energy, (2) switching appliances and vehicles from fossil fuels to electric, (3) improving building energy efficiency, (4) reducing vehicle miles traveled by at least 50%, (5) changing how materials are used and disposed of, (6) enhancing community resilience, and (7) cross-cutting engagement, equity, and institutional programs. A final section outlines Year 3 priorities across all strategies.

Year 2 saw significant programmatic scaling across multiple fronts. The Solarize program crossed 3 MW of installed residential solar and 430 participating households. Michigan's first [[initiatives/commercial-benchmarking-ordinance|commercial benchmarking ordinance]] was passed. The City achieved a 20% [[initiatives/municipal-fleet-electrification|municipal fleet electrification]] rate and won awards to bring two electric refuse trucks into service. The [[initiatives/a2zero-collaborators-network|A2Zero Collaborators network]] grew to over 110 organizations. The City also secured over $8 million in grants from federal, state, and philanthropic sources — including $4.5 million from the American Rescue Plan Act for solar and energy savings at City facilities and $2.5 million for affordable housing energy improvements ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2]]).

The report also documents significant institutional recognition: the City was named to CDP's 'A List' of 95 communities worldwide for climate transparency and action, received a gold-level designation from Michigan Green Communities, and multiple OSI staff members received individual awards. Year 3 priorities include breaking ground on the [[initiatives/wheeler-center-solar-park|Wheeler Center Solar Park]], expanding County-wide climate action in collaboration with regional stakeholders, continuing to investigate pathways to 100% renewable energy including both the Sustainable Energy Utility and traditional municipalization, creating Ann Arbor's second and third [[initiatives/resilience-hubs|resilience hubs]], and launching a metrics dashboard to transparently demonstrate progress toward the 2030 carbon neutrality goal ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2]]).
