The A2Zero Year Three Annual Report covers the period July 1, 2022 through June 2023 (the source document header reads 'JUNE 3, 2023,' likely a typographic error for June 30) and was produced by the [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Ann Arbor Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI)]], the thirteen-person team responsible for stewarding the City's community-wide carbon neutrality plan. The report is structured around the six A2Zero strategies plus a cross-cutting 'other strategies' section, and it opens with a greenhouse gas emissions summary grounding all programmatic activity in measurable progress toward the 2030 carbon neutrality goal.

The central headline of the report is that Ann Arbor has reduced community-wide greenhouse gas emissions by over 22% from baseline, representing meaningful momentum but also underscoring the scale of work remaining. The 2022 GHG inventory reveals that electricity consumption accounts for 41% of community emissions, natural gas for 27%, transportation for roughly 30%, and waste for 2%. Critically, over 54% of DTE Electric's fuel mix supplying Ann Arbor was still generated from coal in 2022, while only 13% came from renewables (plus 2% additionally purchased), making grid decarbonization the single largest lever available. Michigan requires DTE meet a 15% renewable portfolio standard. The [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]] purchased the equivalent of 39% of its electricity from renewables in 2022, offsetting approximately 6% of community-wide emissions. The report also highlights that combusting fossil gas — responsible for 27% of local emissions — will always generate greenhouse gas emissions and has been associated with serious public health impacts including childhood asthma, cancer, and lung disease, providing a public health rationale for electrification alongside the climate imperative.

Year three is characterized throughout as a 'foundational' year — one in which OSI designed programs, secured grants, piloted services, and built institutional relationships that are expected to yield larger-scale deployment in year four. Major milestones include passage of the [[initiatives/community-climate-action-millage|Community Climate Action Millage]] in November 2022 (making Ann Arbor the 8th U.S. city to self-tax for local climate action), installation of over 4 MW of rooftop solar through [[initiatives/solarize-ann-arbor|Solarize Ann Arbor]], completion of the second resilience hub at the [[locations/bryant-neighborhood|Bryant Neighborhood]] Community Center, and a suite of federal and state grants totaling several million dollars across strategies. OSI was also recognized on the CDP's A List for the second consecutive year as one of 122 cities and counties globally taking bold leadership on environmental action, and received a Gold Level achievement award from the Michigan Green Communities program.

The report closes with a Year Four priorities list that signals the transition from foundation-building to scaled deployment: launching the [[initiatives/a2zero-assessment-path-to-zero|A2Zero Assessment: Path to Zero]] for all residents, launching residential rebates funded by the millage, breaking ground on the [[locations/wheeler-center|Wheeler Center]] [[initiatives/landfill-solar-project|Landfill Solar Project]], creating Ann Arbor's third and fourth [[initiatives/resilience-hubs|resilience hubs]], embedding A2Zero in the comprehensive land use plan, fully launching the Home Energy Advisor Program, finalizing the green rental housing initiative, securing funding to scale the Aging in Place Efficiently program, installing at least four more air quality monitors, and planting 10,000 trees with a survival study. Together, these priorities indicate that year three's investments in planning, piloting, and grant-securing are intended to unlock a more visible and resident-facing phase of implementation beginning in 2023–2024.
