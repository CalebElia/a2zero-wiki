title: A2ZERO Year One Annual Report (2021)
type: overview
---

This document is the first annual progress report for A2ZERO, Ann Arbor's plan for achieving a just transition to community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030. The plan was adopted by City Council in June 2020, and this report reflects on the first year of implementation: cataloguing accomplishments, acknowledging shortcomings, and previewing initiatives planned for Year Two. It is explicitly framed as a 'living plan' review, signaling that A2ZERO is designed to be iteratively updated rather than treated as a fixed blueprint. Readers are directed to www.a2gov.org/sustainability for additional detail and ways to get involved.

The report is structured around A2ZERO's seven strategies, with each section presenting a bulleted list of Year One activities. Coverage is uneven across strategies: Strategies 1 (Renewable Grid), 2 (Electrification), 3 (Building Efficiency), and 7 (Engagement/Other) receive the most substantive entries, while Strategies 4 (VMT Reduction), 5 (Materials/Waste), and 6 (Resilience) are covered more briefly. The document does not provide quantitative GHG reduction outcomes or financial expenditure figures for Year One — it is primarily a programmatic activity log rather than a performance measurement report.

The report documents a broad range of first-year actions: installation of approximately 1.3 MW of rooftop solar on over 200 homes saving residents over $650,000 on upfront costs, transition of 10% of the City's light-duty fleet to electric, launch of the Aging in Place Efficiently program, planting of over 1,000 trees under the 10,000 Trees Initiative, creation of the first resilience hub at Northside Community Center, and the securing of 92 organizational collaborators. Several new ordinances and programs were drafted or piloted, including an EV Readiness Ordinance, a commercial and multi-family benchmarking ordinance, and a reusable container pilot with four restaurants.

The final section previews Year Two priorities, including a community solar pilot, expansion of Solarize to commercial enterprises, a new low-to-moderate income solar program, installation of the City's first all-electric refuse truck, a second Ambassadors cohort, and deeper neighborhood-based engagement. These forward-looking commitments provide a useful baseline against which subsequent annual reports can measure progress.
