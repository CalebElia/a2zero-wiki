The Bryant neighborhood in Ann Arbor was selected as the site for a neighborhood-scale decarbonization initiative during Year One of A2ZERO, in partnership with the Community Action Network ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). The initiative focuses on co-creating whole home health, safety, comfort, and decarbonization assessments with residents ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). The broader effort, involving Bryant residents, the Community Action Network, Elevate, and the University of Michigan, aims to make Bryant the first fully decarbonized existing neighborhood in America ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).

Bryant served as the initial pilot site for the Whole-Home Decarbonization Assessment in Year Two of A2ZERO, with plans to expand the program city-wide in Year Three ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The neighborhood is the site of the [[initiatives/bryant-neighborhood-decarbonization|Bryant Neighborhood Decarbonization Program]], which carried Year Three priorities including growing community support and fundraising ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). During Year Two, the City also applied for a federal grant specifically to support climate action work in this neighborhood ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). Funding for work in Bryant includes grants from the U.S. EPA, the McKnight Foundation, and Solar Moonshot ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
