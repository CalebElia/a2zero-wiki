Ann Arbor, Michigan is the geographic scope of the [[initiatives/a2zero-carbon-neutrality-plan|A2Zero]] carbon neutrality initiative, with a community-wide greenhouse gas emissions baseline of 2.1 million metric tons as of 2018 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The city faces documented climate impacts including a nearly 1°F increase in average annual temperature since the 1900s, a projected 3–7°F additional rise by 2050, and a 44% increase in average annual precipitation since the 1950s ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Extreme precipitation events have also intensified, with total rainfall during such events increasing 37% since 1981, and Ann Arbor is projected to experience 12–36 more days per year above 90°F by 2050 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
