The Ann Arbor Greenbelt is a network of permanently protected open lands surrounding Ann Arbor, preserved through the City's Greenbelt Millage program. By Year 3 of A2ZERO, the Greenbelt had reached 7,600 acres of farmland and natural areas permanently protected surrounding the City ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The farmland protected by the Greenbelt has the potential to produce the calories needed to feed over 52,000 people ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The A2ZERO plan also identified greenbelt lands as a potential site for solar energy installations, noting that publicly protected open land could expand the city's renewable energy capacity ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
