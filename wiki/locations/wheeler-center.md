The Wheeler Center (also referred to as the Wheeler Service Center) is a City of Ann Arbor municipal facility located at the site of the former Ann Arbor landfill. It is designated as the location for the planned 20MW [[initiatives/wheeler-center-solar-park|Wheeler Center Solar Park]], which would be one of the largest landfill solar projects in the nation ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]], [[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The site is also the location of the first community solar offering secured in DTE Energy's service territory through the settlement of utility case U-20713 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).

In addition to the landfill solar project, the Wheeler Service Center was selected as a site for approximately 3 MW of solar installation under the ARPA City Facilities Solar initiative, with construction set to begin fall 2023 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). Breaking ground on the Wheeler Center Solar Park was listed as a Year 3 priority in the A2Zero Year 2 Annual Report ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).

The Wheeler Service Center also hosted a Climate Reality Presentation on December 20, 2019, reaching approximately 140 city staff members and including an introduction to the A2Zero initiative ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
