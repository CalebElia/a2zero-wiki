[[locations/bryant-community-center|Bryant Community Center]] in Ann Arbor is a City of Ann Arbor community facility located in the Bryant neighborhood, serving as a focal point for the [[initiatives/bryant-neighborhood-decarbonization|Bryant Neighborhood Decarbonization]] initiative ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The center served as the site of a joint A2Zero and Ann Arbor Housing Commission community outreach event on February 19, 2020, as part of the broader A2Zero public engagement campaign ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The center also functions as an AQMesh air quality monitoring site and a planned Resilience Hub ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The design for the second Resilience Hub at this location was completed in Year Two, with implementation planned for Year Three ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]), and the hub was completed in Year Three, making it a designated emergency preparedness and climate resilience site for residents ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The center is also targeted as a carbon-negative building as part of the broader Bryant neighborhood decarbonization effort ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
