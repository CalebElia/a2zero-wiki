Northside Community Center is a City of Ann Arbor facility that was selected as the site for the city's first A2ZERO resilience hub during Year One of the plan ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]), and became the first fully launched Resilience Hub under the A2Zero Resilience Hubs initiative in Year Two ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The resilience hub features a solar-powered battery storage system capable of providing up to three days of backup power during an outage ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]).
