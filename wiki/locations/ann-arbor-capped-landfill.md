The Ann Arbor capped landfill is a closed municipal solid waste facility owned by the City of Ann Arbor and serves as the primary site for the Landfill Solar Project. During Year One of A2ZERO, the city commissioned a feasibility and cost study for a 24MW solar installation on the site, exploring the potential to convert the capped landfill into a large-scale renewable energy generation facility ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). The project targets 24MW of capacity by 2023 and also includes a pilot of floating solar panels installed on a retention pond at the site, as well as pollinator habitat creation as a co-benefit ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). If enabling legislation is passed and community demand exists, the landfill solar site is planned to open for community solar subscriptions in 2026 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
