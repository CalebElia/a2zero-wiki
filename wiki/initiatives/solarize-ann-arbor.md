Solarize Ann Arbor is a group-purchasing solar program that reduces upfront costs for residential rooftop solar installations. In its first year, the program installed approximately 1.3MW of solar on over 200 residential roofs, saving participants over $650,000 in upfront costs ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). The program also produced a Solarize Toolkit — a step-by-step guide designed to help other communities replicate the Solarize model ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). Year Two plans included expanding the Solarize model to commercial enterprises ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]).
