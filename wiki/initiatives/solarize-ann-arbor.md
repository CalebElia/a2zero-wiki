Solarize Ann Arbor is a group-purchasing solar program that reduces upfront costs for residential rooftop solar installations through bulk procurement and community outreach ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). In its first year the program installed approximately 1.3MW of solar on over 200 residential roofs, saving participants over $650,000 in upfront costs ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). Year Two added another 1.7MW, bringing the cumulative total to over 3MW across more than 430 households — representing over $1.5 million in upfront savings and an estimated $15 million in lifetime energy savings ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The program's long-term target is 78MW of local generation by 2030, projected to reduce 85,000 MTCO₂e (3.9%) with UM participation ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Solarize also functions as an entry point for introducing residents to electrification and efficiency concepts ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).

OSI developed a Solarize Toolkit in Year One — a step-by-step replication guide distributed through the [[actors/great-lakes-renewable-energy-association|Great Lakes Renewable Energy Association (GLREA)]] to help other communities adopt the bulk-purchase model beyond Washtenaw County ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]], [[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). In Year Two, OSI initiated design of a [[initiatives/commercial-solarize-pilot|Commercial Solarize Pilot]] in collaboration with the [[actors/ann-arbor-2030-district|2030 District]], extending the model to businesses for the first time ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
