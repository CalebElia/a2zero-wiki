The City of Ann Arbor intervened in Michigan utility case U-20713 and secured a settlement with [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] with three key outcomes ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). First, the settlement created the first community solar offering in DTE's service territory, to be located at the [[initiatives/wheeler-center-solar-park|Wheeler Center Solar Park]] ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). Second, it established a working group to explore carbon reduction offerings including a Virtual Power Reduction Agreement and 24/7 renewable power options ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). Third, it secured reduced rates for customers enrolling in the MIGreenPower program ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
