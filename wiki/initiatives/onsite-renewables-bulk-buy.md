The Onsite Renewables Bulk Buy program enables residents and businesses to procure solar, geothermal, and energy storage systems at discounted prices (5%, 10%, or potentially 15%) by achieving economies of scale through group purchasing ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program targets 78MW of local renewable energy generation on viable buildings, parcels of land, and carports by 2030, with at least one-quarter of installations also incorporating battery storage to enhance community resilience ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). OSI intends to bundle energy efficiency and electrification support into the program so that each household or business receives a comprehensive package rather than multiple separate offerings ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The estimated 10-year cost is $605,000 ($540,000 staffing, $65,000 in community stipends), with a greenhouse gas reduction potential of 81,000 metric tons CO₂e (3.7% of community-wide emissions), rising to 85,000 metric tons CO₂e with University of Michigan participation ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
