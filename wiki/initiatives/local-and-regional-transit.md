Local and Regional Transit is projected to reduce 93,200 MT CO2e, representing 4.3% of total community emissions, at a total cost of $656,780,000 and approximately $7,047 per ton, making it the most expensive initiative in the plan by total cost ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Co-benefits include local energy production, air quality improvement, job creation, resilience, public health, economic savings, and equity benefits ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
