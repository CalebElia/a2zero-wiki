The Electrify Buses initiative targets the full electrification of [[actors/ann-arbor-area-transit-authority|TheRide]]'s fleet of 87 diesel and diesel-hybrid buses and the University of Michigan's ethanol, biodiesel, and gasoline buses on an aggressive timeline ending in 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan assumes TheRide purchases 9 new electric buses annually starting in 2021, while UM replaces approximately 5 buses per year; a traditional bus costs $525,000 compared to $750,000 for an electric bus ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The estimated 10-year cost for TheRide's portion alone is $86,000,000, with a GHG reduction potential of 10,700 MTCO2e (0.5% of community-wide emissions), rising to 13,800 MTCO2e (0.6%) if UM also participates ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Communities living along electrified bus routes are expected to benefit from reduced asthma and COPD rates, which disproportionately affect communities of color and low-income residents ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
