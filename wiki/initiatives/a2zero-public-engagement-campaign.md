The [[initiatives/a2zero-public-engagement-campaign|A2Zero Public Engagement Campaign]] was launched on November 13, 2019 at [[locations/cobblestone-farm|Cobblestone Farm]], drawing over 250 attendees to the public debut of the A2Zero initiative ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Between November 2019 and March 2020, city staff conducted at least 69 documented public engagement events spanning neighborhood associations, high schools, city commissions, nonprofit organizations, businesses, and community centers across Ann Arbor and Washtenaw County ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Outreach deliberately targeted diverse audiences including youth, environmental justice communities, apartment residents, commercial building owners, and city department staff — with events held at venues such as [[locations/wheeler-service-center|Wheeler Service Center]], [[locations/bryant-community-center|Bryant Community Center]], and the [[locations/ann-arbor-farmers-market|Ann Arbor Farmers Market]] ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Partner organizations engaged during this period included [[actors/washtenaw-350|Washtenaw 350]], [[actors/citizens-climate-lobby|Citizens Climate Lobby]], [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|Ann Arbor Housing Commission]], [[actors/huron-valley-sierra-club|Huron Valley Sierra Club]], and [[actors/united-way-of-washtenaw-county|United Way of Washtenaw County]], reflecting a broad coalition-building approach to plan development ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
