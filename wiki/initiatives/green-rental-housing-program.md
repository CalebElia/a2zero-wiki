The Green Rental Housing Program integrates energy efficiency requirements into the City's existing rental licensing process, ensuring every rental unit in Ann Arbor meets a minimum energy efficiency performance standard ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Rental units make up 55% of Ann Arbor's housing stock, making this program critical to community-wide GHG reductions; the program also provides trainings, rebates, and financing support to landlords ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program assumes 80% compliance after four years in effect and no net increase in average rents outside of normal market inflation within five years of policy adoption ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year cost is estimated at $530,000; GHG reduction potential is incorporated into other plan calculations rather than reported separately ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
