The 10,000 Trees Initiative was developed and launched during Year One of A2ZERO to improve the city's resilience to climate change through green infrastructure, supporting both climate resilience and carbon sequestration goals ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). The initiative aims to expand Ann Arbor's urban tree canopy through FreeTree Giveaways and Community Tree Plantings, with two FreeTree Giveaways held each year in celebration of fall and spring growing seasons ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). Over 1,000 trees were planted in Year One, with grant funding secured for tree planting in underserved neighborhoods ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). Since spring 2021, nearly 2,500 trees have been distributed to residents through these events ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). A tree maintenance and care app was developed to support recipients in caring for trees received through the initiative ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). Year Two plans included launching the BranchOut program and additional FreeTree Giveaways ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]), and the city was also investigating additional funding opportunities to expand Community Tree Plantings in fall 2022 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). A Year 3 priority was to plant 1,500 more trees through the program, indicating substantial planting activity had already occurred in prior years ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
