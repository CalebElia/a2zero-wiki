Ann Arbor launched a neighborhood decarbonization initiative in the Bryant neighborhood during Year One of A2ZERO, partnering with the [[actors/community-action-network|Community Action Network]] to co-create a whole home health, safety, comfort, and decarbonization assessment with community members — centering equity in the design process ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). By Year Two the initiative had expanded in ambition and partnership scope: the City, Community Action Network, [[actors/elevate|Elevate]], and the [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]] are collaborating to design and create what is described as the first fully decarbonized existing neighborhood in America ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). Year 3 priorities include continuing to grow momentum and community support, and completing fundraising to advance implementation ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).

Funding secured by the end of Year Two includes $25,000 from the U.S. EPA ([[funding-events/epa-bryant-2022|epa-bryant-2022]]), $75,000 from the McKnight Foundation ([[funding-events/mcknight-foundation-bryant-2022|mcknight-foundation-bryant-2022]]), and $25,000 from [[actors/solar-moonshot|Solar Moonshot]] to install solar on two homes in Bryant ([[funding-events/solar-moonshot-bryant-2022|solar-moonshot-bryant-2022]]). The City also applied for a federal neighborhood climate action grant during Year Two ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The initiative spans Strategies 2, 3, and 6, and is co-listed under community engagement as a flagship place-based equity project.
