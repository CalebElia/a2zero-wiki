The Support a Plant Rich Diet initiative educates the Ann Arbor community about the environmental and health benefits of reducing meat consumption and increasing fruits, vegetables, legumes, whole grains, nuts, and seeds ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). A key component is exploring a greenhouse gas emissions footprint disclosure on food sold at City facilities — analogous to calorie disclosure — to inform residents about the environmental footprint of their dietary choices ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The initiative addresses food equity by acknowledging food deserts in the region and aiming to ensure low-income residents have access to healthy, nutritious options ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The estimated 10-year cost is $208,400; greenhouse gas reduction potential was not calculated ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
