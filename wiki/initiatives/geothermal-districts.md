title: Geothermal Districts
type: initiative
---

Geothermal districts were proposed in the A2Zero plan as a shared infrastructure approach to providing low-carbon heating and cooling across multiple buildings or neighborhoods, with district systems achieving greater efficiency and lower per-unit costs than individual building installations by sharing ground loop infrastructure ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). In Year Three, the City won a $580,000 planning grant from the U.S. Department of Energy to design a district geothermal loop in the Bryant neighborhood, advancing that site as a flagship decarbonization model ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) also provided Clean Energy to Communities technical assistance to determine data needs and draft a request for proposals for a district geothermal study around Veterans Park ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). Additionally, [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|OSI]] continued to support geothermal studies and analysis at two future [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|Ann Arbor Housing Commission]] sites ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
