The Ann Arbor Resilience Network is an emerging initiative to formally coordinate resilience hub activities across Washtenaw County through a network of community organizations ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). In Year Three, the City of Ann Arbor hosted over a dozen community-based organizations to explore the creation of this coordinated resilience network ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The City also applied for a federal EPA grant to formally launch the resilience network and expand hubs county-wide, building on the completion of the second resilience hub at Bryant Neighborhood Community Center ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). This emerging network builds on the existing [[initiatives/resilience-hubs|Resilience Hubs]] infrastructure and aims to coordinate county-wide climate resilience capacity.
