The Bulk Purchase of Energy Efficiency initiative is projected to reduce 242,500 MT CO2e, representing 11.1% of total community emissions, at a total cost of $950,000 and approximately $4 per ton ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Co-benefits include local energy production, air quality improvement, job creation, public health, economic savings, equity benefits, and scalability ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
