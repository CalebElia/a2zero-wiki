The Building Code Requirements initiative is projected to reduce 48,300 MT CO2e, representing 2.2% of total community emissions, at a total cost of $1,935,000 and approximately $40 per ton ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Co-benefits include local energy production, job creation, resilience, economic savings, equity benefits, and scalability ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
