The [[initiatives/develop-mixed-use-neighborhoods|Develop Mixed-Use Neighborhoods]] initiative focuses on updating Ann Arbor's zoning code to allow mixed uses in residential neighborhoods, enabling bakeries, grocery stores, breweries, coffee shops, and similar amenities to be accessible within walking and biking distance ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The initiative targets 20% of in-city trips conducted by walking or biking by 2030, with full zoning code amendments expected to be adopted by 2024 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year cost is estimated at $365,000, and the GHG reduction potential is captured within other actions rather than independently quantified ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Assumptions include increased social cohesion, increased walking and biking, and reduced driving and car ownership in mixed-use neighborhoods ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
