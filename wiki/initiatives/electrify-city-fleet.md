The Electrify City Fleet initiative was authorized by City Council Resolution R-17-237 in June 2017, which directed the City Administrator to update the Green Fleets Policy and promote plug-in electric vehicle purchases ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The Green Fleets Team was tasked with developing a plan to electrify the City fleet by 2025, prioritizing light-duty vehicles first given the broader EV market availability, before addressing construction equipment and medium- and heavy-duty vehicles ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year estimated cost is $4,000,000, with a GHG reduction potential of 1,100 MTCO2e (0.05% of community-wide emissions), with all reductions expected to be achieved by 2026 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The remaining 10% of the fleet that cannot be electrified by 2025 consists of medium- and heavy-duty vehicles and specialized equipment ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
