The Move Toward a Circular Economy initiative promotes resource reduction, material reuse, and regeneration across Ann Arbor, with the City aiming to create a model where goods and materials are recovered, reused, and recycled ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Key actions include promoting repair centers for bicycles and appliances, supporting toy, book, and tool libraries to decrease new purchases, and contracting with a private collector for weekly curbside textile collection from single-family homes with potential future expansion to multi-family properties ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The initiative also involves working with peer municipalities to create tools for transparency, traceability, and calculation of embedded greenhouse gas emissions for different materials and goods ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Lower-income residents are identified as potential equity beneficiaries through access to repurposed and refurbished items; the estimated 10-year cost is $270,000 with greenhouse gas reduction potential not yet calculated ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
