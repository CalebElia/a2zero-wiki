The Update Building Codes initiative focuses on working with the State of Michigan to adopt the 2021 building code and zero code appendix so that all new developments and major renovations in Ann Arbor can be built to net zero energy standards ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Because Michigan municipalities are constrained by the State's building code, local action requires state-level change ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The initiative assumes all new construction from 2022 through 2030 will include no natural gas consumption and all electricity usage offset through the Community Choice Aggregation program, with annual growth rates of 265 residential units and 21 commercial units ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year cost is estimated at $1,935,000, with a GHG reduction potential of 48,300 MTCO²e, representing 2.2% of community-wide emissions ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
