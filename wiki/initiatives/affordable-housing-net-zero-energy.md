The Transition Affordable Housing Sites to Net Zero Energy initiative is a partnership between the [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] and the [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|Ann Arbor Housing Commission]] to retrofit all existing and new affordable housing sites to net zero energy, including infrastructure for EV charging and battery storage ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). OSI committed to providing $200,000 annually through 2021 and $100,000 per year from 2022 through 2025 in direct support to the Housing Commission, with additional fundraising to finance remaining improvements ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year program cost is estimated at $800,000, with a GHG reduction potential of approximately 400 MTCO²e (less than 1% of community-wide emissions) ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Energy savings generated by the program are intended to be reinvested in programming for Housing Commission tenants and in expanding affordable housing sites ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
