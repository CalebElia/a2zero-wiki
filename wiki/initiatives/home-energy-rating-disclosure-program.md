A draft Home Energy Rating Disclosure program was advanced during Year Two of A2Zero, with the program expected to be considered by City Council in Year Three ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The policy takes the form of a drafted ordinance that would require energy rating disclosure at the time a home is listed for sale in Ann Arbor ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The program is part of the City's broader strategy to improve building energy efficiency and transparency for residential properties, enabling buyers and renters to make informed decisions about energy costs and efficiency ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).

The ordinance was fully drafted during Year Three of A2Zero, with the goal of formally introducing it in summer 2023 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). Finalizing and formally launching this initiative was subsequently identified as a Year Four priority in the A2Zero Year Three Annual Report ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). This initiative complements the commercial energy and water benchmarking ordinance — the first in the State of Michigan — by extending energy transparency requirements to the residential real estate market ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
