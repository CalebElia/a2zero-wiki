Ann Arbor conducts an annual community-wide greenhouse gas inventory to track progress toward its A2ZERO goal of reducing community emissions from over 2.1 million metric tons of CO2 equivalent to zero by 2030 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The inventory monitors key emission sources including electricity consumption, fossil fuels burned in homes and vehicles, and waste disposal, and is used to guide program design and science-based target-setting ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The City adheres to established local government GHG measurement standards and actively participates in developing and refining those standards ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).

As of the 2022 inventory, Ann Arbor's largest source of GHG emissions is electricity consumption in homes and businesses, accounting for 41% of total community emissions, followed by transportation at approximately 30% and natural gas at 27%, with fuel oil (0.06%), propane (0.5%), and waste (2%) making up the remainder ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). In 2022, over 54% of DTE Electric's fuel mix supplying Ann Arbor was generated using coal, while only 13% came from renewables (with an additional 2% purchased) ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). By Year Three, cumulative A2ZERO program activity had achieved a community-wide emissions reduction of over 22% from the baseline ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
