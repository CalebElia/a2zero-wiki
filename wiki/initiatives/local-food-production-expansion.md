type: initiative

The A2Zero plan considers scaling community gardens and local food production, recovering local food waste from gardeners, farms, restaurants, and grocery stores, and expanding farmers markets to year-round operation at more locations ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Expanded food hubs, food stops, and increased local food availability in grocery stores are also identified, along with protecting SNAP access at appropriately scaled sizes ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). A pilot for local food subscription meals with circular and reusable packaging is proposed, and collaboration with Ann Arbor Public Schools and the University of Michigan on food planning, purchasing, and waste is recommended ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
