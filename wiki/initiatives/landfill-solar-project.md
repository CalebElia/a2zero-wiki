The Landfill Solar Project is a utility-scale solar installation being developed by the City of Ann Arbor in partnership with [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] on the City-owned landfill and in Pittsfield Township, targeting 24MW of installed capacity by 2023 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The installation is intended to offset roughly 80% of Ann Arbor's municipal energy usage and includes a pilot of floating solar panels on a retention pond, a portion of clean energy for Pittsfield Township's municipal operations, and pollinator habitat co-benefits ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). If demand exists and enabling legislation is passed, the project may be integrated into a community solar program open to public subscriptions ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The estimated 10-year staffing cost is $80,000 (with grid upgrade costs to be determined), and the greenhouse gas reduction potential is 23,000 metric tons CO₂e (1% of community-wide emissions) ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
