The Landfill Solar Project, also referred to as the Wheeler Center Landfill Solar Project, is a utility-scale solar installation being developed by the City of Ann Arbor in partnership with [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] on the City-owned capped landfill and in Pittsfield Township, targeting 24MW of installed capacity by 2023 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]), with a planned capacity of 20 MW reported in later planning documents ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). When complete, the project would be one of the largest landfill solar installations in the nation ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The installation is intended to offset roughly 80% of Ann Arbor's municipal energy usage and includes a pilot of floating solar panels on a retention pond, a portion of clean energy for Pittsfield Township's municipal operations, and pollinator habitat co-benefits ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

Work on the project continued in Year Three of A2ZERO as part of the broader effort to power the community's electric grid with 100% renewable energy, with groundbreaking identified as a Year Four priority targeted for 2024 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The project is also part of the broader ARPA-funded city facilities solar effort that includes installations at Wheeler, Water/Wastewater facilities, and the airport ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).

If demand exists and enabling legislation is passed, the project may be integrated into a community solar program open to public subscriptions ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The estimated 10-year staffing cost is $80,000 (with grid upgrade costs to be determined), and the greenhouse gas reduction potential is 23,000 metric tons CO₂e (1% of community-wide emissions) ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
