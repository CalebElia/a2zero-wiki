The Landfill Solar Project is a utility-scale solar installation developed in partnership with [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] on the City-owned landfill and adjacent land in Pittsfield Township, targeting 24MW of installed capacity intended to offset roughly 80% of Ann Arbor municipal energy usage. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The project includes ground-mounted solar, a pilot of floating solar panels on a retention pond, a portion of clean energy for Pittsfield Township municipal operations, and pollinator habitat co-benefits. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The initiative is projected to reduce GHG emissions by 23,000 MTCO₂e (1% of community-wide emissions) at a 10-year staffing cost of $80,000, with final project costs dependent on any required grid interconnection upgrades. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) If enabling legislation passes and demand exists, the project may be integrated into a community solar program. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])
