The Community Solar Program allows residents and businesses who cannot install solar on their own property — due to inadequate solar exposure, renting, or financial barriers — to purchase panels from an existing solar farm and receive an on-bill credit as if those panels were on their roof ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program requires state enabling legislation, and the City was already working with state legislators to advance this as of 2020; it is developed in partnership with [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The target is 11MW of community solar established locally by 2030, with the landfill solar site planned to open for public subscriptions in 2026 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The estimated 10-year cost to the City is $205,000 (which would increase if the City becomes an anchor tenant), with a greenhouse gas reduction potential of 11,500 metric tons CO₂e (0.5% of community-wide emissions) ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
