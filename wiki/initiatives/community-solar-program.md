The Community Solar Program allows residents and businesses who cannot install solar on their own property — including renters, low-income residents, and those with shaded roofs — to purchase panels from an existing solar farm and receive an on-bill credit. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) State legislation enabling community solar is required before the program can launch; the City was already working with state legislators as of 2020. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The program targets 11MW of community solar by 2030 and is projected to reduce GHG emissions by 11,500 MTCO₂e (0.5% of community-wide emissions) at a 10-year administrative cost of $205,000. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])
