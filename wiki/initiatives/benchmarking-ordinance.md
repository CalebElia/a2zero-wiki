During Year One of A2ZERO, Ann Arbor drafted a commercial and multi-family benchmarking ordinance as well as a home energy disclosure ordinance, with input sourced from stakeholder engagement ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). These ordinances aim to increase transparency around building energy performance and drive efficiency improvements across the city's building stock ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). By Year 3, the commercial energy and water benchmarking ordinance was fully implemented, making Ann Arbor the first city in the State of Michigan to enact such a requirement ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). A companion Home Energy Rating Disclosure policy for residential home sales was also fully drafted during Year 3, with introduction targeted for summer 2023 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
