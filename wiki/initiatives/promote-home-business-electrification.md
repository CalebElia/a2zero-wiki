title: Promote Home & Business Electrification
type: initiative

The Promote Home & Business Electrification initiative aims to transition Ann Arbor's residential and commercial buildings away from natural gas for space heating, water heating, and cooking, which together represent over 25% of community greenhouse gas emissions ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The City of Ann Arbor will lead by electrifying all municipal buildings, while the broader program targets 30% of owner-occupied homes and 25% of rental properties by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year administrative and staffing cost is estimated at $1,400,000 for the community program and $5,700,000 for transitioning city facilities, with a combined GHG reduction potential of 79,200 MTCO2e (3.6% of community-wide emissions) — rising to 362,200 MTCO2e (16.5%) if the University of Michigan also electrifies all operations ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). All new residential and commercial buildings are assumed to be designed and built without natural gas, and all additional electricity demand is assumed to be met by renewable energy sources ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
