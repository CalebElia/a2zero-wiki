The [[initiatives/expand-improve-local-transit|Expand and Improve Local Transit]] initiative aims for 95% of Ann Arbor residents to have transit access within a 10-minute walk at frequencies of 20 minutes or faster by 2030, with residents taking transit for 40% of their work journeys ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The initiative includes building bus rapid transit along major transportation corridors, adding 2 new electric buses per year for 10 years, and expanding the Get Downtown Program to all businesses with more than 20 employees citywide ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year cost is estimated at $656,780,000, with a GHG reduction potential of 93,200 metric tons CO²e, representing 4.3% of community-wide emissions ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Modeling assumes a quadrupling of current bus ridership at an average of 3.5 miles per trip, with 22 queue jump facilities for regular buses and signal priority equipment for priority corridors ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
