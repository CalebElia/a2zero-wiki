The A2ZERO Ambassadors Program was conceived in the CAP 2020 as a mechanism to rapidly scale community-wide carbon neutrality work by training residents to serve as peer educators and project leaders in their neighborhoods ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program was also discussed at a planning meeting on December 4, 2019, focused on how to disseminate information about A2Zero and recruit community members to serve as neighborhood-level climate advocates, functioning as a grassroots outreach mechanism to extend A2Zero's reach into Ann Arbor neighborhoods beyond formal public events ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|OSI]] designed the program to partner with nonprofits including [[actors/community-action-network|Community Action Network]], [[actors/neutral-zone|Neutral Zone]], [[actors/united-way-of-washtenaw-county|United Way of Washtenaw County]], and [[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]] to train stipended cohorts of low-income adults and youth from underserved communities as Climate Ambassadors ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The ten-year plan targeted 100 trained ambassadors by 2025 covering energy, water, waste, transportation, and local food efforts at an estimated cost of $350,000 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program trains community members to serve as informed advocates and educators for A2Zero climate action initiatives ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).

In Year One, 22 community members were trained as A2ZERO Ambassadors, and they designed and implemented seven sustainability-focused community projects ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). The program also developed educational materials for Ambassadors to use in their neighborhoods ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). By the end of Year Two, the 2nd and 3rd cohorts had been completed, bringing the total number of trained ambassadors to over 50 individuals ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). A Year 3 priority was to engage the next two rounds of ambassadors, with a goal of bringing the total number trained through the program to nearly 100 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
