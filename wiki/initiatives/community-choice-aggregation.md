Community Choice Aggregation (CCA) is an opt-out program that allows local governments to procure power on behalf of residents, businesses, and municipal accounts from an alternative supplier while retaining transmission and distribution services from the existing utility ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Ann Arbor's CCA vision calls for the City to administer its first bulk buy of 100% new renewable energy by 2027, with all residents, commercial entities, and potentially the University of Michigan participating ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program requires enabling legislation at the state level before it can launch, and the City planned to begin working with legislators in 2021 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The estimated 10-year cost is $3,245,000, with a greenhouse gas reduction potential of 462,100 metric tons CO₂e (21% of community-wide emissions), rising to approximately 784,000 metric tons CO₂e (~36%) if the University of Michigan participates ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
