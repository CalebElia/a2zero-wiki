The Expand Weatherization Program extends existing Washtenaw County and nonprofit weatherization services to more low-income homeowners in Ann Arbor than the County program alone can serve, with the City providing funding and administrative support through a contract passed by Council ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program assumes that weatherization expansion will also include funding to support home electrification ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year cost is estimated at $1,550,000; GHG reduction potential is incorporated into other plan calculations rather than reported separately ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The goal is for all low-income residents in the City to have been offered weatherization services by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
