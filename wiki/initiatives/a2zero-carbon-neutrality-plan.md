The 2030 Carbon Neutral Ann Arbor Plan, known as A2Zero, was initiated by a November 4, 2019 City Council resolution that declared a climate emergency and directed the City Administration to develop a community-wide carbon neutrality plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The resolution called for a public engagement process beginning immediately to outline how the entire Ann Arbor community could achieve carbon neutrality by 2030, described as 'both ambitious and achievable' ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]], created in FY19, was the lead office responsible for developing the plan, with funding for public engagement available in the FY2020 OSI budget ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan built on Ann Arbor's prior 2012 Climate Action Plan, which had set GHG reduction targets of 8% by 2015, 25% by 2025, and 90% by 2050 relative to a year 2000 baseline ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
