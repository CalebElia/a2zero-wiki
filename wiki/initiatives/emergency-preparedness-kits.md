This initiative works directly with partners and community organizations to educate Ann Arborites on emergency preparedness and distribute emergency preparedness kits and plans, with priority given to low-income residents who may face cost, language, or other barriers ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program plans to integrate emergency preparedness education into the [[initiatives/a2zero-ambassadors-program|Neighborhood and Youth Ambassador Programs]] by 2022 and partner with [[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]] for community-wide preparedness sessions ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The ten-year estimated cost is $350,000, and no GHG reduction potential has been calculated for this action ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The goal is for 1,000 residents to have emergency preparedness kits by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
