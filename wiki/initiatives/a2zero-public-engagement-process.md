The A2Zero Public Engagement Process was the community-facing planning pillar of the A2Zero carbon neutrality plan, designed and led by the [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] (OSI) ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The campaign launched on November 13, 2019 at [[locations/cobblestone-farm|Cobblestone Farm]], drawing over 250 attendees to the public debut of the A2Zero initiative ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Between November 2019 and March 12, 2020, OSI staff hosted at least 68–69 documented public engagement events spanning neighborhood associations, high schools, city commissions, nonprofit organizations, businesses, and community centers across Ann Arbor and Washtenaw County ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Outreach deliberately targeted diverse audiences including youth, environmental justice communities, apartment residents, commercial building owners, and city department staff — with events held at venues such as [[locations/wheeler-service-center|Wheeler Service Center]], [[locations/bryant-community-center|Bryant Community Center]], and the [[locations/ann-arbor-farmers-market|Ann Arbor Farmers Market]] — reflecting a focus on engaging underrepresented constituencies and leveraging partner networks to reach as wide a swath of the community as possible ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

Three sequential public surveys were administered: Survey 1 gathered ideas from the public; Survey 2 collected feedback on preferred emissions-reduction actions and funding approaches; and Survey 3 identified what stakeholders needed to support implementation of the final strategy ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Supporting outreach, OSI created a website (www.A2ZERO.org), a stakeholder listserv, a meeting-in-a-box toolkit enabling community members to host their own events, and distributed 11,000 backpack fliers to K-5th grade students in Ann Arbor Public Schools ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). OSI also hired a communications firm to develop the A2Zero brand identity and worked with [[actors/elevate-energy|Elevate Energy]] to assess the greenhouse gas reduction potential and costs of each action in the final plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

Partner organizations engaged during this period included [[actors/washtenaw-350|Washtenaw 350]], [[actors/citizens-climate-lobby-ann-arbor|Citizens Climate Lobby]], [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|Ann Arbor Housing Commission]], [[actors/huron-valley-sierra-club|Huron Valley Sierra Club]], and [[actors/united-way-of-washtenaw-county|United Way of Washtenaw County]], reflecting a broad coalition-building approach to plan development ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
