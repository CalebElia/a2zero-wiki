title: Preserve and Enhance the Local Tree Canopy

This initiative involves conducting a tree canopy inventory, updating the master plan to incentivize shade tree planting, and launching neighborhood tree planting efforts to reduce urban heat island effects and support biodiversity ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan assumes the City plants 1,000 trees per year on city property and the community plants 1,000 trees per year on private property, with each tree absorbing an estimated 48 pounds of CO₂ over the plan's duration ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Equity considerations note that tree canopies in low-income communities and communities of color tend to be smaller, and Ann Arbor's Health Impact Assessment should be updated to prioritize equitable canopy enhancement in underserved neighborhoods ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The ten-year estimated cost is $1,160,000, with a GHG reduction potential of approximately 450 MTCO²e (0.02% of community-wide emissions) ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
