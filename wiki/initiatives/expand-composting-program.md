The Expand Composting Program is a Strategy 5 action under [[strategies/strategy-5-materials-waste|A2Zero's materials and waste strategy]] focused on moving Ann Arbor to year-round composting, diverting food and yard waste from the waste stream to reduce methane emissions and produce locally usable compost ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The Solid Waste Resource Management Plan estimates that year-round composting would add 1,000–2,784 tons of collection, and expanding food collection from food-related businesses would add another 1,000–2,400 tons per year ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program also includes creating markets for imperfect fruits and vegetables to prevent them from entering the waste stream, with equity benefits for low-income residents who could access more affordable produce ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The estimated 10-year cost is $26,000,000, with a greenhouse gas reduction potential of approximately 400 metric tons CO₂e (0.02% of community-wide emissions) ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
