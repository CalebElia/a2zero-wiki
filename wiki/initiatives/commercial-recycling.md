The Expand Commercial Recycling initiative is a Strategy 5 action targeting Ann Arbor businesses, using incentives, education, and enforcement policies to achieve at least 30% commercial sector recycling rates by 2028 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The Solid Waste Resource Management Plan estimates that increased commercial participation would divert 1,700–4,400 tons of waste from landfill annually, with a commercial sector diversion rate assumption of 4,400 annual tons ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Implementation is led by the [[actors/department-of-public-works|Department of Public Works]] with support from [[actors/resource-recycling-associates|Resource Recycling Associates]], and includes increased enforcement of commercial establishments that do not recycle or compost ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The estimated 10-year cost is $18,400,000, with a greenhouse gas reduction potential of 5,600 metric tons CO₂e (0.3% of community-wide emissions), at approximately $3,286 per ton ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Co-benefits include job creation and scalability ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
