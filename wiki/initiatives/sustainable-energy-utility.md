The Sustainable Energy Utility (SEU) is a proposed community-owned energy utility that would provide electricity generated from local solar and battery storage systems installed on homes and businesses throughout Ann Arbor — delivering 100% clean, reliable, locally built, and affordable electricity, described as built by the community for the community ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). A formal proposal for the SEU was developed in Year Two, and the City opened a public engagement portal at www.a2gov.org/a2seu for residents to learn more and register their input ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The SEU proposal was awarded the Project of the Year by the [[actors/great-lakes-renewable-energy-association|Great Lakes Renewable Energy Association]] ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).

The SEU is one of two parallel pathways under active exploration for achieving 100% renewable energy: the other is traditional municipalization of the electric grid ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). In Year Three, [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|OSI]] commissioned an Energy Options Analysis to determine the financial viability of the SEU, traditional municipalization, and other options for meeting community-wide renewable energy goals ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). A grant application was also submitted to the U.S. Department of Energy to formally create the SEU, with decisions expected in mid-2024 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). A strategy is expected to be brought to Council for formal consideration by the end of Year Four ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The initiative is rooted in Strategy 1's goal of a 100% renewable, reliable, and affordable electric grid for Ann Arbor by 2030.
