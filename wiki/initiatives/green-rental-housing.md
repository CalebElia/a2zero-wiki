The Green Rental Housing (GRH) program expands on Ann Arbor's existing rental inspection program by adding standards for rentals designed to promote safety, comfort, health, and efficiency citywide, including appliance electrification requirements ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). In Year Two, staff examined model cities, participated in regional cohorts, and facilitated a task force of tenants, landlords, engineers, architects, and low-income representatives to develop requirements tailored for Ann Arbor ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). In addition to lowering carbon emissions from rentals, GRH is intended to standardize quality expectations, lower energy costs for tenants, and provide resources for landlords ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The draft initiative was in review as of Year Two and expected to proceed to City Council in Year Three, with finalization identified as a Year 3 priority in the A2Zero Year 2 Annual Report ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
