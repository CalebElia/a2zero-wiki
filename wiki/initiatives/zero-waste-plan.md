The A2Zero Living Carbon Neutrality Plan identifies creation of a Zero Waste Plan as a key idea considered for resource reduction in Ann Arbor ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan envisions requiring all city-wide events to be zero waste and reporting publicly on what happens to recovered waste streams ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Strategies to discourage landfilled waste include extending time between trash pickups, initiating a pay-by-weight trash model, limiting container sizes, and using trash can fees to fund educational campaigns ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Single-use plastic reduction measures considered include organizing a local business cohort, taxing or banning single-use plastics, requiring bottle-filling stations, and developing A2Zero-branded compostable alternatives ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
