The Ann Arbor Reduce, Reuse, Return (A2R3) program offers US-made, USDA-approved, microwave-, freezer-, and dishwasher-safe reusable containers for takeout orders at participating local restaurants ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). Launched in Year Two of A2Zero, the program initially included [[actors/zingermans-next-door-cafe|Zingerman's Next Door Café]], [[actors/ginger-deli|Ginger Deli]], and [[actors/el-harissa-market-cafe|El Harissa Market Café]], with plans to add additional businesses continually ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). Containers can be returned to any participating business to be cleaned and reused, reducing single-use container waste and building Ann Arbor's circular economy ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The program is administered in partnership with [[actors/live-zero-waste|Live Zero Waste]], which maintains up-to-date program information and allows residents to check out containers when visiting participating businesses ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
