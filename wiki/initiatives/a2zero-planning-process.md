The A2Zero Planning Process was initiated on November 13, 2019, with a deadline of plan completion by mid-March 2020, representing an accelerated four-month creation period spanning 82 working days. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The process followed three phases — Ideation, Initial Prioritization, and Quantify & Determine 'How' — drawing on four main input sources: Peer Cities, Public Input, Staff Expertise, and Technical Advisor Expertise. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) Public engagement included 3 public surveys, over 60 public events, and 2 large town halls, with contributions from over 80 technical advisors and support from 66 partner organizations. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) Ann Arbor's climate action work dates to the early 2000s, and A2Zero builds upon nearly two decades of prior planning and local leadership. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])
