The A2Zero Planning Process was initiated by the City of Ann Arbor on November 13, 2019, with a deadline of plan completion by mid-March 2020, representing an accelerated four-month creation period spanning 82 working days ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The process was organized into three phases — Phase 1 (Ideation), Phase 2 (Initial Prioritization), and Phase 3 (Quantify & Determine 'How') — drawing on four main input streams: Peer Cities, Public Input, Staff Expertise, and Technical Advisor Expertise, as well as technical documents and peer-reviewed literature ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Public engagement included 3 public surveys, over 60 public events, and 2 large town halls, while over 80 technical advisors and 66 partner organizations contributed to the process ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The process built upon nearly two decades of prior climate action planning in Ann Arbor and culminated in the A2Zero Living Carbon Neutrality Plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
