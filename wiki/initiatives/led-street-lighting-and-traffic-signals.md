The LED Street Lighting and Traffic Signals initiative converts all municipal streetlights and traffic signals to LED technology, which can reduce energy usage by up to 70% compared to conventional lighting ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The City plans to acquire ownership of approximately two-thirds of streetlights currently owned by [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]], replacing 4,467 lights and achieving approximately 60% kWh savings, while traffic signal replacement is projected to yield 85% efficiency savings ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The total 10-year cost is estimated at $3,100,000, which includes purchasing assets from DTE Energy and financing the LED conversion, with a GHG reduction potential of 2,600 MTCO²e (0.1% of community-wide emissions) ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Full conversion is targeted for completion by 2029 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
