The AQMesh Air Quality Monitoring Network deploys low-cost air quality sensors at community sites across Ann Arbor to measure pollutants and make data publicly available online ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The initiative expands the city's capacity to track air quality conditions across different neighborhoods ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). In Year Two, two AQMesh monitors were installed — one at the [[locations/ann-arbor-farmers-market|Kerrytown Farmers Market]] and one at the [[locations/bryant-community-center|Bryant Community Center]] ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). Two additional monitors were ordered and ready for installation in Year 3, with a Year 3 priority to install at least four more air quality monitors throughout the city ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]).
