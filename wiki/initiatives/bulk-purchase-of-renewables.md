The Bulk Purchase of Renewables initiative is projected to reduce 85,000 MT CO2e, representing 3.9% of total community emissions, at a total cost of $605,000 and approximately $7 per ton ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Co-benefits include local energy production, air quality improvement, job creation, resilience, economic savings, and scalability ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
