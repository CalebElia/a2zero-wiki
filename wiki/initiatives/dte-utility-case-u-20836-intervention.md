The City of Ann Arbor intervened in Michigan Public Service Commission case U-20836 to challenge [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]]'s proposed rate increase and proposed changes to compensation structures for customers with solar systems ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). This intervention was undertaken to protect the financial interests of Ann Arbor residents who had invested in residential solar, including those who participated in the [[initiatives/solarize-ann-arbor|Ann Arbor Solarize Program]] ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). In Year Three, the City submitted testimony in U-20836 objecting to the proposed rate increase, challenging proposed new distributed generation rates, promoting increased reliability standards, supporting new clean energy and energy storage pilots, and calling for improved streetlighting services and rates ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The City also intervened and submitted testimony in three additional MPSC cases — U-21172, U-21297, and U-21376 — to improve renewable energy opportunities, decrease rates, and improve reliability ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). These interventions link grid decarbonization goals in Strategy 1 to rate equity and distributed generation access across all electrification strategies ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|OSI]] staff also participated in multiple Michigan Public Service Commission working groups, technical conferences, and stakeholder meetings related to reliability and distributed energy resources ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
