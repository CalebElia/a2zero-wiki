The Loan Loss Reserve initiative proposes a $1,000,000 city-funded reserve to provide credit enhancements for residents with lower credit scores, enabling them to finance energy efficiency, renewable energy, fuel switching, and electric vehicle purchases ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program assumes average loan terms of 6% APR over 10 years, a seed funding leverage ratio of 1:20, and a 5% default rate ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year program cost is estimated at $1,578,000; the initiative does not directly reduce GHG emissions but enables other activities that do ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). [[actors/michigan-saves|Michigan Saves]] is the party responsible for implementation, with support from the [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] and local and regional banks ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
