title: ARPA Solar and Energy Savings at City Facilities
type: initiative

This initiative supports solar and battery storage installations on critical City of Ann Arbor facilities, funded through the American Rescue Plan Act ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). A total of $4,500,000 in ARPA funding was secured for this purpose ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]), including $2.5 million in federal support confirmed in Year Three ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).

A request for proposals was released and vendors Enerlogics and Melink Solar were selected for installation of roughly 3 MW of solar on municipal sites including Wheeler Service Center, the Water and Wastewater Treatment Plants, and the municipal airport, with construction set to begin fall 2023 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). A separate contract was executed for nearly 1 MW of solar at various Parks facilities, with installations currently underway as of Year Three ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). Installing at least 4 MW of solar at City facilities including multiple energy storage systems was identified as a Year 4 priority ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).

Additionally, [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|OSI]] released a joint RFP with the [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]] to add an additional 1 MW of solar to City facilities and up to 20 MW at University sites ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
