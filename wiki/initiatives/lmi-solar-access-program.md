Ann Arbor began developing a low-to-moderate income (LMI) solar access program during Year One of A2ZERO to expand solar access to residents who face financial barriers ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). The city won a National Community Solar Partnership (NCSP) grant for technical assistance to support this program's development ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]). Year Two plans called for the creation of a full LMI solar program ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1|a2zero-year1]]).
