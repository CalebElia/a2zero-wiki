Bus Electrification is projected to reduce 13,800 MT CO2e, representing 0.6% of total community emissions, at a total cost of $86,000,000 and approximately $5,839 per ton, making it one of the highest-cost-per-ton initiatives in the plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Co-benefits include local air quality improvement, resilience, public health, and equity benefits ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
