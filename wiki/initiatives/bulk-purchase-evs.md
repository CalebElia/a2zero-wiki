The Bulk Purchase EVs initiative is projected to reduce 122,900 MT CO2e, representing 5.6% of total community emissions, at a total cost of $700,000 and approximately $6 per ton ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Co-benefits include air quality improvement, job creation, resilience, public health, economic savings, and scalability ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
