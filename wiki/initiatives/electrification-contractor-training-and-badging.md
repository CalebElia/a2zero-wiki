The Electrification Contractor Training and Badging Program was launched in Year Two of A2Zero to ensure contractors are knowledgeable about electrification opportunities in Michigan, connecting residents with qualified professionals to assist in the transition to home electrification ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The program operates through the [[actors/michigan-saves|Michigan Saves]] Electrification Badge, a badging system created by Michigan Saves with assistance from the City of Ann Arbor, with participating contractors listed on the Michigan Saves Locator ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). By Year Three, 22 contractors statewide had achieved the Michigan Saves Electrification Badge, reflecting growing contractor capacity across Michigan ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). OSI also held a convening of 30+ contractors across multiple disciplines to discuss misconceptions around electrification, promote City electrification programs and resources, collect feedback on soon-to-be-created A2ZERO rebate programs, and identify best practices for increasing electrification through contractor collaboration ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The program also collaborated with [[actors/ibew-252|IBEW/NECA]] on electrification readiness and supported their 'The Future is Electric' campaign, which included an informational video series to help residents understand home electrification upgrades ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
