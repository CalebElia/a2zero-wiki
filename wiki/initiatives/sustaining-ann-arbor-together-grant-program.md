The [[initiatives/sustaining-ann-arbor-together-grant-program|Sustaining Ann Arbor Together Grant Program]] provides small grants to residents, schools, local nonprofits, and local businesses to advance sustainability-related activities in Ann Arbor's right of way ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program assumes $100,000 will continue to be available annually and that the program will be fully subscribed, with a goal of awarding at least 10 grants per year ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Collaborators include neighborhood associations, the [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]], and [[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]], with an equity focus on helping underserved areas unlock capital to address their specific sustainability concerns ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year estimated cost is $1,000,000, and greenhouse gas reduction potential was not calculated for this initiative ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
