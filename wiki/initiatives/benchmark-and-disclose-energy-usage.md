title: Benchmark and Disclose Energy Usage
type: initiative

The Benchmark and Disclose Energy Usage initiative establishes standardized energy audits and self-reporting requirements for homeowners, landlords, businesses, and institutions to identify where the most significant efficiency improvements can be made ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program requires building a back-end portal to automatically report energy usage between utilities and the City, with sufficient code enforcement staff to ensure compliance ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year cost is estimated at $2,370,000; the initiative does not directly reduce GHG emissions but is described as foundational for enabling other energy efficiency actions in the plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). An equity concern noted is that lower-income homeowners may face lower home sale prices or rising rents in efficient rental units as a result of the program ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
