The Transition to More Energy Efficient Homes and Businesses initiative uses a widespread group-buy program to help residents and businesses procure energy efficiency products at discounted prices through economies of scale ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The program targets retrofitting 85% of owner-occupied homes and 80% of tenant-occupied and commercial buildings to achieve a 20% electricity savings and 15% natural gas savings ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The total 10-year program cost is estimated at $950,000, and the initiative is projected to reduce community-wide GHG emissions by approximately 161,000 MTCO²e if only the community participates, or up to 242,500 MTCO²e (11.1% of community-wide emissions) if the University of Michigan also achieves a 20% reduction in total energy usage ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). All emissions reduction potential is projected to be fully realized by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
