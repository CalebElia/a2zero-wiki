The A2Zero plan proposes "upzoning" to encourage density and affordability, making zoning allowances for mixed-use neighborhoods, and encouraging tiny houses and accessory dwelling units (ADUs) ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Transit-oriented urban planning along transit corridors is identified as a key strategy to reduce vehicle miles traveled by placing residents closer to destinations and transit options ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). In pursuit of this strategy, the City of Ann Arbor rezoned approximately 400 acres to TC1 (transit corridor) status, concentrating density and mixed uses along transit corridors to reduce car dependency and support A2Zero's VMT reduction goals ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). Building affordable workforce housing near workplaces to reduce commuting is also identified as a complementary land-use strategy ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Looking ahead, a Year Four priority is to embed A2Zero goals in the community's comprehensive land use plan and unified development code ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
