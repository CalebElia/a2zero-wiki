The A2Zero plan identifies development and leveraging of existing infrastructure as resilience hubs as a key preparedness action, including activating some homes and community spaces as shelters with resources and energy ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan also calls for identifying safe gathering points in neighborhoods during power outages and extreme heat or cold events ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Needs mapping of neighborhoods based on specific resident support requirements is proposed to inform hub placement and services ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

The Resilience Hubs initiative establishes community centers as designated sites for emergency preparedness and climate resilience support for Ann Arbor residents ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). In Year Two, the first resilience hub was launched at the [[locations/northside-community-center|Northside Community Center]], and designs for a 2nd hub at [[locations/bryant-community-center|Bryant Community Center]] and a 3rd at the [[locations/ann-arbor-senior-center|Ann Arbor Senior Center]] were completed for Year 3 implementation ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). The city also deployed over 200 emergency preparedness kits to residents and staffed an emergency preparedness shelter during a 5-day power outage in August 2021 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]). By Year Three, the second resilience hub was completed at the Bryant Neighborhood Community Center, and the city also applied for an EPA grant to formally launch a resilience network and expand hubs throughout Washtenaw County ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). Creating Ann Arbor's third and fourth resilience hubs is identified as a Year Four priority ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]).
