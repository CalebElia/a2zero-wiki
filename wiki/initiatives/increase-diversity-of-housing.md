The [[initiatives/increase-diversity-of-housing|Increase Diversity of Housing]] initiative supports policies to increase housing opportunities within Ann Arbor, targeting 2,000 new units of residential multi-family housing or accessory dwelling units (ADUs) in residential neighborhoods by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The initiative assumes a 7–10% increase in housing availability, with 85% of new residential units located in mixed-use areas, reducing new occupants' commutes by 20 miles each way and increasing public transit usage ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The 10-year cost is estimated at $250,000 (staffing, hard, and soft costs), with a GHG reduction potential of 2,200 metric tons CO²e, representing 0.1% of community-wide emissions ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Allowing multi-units and promoting ADUs is identified as benefiting renters, lower-income residents, in-commuters, and project developers ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
