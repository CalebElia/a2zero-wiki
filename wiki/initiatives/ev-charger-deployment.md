Ann Arbor's EV Charger Deployment initiative expands public electric vehicle charging infrastructure across the city in coordination with multiple institutional partners. ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]]) A Year 3 priority was to deploy 50 more EV chargers in coordination with the [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]], the [[actors/downtown-development-authority|Downtown Development Authority]], the [[actors/state-of-michigan|State of Michigan]], and other collaborators. ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2|a2zero-year2]])

In Year 3, the City received a $54,000 State of Michigan grant to install the city's first utility pole EV charging program, with installation targeted for fall 2023 ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The City's light fleet reached 20% electric, with the first electric pickups and vans integrated into the fleet ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). The City also introduced its first heavy electric truck — a fully electric bucket truck for use by the Signs and Signals division — marking a milestone in heavy fleet electrification ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]]). Deploying 50 more EV chargers is identified as a Year 4 priority. ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year3|a2zero-year3]])
