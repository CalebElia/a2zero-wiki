## Cross-strategy synthesis

**Strategy 1: 100% Renewable Energy Grid** establishes Ann Arbor's foundation for decarbonization through a diverse portfolio of solar deployment—spanning rooftop installations, community solar pilots, and landfill arrays—alongside strategic interventions in [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] utility proceedings and early-stage financing mechanisms including the [[initiatives/sustainable-energy-utility|Sustainable Energy Utility]]. The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City of Ann Arbor]] and [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] lead this work in partnership with [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]], [[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]], and [[actors/washtenaw-county|Washtenaw County]], pursuing both distributed generation and bulk renewable procurement pathways. Key initiatives include [[initiatives/community-choice-aggregation|Community Choice Aggregation]] feasibility studies, [[initiatives/lmi-solar-access-program|LMI Solar Access Program]] equity measures, and [[initiatives/grid-capacity-expansion-electrification|grid capacity expansion]] planning to support electrification loads. The strategy remains in early deployment with no multi-year trend data yet available to assess penetration rates or subscription uptake.

**Strategy 2: Building Electrification** deploys a comprehensive suite of programs to transition buildings and fleets away from fossil fuels, anchored by the [[initiatives/community-climate-action-millage-rebates|Community Climate Action Millage rebates]] and the [[initiatives/appliance-hvac-electrification-requirement|appliance and HVAC electrification requirement]]. The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City]], [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|Ann Arbor Housing Commission]], and [[actors/ann-arbor-area-transit-authority|TheRide]] collaborate on residential upgrades, affordable housing retrofits, and transit fleet conversion, with [[actors/ibew-252|IBEW 252]] and [[actors/rmi|RMI]] supporting workforce development through [[initiatives/electrification-contractor-training-and-badging|contractor training and badging programs]]. Pilot projects such as [[initiatives/bryant-neighborhood-decarbonization|Bryant Neighborhood Decarbonization]] and [[initiatives/aahc-energy-improvements|AAHC energy improvements]] test equity-centered implementation models. The strategy is in its program-building phase, with enforcement timelines, grid capacity coordination with DTE, and affordability mechanisms for renters and low-income households still under development.

**Strategy 3: Building Energy Efficiency** pursues deep energy savings through code updates, weatherization expansion, and data-driven benchmarking, with the [[initiatives/community-climate-action-millage-rebates|Community Climate Action Millage]] providing new capital for residential and commercial retrofits. The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City]] and [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] coordinate [[initiatives/benchmarking-ordinance|benchmarking ordinance]] implementation, [[initiatives/update-building-codes|building code updates]], and [[initiatives/weatherization-expansion|weatherization expansion]] in partnership with [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] and [[actors/washtenaw-county|Washtenaw County]]. The [[initiatives/energy-concierge-and-community-engagement-program|Energy Concierge and Community Engagement Program]] serves as a one-stop resource for residents navigating efficiency upgrades, while [[initiatives/aahc-energy-improvements|AAHC energy improvements]] and [[initiatives/bryant-neighborhood-decarbonization|Bryant Neighborhood Decarbonization]] pilot equity-focused retrofit models in affordable housing. Enforcement mechanisms for the benchmarking ordinance, capitalization of [[initiatives/on-bill-financing-renewables-efficiency|on-bill financing]] programs, and retroactive application of updated codes remain open questions.

**Strategy 4: Vehicle Miles Traveled Reduction** combines transit expansion, active transportation infrastructure, and land-use reform to reduce car dependency and associated emissions. The [[actors/ann-arbor-area-transit-authority|Ann Arbor Area Transit Authority]] leads [[initiatives/expand-improve-local-transit|transit service expansion]] and [[initiatives/electrify-buses|bus electrification]], while the [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City]] advances [[initiatives/protected-bike-lanes-expansion|protected bike lane networks]], [[initiatives/mobility-hubs|mobility hubs]], and [[initiatives/parking-reform|parking reform]] to shift mode share toward walking, biking, and transit. [[initiatives/transit-oriented-development-zoning|Transit-oriented development zoning]] and [[initiatives/develop-mixed-use-neighborhoods|mixed-use neighborhood development]] aim to reduce trip distances, with [[initiatives/go-pass-expansion|Go-Pass expansion]] lowering cost barriers to transit access. The strategy's success hinges on securing sustainable transit funding through Act 51 reform advocacy, coordinating parking and density policies to avoid displacement, and establishing measurable VMT reduction targets tied to the [[initiatives/ghg-inventory|GHG inventory]].

**Strategy 5: Materials & Waste** pursues circular economy principles and zero-waste goals through composting expansion, reuse infrastructure, and sustainable procurement policies. The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City]] and [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] coordinate [[initiatives/composting-expansion|composting expansion]], [[initiatives/a2r3-reduce-reuse-return|A2R3 reuse programs]], and [[initiatives/electric-refuse-truck-program|electric refuse truck procurement]], with [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]] and [[actors/downtown-development-authority|Downtown Development Authority]] piloting commercial recycling and sustainable purchasing initiatives. The [[initiatives/solid-waste-resource-management-plan|Solid Waste Resource Management Plan]] update will set enforceable diversion targets, while a proposed regional methane digester awaits multi-jurisdictional partnership and funding. Most programmatic elements—including the [[initiatives/zero-waste-plan|Zero Waste Plan]], [[initiatives/sustainable-purchasing-policy|sustainable purchasing policy]], and equitable access to reuse and repair services—remain in planning or early implementation phases.

**Strategy 6: Community Resilience** builds adaptive capacity through resilience hubs, decentralized energy systems, and green infrastructure investments targeting climate hazards and equity gaps. The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City]] and [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] have operationalized the [[initiatives/northside-resilience-hub|Northside Community Center as a pilot resilience hub]], launched the [[initiatives/10000-trees-initiative|10,000 Trees Initiative]] to expand canopy in underserved areas, and deployed [[initiatives/environmental-sensors-monitoring|environmental sensor networks]] for air quality and climate monitoring. The [[initiatives/resilience-fund-equity|Resilience Fund]] provides equity-focused financial support, while [[initiatives/decentralized-energy-microgrids|microgrid planning]] explores backup power for critical facilities. Scaling beyond the Northside pilot, securing long-term funding for the Resilience Fund, prioritizing microgrid sites, and enforcing tree canopy equity goals remain open implementation questions.

**Strategy 7: Community Engagement & Equity** ensures that A2Zero's benefits and decision-making processes reach all residents, particularly those historically underserved by climate and energy programs. The [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] coordinates the [[initiatives/a2zero-ambassadors-program|A2Zero Ambassadors Program]], [[initiatives/neighborhood-resiliency-plans|neighborhood resiliency planning]], and the [[initiatives/sa2t-grant-program|SA2T grant program]] to resource community-led climate action, with [[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]] integrating [[initiatives/youth-education-carbon-neutrality|youth education on carbon neutrality]]. The [[initiatives/bryant-neighborhood-decarbonization|Bryant Neighborhood Decarbonization]] pilot tests whole-neighborhood engagement and retrofit models, while the [[initiatives/energy-concierge-and-community-engagement-program|Energy Concierge]] provides personalized support for navigating incentives and programs. Measurable equity outcomes, demographic reach targets, funding sustainability beyond initial plan cycles, and integration of engagement metrics into the [[initiatives/a2zero-metrics-dashboard|A2Zero Metrics Dashboard]] remain critical open questions as the strategy scales.
