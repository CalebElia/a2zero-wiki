## Cross-strategy synthesis

**[[strategies/strategy-1-renewable-grid|100% Renewable Energy Grid]]** has progressed from baseline planning in 2020 to active deployment across three implementation years, with [[initiatives/community-choice-aggregation|Community Choice Aggregation]] remaining the dominant emissions-reduction mechanism—though still contingent on state enabling legislation. The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City of Ann Arbor]] and [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] have advanced municipal solar installations through [[initiatives/arpa-city-facilities-solar|ARPA-funded city facility solar]] and the [[initiatives/municipal-rooftop-solar-rfp|municipal rooftop solar RFP]], while [[initiatives/solarize-ann-arbor|Solarize Ann Arbor]] and the [[initiatives/lmi-solar-access-program|LMI Solar Access Program]] aim to expand residential adoption. The strategy targets 113 MW of local generation by 2030, split between 78 MW onsite solar and 11 MW community solar, but faces open questions about installation pace and equitable access for low-to-moderate income households. [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] remains a critical actor whose grid decarbonization commitments lie largely beyond the city's direct control.

**[[strategies/strategy-2-electrification|Building Electrification]]** has evolved from a six-action framework targeting 23% GHG reduction into a multi-year implementation effort spanning homes, businesses, and fleets. Year 1 launched the [[initiatives/home-and-business-electrification|Future is Electric Campaign]] and [[initiatives/expand-ev-charging-infrastructure|EV charger deployment]], while Year 2 expanded [[initiatives/community-climate-action-millage-rebates|community millage rebates]] and contractor training, and Year 3 deepened neighborhood-scale work through [[initiatives/carbon-neutral-districts|Bryant decarbonization]] and [[initiatives/grid-capacity-expansion-electrification|grid capacity expansion]]. The [[actors/ann-arbor-area-transit-authority|Ann Arbor Area Transit Authority]] is advancing [[initiatives/electrify-buses|bus electrification]], and the [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|Ann Arbor Housing Commission]] is pursuing [[initiatives/aahc-energy-improvements|energy improvements]] across its portfolio. Open questions center on whether [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] grid capacity can keep pace with accelerating demand, and whether the proposed [[initiatives/sustainable-energy-utility|Sustainable Energy Utility]] will achieve sufficient scale to drive deep building decarbonization citywide.

**[[strategies/strategy-3-building-efficiency|Building Energy Efficiency]]** has moved from an 11-action baseline framework into active implementation across weatherization, code updates, and benchmarking. The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City]] completed [[initiatives/led-street-lighting-and-traffic-signals|LED street lighting]] conversions, expanded the [[initiatives/expand-weatherization-program|weatherization program]], and launched the [[initiatives/green-rental-housing-program|Green Rental Housing Program]] and [[initiatives/energy-concierge-and-community-engagement-program|home energy advisor services]]. The [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|Ann Arbor Housing Commission]] is pursuing [[initiatives/net-zero-energy-affordable-housing|net-zero energy retrofits]] across its affordable housing portfolio, while the [[initiatives/benchmark-and-disclose-energy-usage|benchmarking ordinance]] for commercial and multi-family buildings remains in development. Year 3 deepened district-scale work through [[initiatives/whole-home-decarbonization-assessment|whole-home decarbonization assessments]] and the [[initiatives/carbon-neutral-districts|Bryant neighborhood pilot]]. Open questions include enforcement of updated building codes, pace of AAHC retrofits, and whether [[initiatives/financing-loan-loss-reserve|loan loss reserve]] and [[initiatives/on-bill-financing-renewables-efficiency|on-bill financing]] mechanisms have achieved meaningful uptake among low-income residents.

**[[strategies/strategy-4-vmt-reduction|Vehicle Miles Traveled Reduction]]** targets a 50% VMT reduction with a $901M cost projection, advancing transit expansion, non-motorized infrastructure, and land-use reform. The [[actors/ann-arbor-area-transit-authority|Ann Arbor Area Transit Authority]] is pursuing [[initiatives/theride-electrification-expansion|bus electrification and route expansion]], while the [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City]] has advanced [[initiatives/go-pass-expansion|Go-Pass expansion]], [[initiatives/non-motorized-transportation-plan|protected bike lanes]], and [[initiatives/mobility-hubs|mobility hubs]]. Year 2 integrated [[initiatives/transit-oriented-development-zoning|transit-oriented development zoning]] into the [[initiatives/a2zero-unified-development-code-integration|Unified Development Code]], and Year 3 continued [[initiatives/park-and-ride-expansion|park-and-ride expansion]] and [[initiatives/tiered-parking-rates|parking reform]] efforts. Regional transit expansion (WALLY, BRT) remains contingent on state and federal funding given Act 51 constraints, and it is unclear how much of the 50% VMT reduction target has been achieved to date or whether the trajectory is on pace. Open questions also include whether parking reform has generated measurable mode-shift and how zoning changes translate into actual density outcomes that reduce vehicle trip generation.

**[[strategies/strategy-5-materials-waste|Materials & Waste]]** has progressed from a six-action baseline targeting 0.3% GHG reduction into steady programmatic expansion, though it remains reliant on voluntary and pilot-scale interventions. Year 1 launched the [[initiatives/move-toward-circular-economy|circular economy working group]] and [[initiatives/support-plant-rich-diet|plant-based challenge]], Year 2 advanced [[initiatives/commercial-recycling|commercial recycling Saturday service]] and a [[initiatives/move-toward-circular-economy|reusable container pilot]], and Year 3 progressed the [[initiatives/electric-refuse-truck-program|electric refuse truck program]] and [[initiatives/solid-waste-resource-management-plan|Materials Recovery Facility planning]]. The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City]] and [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] have led implementation, with [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]] as a key partner. Open questions include whether the [[initiatives/solid-waste-resource-management-plan|Solid Waste Resource Management Plan]] will codify enforceable construction and demolition waste ordinances, whether the Materials Recovery Facility renovation will be funded at the $45M+ scale, and how upstream production-phase emissions will eventually be incorporated into GHG accounting.

**[[strategies/strategy-6-resilience|Community Resilience]]** has advanced from a six-action, $7.5M baseline framework into active deployment of resilience hubs, tree canopy expansion, and environmental monitoring. Year 1 launched the [[initiatives/a2zero-ambassadors-program|Ambassadors Program]] and tree canopy planning, Year 2 advanced the [[initiatives/northside-resilience-hub|Northside Resilience Hub]] and [[initiatives/environmental-sensors-monitoring|AQMesh air quality monitoring]], and Year 3 expanded the [[initiatives/10000-trees-initiative|10,000 Trees Initiative]] into underserved areas and progressed [[initiatives/resilience-hubs|microgrid]] and [[initiatives/resilience-fund-equity|resilience fund equity]] work. The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City]], [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]], and [[actors/avalon-housing|Avalon Housing]] are key actors. Open questions include whether the [[initiatives/northside-resilience-hub|Northside Community Center Resilience Hub]] has reached full operational status and what the replication plan is for additional hubs, what the current tree canopy coverage percentage is and whether the 10,000 Trees Initiative is on track, how the [[initiatives/resilience-fund-equity|Resilience Fund Focused on Equity]] is being capitalized and governed, and whether the AQMesh sensor network has achieved sufficient geographic coverage to inform equitable interventions.

**[[strategies/strategy-7-engagement|Community Engagement & Equity]]** has evolved from a four-action baseline targeting ~14% of community-wide emissions reductions into a multi-year engagement infrastructure spanning ambassadors, grants, and neighborhood-level planning. Year 1 stood up the [[initiatives/a2zero-ambassadors-program|Ambassadors Program]], [[initiatives/sa2t-grant-program|SA2T grants]], and [[initiatives/advance-equity-programs|equity program infrastructure]], Year 2 expanded neighborhood-level engagement including [[initiatives/bryant-neighborhood-decarbonization|Bryant Neighborhood Decarbonization]] and [[initiatives/neighborhood-resiliency-plans|resiliency planning]], and Year 3 deepened community outreach channels and continued [[initiatives/offset-greenhouse-gas-emissions|offset]] and [[initiatives/internal-carbon-price|internal carbon price]] implementation. The [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|City]] and [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] lead this work, with [[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]] as a key partner. Open questions include whether the internal carbon price has been formally adopted across all City Service Areas and at what dollar-per-ton level, what volume of carbon offsets and RECs has been purchased and retired to date, how equity program impact is being measured, and whether the SA2T grant program is sufficiently funded and scaled to sustain community-led climate action beyond City-directed initiatives.

[[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] is a cross-cutting constraint: grid capacity expansion is essential for electrification, renewable energy integration depends on utility cooperation, and the [[initiatives/community-choice-aggregation|Community Choice Aggregation]] mechanism remains contingent on state enabling legislation. The [[initiatives/sustainable-energy-utility|Sustainable Energy Utility]] proposal, if realized, would integrate renewable energy, electrification, and efficiency financing into a single entity. VMT reduction through [[initiatives/transit-oriented-development-zoning|transit-oriented development zoning]] and [[initiatives/develop-mixed-use-neighborhoods|mixed-use neighborhoods]] enables building efficiency by reducing per-capita energy demand, while [[initiatives/electrify-buses|bus electrification]] links VMT reduction to the electrification strategy. Finally, the [[initiatives/ghg-inventory|GHG inventory]] and [[initiatives/a2zero-carbon-neutrality-plan|A2Zero Carbon Neutrality Plan]] serve as cross-strategy measurement and accountability mechanisms, tracking progress and identifying gaps across all seven strategies.
