## Cross-strategy synthesis

**Strategy 1: 100% Renewable Energy Grid** has established a multi-pronged decarbonization framework anchored by [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|the City of Ann Arbor]]'s intervention in [[initiatives/dte-utility-case-u-20836-intervention|DTE Energy's utility rate case]] and exploration of [[initiatives/community-choice-aggregation|Community Choice Aggregation]] as an alternative procurement model. [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]] remains the incumbent utility, while [[initiatives/sustainable-energy-utility|the Sustainable Energy Utility]] is being designed to finance distributed solar across residential and commercial properties. Early deployment includes [[initiatives/arpa-city-facilities-solar|ARPA-funded solar on city facilities]], [[initiatives/lmi-solar-access-program|low- and moderate-income solar access programs]], and [[initiatives/community-solar-pilot|a community solar pilot]], though financing mechanisms and grid capacity expansion to support accelerating electrification remain open questions. [[actors/university-of-michigan|the University of Michigan]], [[actors/washtenaw-county|Washtenaw County]], and [[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]] are institutional partners in [[initiatives/bulk-purchase-of-renewables|bulk renewable energy procurement]].

**Strategy 2: Building Electrification** spans residential, commercial, and municipal sectors through [[initiatives/home-and-business-electrification|home and business electrification programs]], [[initiatives/appliance-hvac-electrification-requirement|mandatory appliance and HVAC electrification requirements]], and [[initiatives/ev-charger-deployment|EV charger deployment]] led by [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|the City]] and [[actors/ann-arbor-area-transit-authority|TheRide]]. [[initiatives/geothermal-districts|Geothermal district pilots]] and [[initiatives/bryant-neighborhood-decarbonization|the Bryant Neighborhood Decarbonization project]] test scalable models, while [[initiatives/electrification-contractor-training-and-badging|contractor training and badging programs]] developed with [[actors/ibew-252|IBEW Local 252]] aim to build local workforce capacity. [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|The Ann Arbor Housing Commission]] is pursuing [[initiatives/aahc-energy-improvements|deep energy retrofits]] in public housing, though enforcement mechanisms for mandatory electrification and equity safeguards for low-income renters remain under development. [[actors/rmi|RMI]] provides technical assistance on heat pump deployment and building decarbonization pathways.

**Strategy 3: Building Energy Efficiency** establishes a regulatory and programmatic baseline through [[initiatives/benchmarking-ordinance|commercial and multi-family benchmarking]], [[initiatives/update-building-codes|updated building codes]], and [[initiatives/weatherization-expansion|expanded weatherization services]] coordinated by [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|the City]] and [[actors/washtenaw-county|Washtenaw County]]. [[initiatives/energy-concierge-and-community-engagement-program|The Energy Concierge program]] provides technical assistance to residents and businesses, while [[initiatives/on-bill-financing-renewables-efficiency|on-bill financing]] and a loan loss reserve are being designed to reduce upfront cost barriers. [[actors/ann-arbor-housing-commission|The Housing Commission]] is piloting [[initiatives/net-zero-energy-affordable-housing|net-zero energy affordable housing]], and [[actors/washtenaw-area-apartment-association|the Washtenaw Area Apartment Association]] is engaged on multi-family efficiency standards. Adoption rates for benchmarking, capitalization of financing tools, and state preemption constraints on local energy codes remain unresolved.

**Strategy 4: Vehicle Miles Traveled Reduction** combines active transportation infrastructure, transit expansion, and land-use reform to shift mode share away from single-occupancy vehicles. [[actors/ann-arbor-area-transit-authority|TheRide]] is expanding service frequency and coverage through [[initiatives/expand-improve-local-transit|transit expansion initiatives]] and [[initiatives/theride-electrification-expansion|fleet electrification]], while [[actors/city-of-ann-arbor|the City]] is implementing [[initiatives/non-motorized-transportation-plan|the Non-Motorized Transportation Plan]], [[initiatives/parking-reform|parking reforms]], and [[initiatives/transit-oriented-development-zoning|transit-oriented development zoning]]. [[initiatives/go-pass-expansion|Go-Pass expansion]] to [[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]] and other institutions aims to reduce commuter trips, and [[initiatives/mobility-hubs|mobility hubs]] are being piloted to integrate bike-share, EV charging, and transit access. Measurable VMT reduction targets, Act 51 transit funding reform outcomes, and equity considerations for underserved communities remain open questions.

**Strategy 5: Materials & Waste** is developing a circular economy framework through [[initiatives/circular-economy-strategy|the Circular Economy Strategy]], [[initiatives/zero-waste-plan|Zero Waste Plan]], and [[initiatives/solid-waste-resource-management-plan|Solid Waste Resource Management Plan]] led by [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|the Office of Sustainability and Innovations]]. [[initiatives/composting-expansion|Composting expansion]] and [[initiatives/electric-refuse-truck-program|electric refuse truck pilots]] are underway, while [[initiatives/materials-recovery-facility-renovation|Materials Recovery Facility renovation]] and [[initiatives/regional-methane-digester|a regional methane digester]] are in planning stages with [[actors/washtenaw-county|Washtenaw County]]. [[initiatives/a2r3-reduce-reuse-return|The A2R3 program]] promotes reuse and repair, and [[actors/ann-arbor-downtown-development-authority|the Downtown Development Authority]] is piloting commercial waste diversion programs. Formal diversion rate targets, MRF renovation funding, and regional digester financing remain unresolved.

**Strategy 6: Community Resilience** establishes equity-centered infrastructure through [[initiatives/resilience-hubs|resilience hubs]], [[initiatives/10000-trees-initiative|tree canopy expansion targeting underserved areas]], and [[initiatives/decentralized-energy-microgrids|decentralized energy microgrids]]. [[initiatives/northside-resilience-hub|The Northside Resilience Hub]] at Northside Community Center is operational, providing cooling, backup power, and community services, while [[initiatives/resilience-fund-equity|a Resilience Fund]] is being designed to support equity-focused adaptation projects. [[initiatives/neighborhood-climate-ambassadors|Neighborhood Climate Ambassadors]] and [[initiatives/environmental-sensors-monitoring|environmental sensor networks]] are building community capacity and data infrastructure, with [[actors/avalon-housing|Avalon Housing]] piloting resilience interventions in affordable housing. Additional hub sites, Resilience Fund capitalization, microgrid financing, and integration with [[actors/dte-energy|DTE Energy]]'s grid infrastructure remain open questions.

**Strategy 7: Community Engagement & Equity** centers grassroots outreach and neighborhood-scale action through [[initiatives/a2zero-ambassadors-program|the A2Zero Ambassadors Program]], [[initiatives/sa2t-grant-program|SA2T community grants]], and [[initiatives/energy-concierge-and-community-engagement-program|the Energy Concierge service]] coordinated by [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|the Office of Sustainability and Innovations]]. [[initiatives/bryant-neighborhood-decarbonization|The Bryant Neighborhood Decarbonization pilot]] is testing integrated electrification, efficiency, and solar deployment in a historically underserved area, while [[initiatives/neighborhood-resiliency-plans|Neighborhood Resiliency Plans]] are being developed to integrate climate adaptation with emergency management. [[initiatives/youth-education-carbon-neutrality|Youth education programs]] engage [[actors/ann-arbor-public-schools|Ann Arbor Public Schools]], and [[initiatives/carbon-neutrality-coordinating-committee|the Carbon Neutrality Coordinating Committee]] provides governance oversight. Measurable equity outcomes, Bryant pilot evaluation for replication, and long-term funding sustainability for engagement programs remain unresolved.
