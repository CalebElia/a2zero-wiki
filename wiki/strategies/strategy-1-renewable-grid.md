Strategy 1 is the single largest contributor to A²ZERO's emissions reduction portfolio, targeting a transition to 100% renewable electricity for the entire community through four complementary actions projected to reduce 41% of community-wide emissions at a combined cost of approximately $4.1 million ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). The dominant mechanism is Community Choice Aggregation (CCA), an opt-out bulk procurement program that would allow the City to purchase 100% new renewable energy on behalf of all residents, businesses, and municipal accounts. CCA alone is projected to reduce 462,100 MTCO₂e (21% of community-wide emissions) if only the non-UM community participates, rising to 784,000 MTCO₂e (~36%) if the University of Michigan joins — an additional 321,900 MTCO₂e attributable to UM participation. CCA requires state enabling legislation — a critical dependency — with the plan's timeline projecting legislation by 2025, a local public vote in 2026, and program launch in 2027. The City began working with legislators in 2021 toward this goal ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]).

Complementing CCA, the plan proposes a bulk purchase program for onsite renewable energy and battery storage (the Solarize program), targeting 78 MW of local generation by 2030 at a program administration cost of $605,000. Projected: 85,000 MTCO₂e reduced (3.9% of community-wide emissions) with UM participation ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). A Community Solar Program targeting 11 MW is designed specifically for residents who cannot install rooftop solar — renters, those with shaded roofs, and low-income households — and also requires separate state enabling legislation. Projected: 11,500 MTCO₂e reduced (0.5%) at a cost of $205,000 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). Finally, the Landfill Solar Project — a 24 MW utility-scale installation on the City-owned former landfill and adjacent Pittsfield Township land, developed in partnership with DTE Energy — is designed to offset roughly 80% of Ann Arbor's municipal energy use. The project incorporates a pilot of floating solar panels on a retention pond, provides a portion of clean energy for Pittsfield Township's municipal operations, and creates habitat for local pollinators. Projected: 23,000 MTCO₂e reduced (1%) with full operation by 2023 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]).

The strategy's emissions reduction profile is heavily back-loaded: the CCA's full impact is realized only after its 2027 launch, creating the sharp drop in electricity-related emissions visible in the plan's 'win scenario' chart. Prior to 2027, the onsite renewables and landfill solar actions carry the bulk of near-term reductions. The plan's modeling assumes that all additional electricity demand from electrification is incorporated into the CCA's renewable procurement, making Strategy 1 a foundational dependency for Strategies 2 and 3.
