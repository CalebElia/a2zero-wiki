Strategy 4 targets a reduction of at least 50% in vehicle miles traveled (VMT) through seven actions spanning active transportation infrastructure, transit expansion, land use reform, and parking policy. Combined, these actions are projected to reduce just over 8% of community-wide emissions at a cost exceeding $901 million — the most capital-intensive strategy in the plan, driven primarily by transit infrastructure costs ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). The plan notes that transportation emissions are on the rise, making VMT reduction essential to prevent gains in other sectors from being offset.

The two largest investments are transit-focused. Expanding and improving local transit — including bus rapid transit along major corridors, quadrupling current bus ridership (assuming an average of 3.5 miles per trip), and extending the Get Downtown Program to all Ann Arbor employers with more than 20 employees — is projected to reduce 93,200 MTCO₂e (4.3%) at a cost of $656.8 million, with a goal that 95% of residents have transit access within a 10-minute walk at 20-minute frequencies by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). Expanding park-and-ride capacity by 10x (from approximately 1,300 to approximately 13,000 spaces) with seamless transit connections is projected to reduce 28,400 MTCO₂e (1.3%) at a cost of $170 million, targeting a 25% reduction in commuter trips from the city border to destination ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). Regional transit connecting Ann Arbor to Detroit, Ypsilanti, Chelsea, Brighton, and other jurisdictions — serving the 80,000+ daily commuters — is modeled to capture 25% of regional commuting trips by 2030; the plan's regional transit modeling also incorporates work-from-home programs, estimating 5% participation for commutes of 10 miles or less and 10% for commutes over 10 miles, with costs largely captured in the local transit line item.

The Non-Motorized Transportation Plan calls for 5 miles of new (primarily protected) bike lanes per year, filling 90% of sidewalk gaps by 2030, and achieving 25% of in-city trips by walking or bicycling. Projected: 60,400 MTCO₂e reduced (2.8%) at a cost of $2.4 million ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). Land use actions address structural drivers of VMT: increasing housing diversity targets 2,000 new multifamily or ADU units by 2030, while enabling mixed-use zoning — with a goal that 20% of in-city trips are done by walking or biking by 2030 — allows daily destinations to be accessible on foot. Tiered parking rates, including dynamic pricing, elimination of parking minimums, and lower rates for EVs and low-income drivers, are designed to disincentivize driving while repurposing street space for pedestrians and cyclists. Equity is a recurring theme: transit investments are noted to generate 31% more jobs per dollar than road construction, and the plan emphasizes keeping fares affordable and ensuring low-income commuters benefit from park-and-ride access.
