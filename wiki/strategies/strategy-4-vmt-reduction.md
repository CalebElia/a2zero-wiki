Strategy 4 targets a reduction of at least 50% in vehicle miles traveled (VMT) through seven actions spanning active transportation infrastructure, transit expansion, land use reform, and parking policy. Combined, these actions are projected to reduce just over 8% of community-wide emissions at a cost exceeding $901 million — the most capital-intensive strategy in the plan, driven primarily by transit infrastructure costs ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). The plan notes that transportation emissions are on the rise, making VMT reduction essential to prevent gains in other sectors from being offset.

The two largest investments are transit-focused. Expanding and improving local transit — including bus rapid transit along major corridors, quadrupling current bus ridership, and extending the Get Downtown Program to all Ann Arbor employers with more than 20 employees — is projected to reduce 93,200 MTCO₂e (4.3%) at a cost of $656.8 million ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). The [[initiatives/non-motorized-transportation-plan|Non-Motorized Transportation Plan]] calls for 5 miles of new protected bike lanes per year, filling 90% of sidewalk gaps by 2030, and achieving 25% of in-city trips by walking or bicycling — projected to reduce 60,400 MTCO₂e (2.8%) at a cost of $2.4 million ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). Land use actions address structural drivers of VMT: increasing housing diversity, mixed-use zoning, and tiered parking rates including dynamic pricing and elimination of parking minimums ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]).

Year 2 activity under this strategy remained comparatively limited but included several meaningful steps. [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|OSI]] launched a collaboration with the Planning Department to integrate A2ZERO into the [[initiatives/unified-development-code|Unified Development Code]], and the Planning Department removed parking minimums from plan review requirements — a structural land use reform aligned with the strategy's goals ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2]]). OSI supported expansion of protected bike lanes and continued the [[initiatives/bike-discount-program|bicycle discount program]] with local vendors ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2]]). OSI also initiated co-development of a 'carbon calculator' using transportation metrics to inform the plan review process, embedding VMT considerations directly into development approvals ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2]]). Year 3 plans include embedding A2ZERO in the community's comprehensive land use plan and Unified Development Code ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year2]]). The modest Year 2 footprint continues to reflect the capital-intensive and multi-agency nature of transit and infrastructure investments, which require longer lead times and external partnerships before significant progress can be reported.
