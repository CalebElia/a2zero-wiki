Strategy 7 — labeled 'Other' in the plan — serves as a catch-all for cross-cutting programs that enable and amplify the other six strategies, while also closing any remaining emissions gap through offsets. The four actions collectively account for just under 14% of community-wide emissions reductions at a cost of approximately $10.7 million, with offsets providing the bulk of that reduction ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]).

The Equity Programs action commits OSI to embedding equity in all of its programs, ensuring staff reflect the diversity of Ann Arbor and Washtenaw County, and building authentic relationships with low-income communities and communities of color, with a goal that by 2028 all OSI programs have an explicit equity focus. The Sustaining Ann Arbor Together (SA2T) Grant Program provides small grants — $100,000 annually — to residents, schools, nonprofits, and businesses for sustainability-related activities in Ann Arbor's right of way, with at least 10 grants awarded per year. An Internal Carbon Price mechanism charges City Service Areas for each ton of GHG they emit — piloted at $5/ton in 2020 and rising to $10/ton in 2021 — with revenues recycled into energy efficiency and renewable energy improvements at City facilities, creating a behavioral and financial incentive for municipal operations to decarbonize ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]).

Greenhouse gas offsets are positioned explicitly as a last resort — used only to close the gap between what can be achieved locally and the 2030 carbon neutrality target, with the first offset purchase not planned until 2028. Projected: 298,500 MTCO₂e reduced (13.6% of community-wide emissions) at a cost of $9.44 million, assuming UM participates in all plan actions. Without UM participation in any actions, the offset requirement rises dramatically — the plan's chart shows approximately 925,000 MTCO₂e annually would need to be offset through 2029 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). The plan specifies that all offsets must be additional, that renewable energy credits will be retired, and that preference will be given to projects in low-income and frontline communities — including potentially in the global south. The plan also identifies a minimum staffing requirement of 15 FTEs as essential to implementation success across all seven strategies.
