Strategy 7 — labeled 'Other' in the plan — serves as a catch-all for cross-cutting programs that enable and amplify the other six strategies, while also closing any remaining emissions gap through offsets. The four actions collectively account for just under 14% of community-wide emissions reductions at a cost of approximately $10.7 million, with offsets providing the bulk of that reduction ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). Year One was particularly active on engagement and institutional infrastructure, with [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|OSI]] building out the organizational scaffolding needed to sustain a decade-long community transition.

The [[initiatives/equity-programs|Equity Programs]] action commits OSI to embedding equity in all of its programs, ensuring staff reflect the diversity of [[locations/ann-arbor|Ann Arbor]] and [[locations/washtenaw-county|Washtenaw County]], and building authentic relationships with low-income communities and communities of color, with a goal that by 2028 all OSI programs have an explicit equity focus ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). In Year One, OSI supported the creation of the City's equitable engagement initiative; Year Two plans include support for an Equitable Engagement Steering Committee, signaling a shift from broad outreach toward more embedded community relationships ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1]]). The [[initiatives/sustaining-ann-arbor-together-grant-program|Sustaining Ann Arbor Together (SA2T) Grant Program]] provides $100,000 annually in small grants to residents, schools, nonprofits, and businesses for sustainability activities, with at least 10 grants awarded per year; the program was continued in Year One ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]], [[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1]]). The [[initiatives/internal-carbon-price|Internal Carbon Price]] mechanism charges City Service Areas for each ton of GHG they emit — piloted at $5/ton in 2020 and rising to $10/ton in 2021 — with revenues recycled into energy efficiency and renewable energy improvements at City facilities, creating a behavioral and financial incentive for municipal operations to decarbonize ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). The plan also identifies a minimum staffing requirement of 15 FTEs as essential to implementation success across all seven strategies ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]). Greenhouse gas offsets are positioned as a last resort to close the gap between local achievements and the 2030 carbon neutrality target, with the first offset purchase not planned until 2028; projected to reduce 298,500 MTCO₂e (13.6%) at a cost of $9.44 million assuming [[actors/university-of-michigan|UM]] participates, rising dramatically without UM ([[sources/cap/cap-2020]]).

The most visible Year One accomplishment was the rapid growth of the collaborators network: 92 organizations were secured as A2ZERO collaborators ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1]]). OSI launched the [[initiatives/re4m-learning-series|RE4M internal learning series]] — a 4-part program focused on pairing renewable energy, efficiency, electrification, and equity — crafted and launched an [[initiatives/a2zero-pledge|A2ZERO Pledge]] for community members to commit to sustainability goals, and launched a [[initiatives/carbon-neutrality-coordinating-committee|Carbon Neutrality Coordinating Committee]] to provide ongoing governance coordination ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1]]). Public engagement in Year One was extensive and multi-channel, including a Sustainability Forum series, Climate Coffee and Conversation open discussions, a revised website, a new YouTube channel, A2ZERO Week (celebrating the one-year anniversary of plan adoption), regular neighborhood association meetings, an OSI stall at the Farmer's Market, the 51st-annual Earth Day celebration, an inaugural Juneteenth Celebration in conjunction with the [[actors/naacp-ann-arbor|Ann Arbor Branch of the NAACP]], a presentation at the 2021 Michigan Sustainability Conference, and a keynote at the Washtenaw Community College Climate Summit ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1]]). OSI was recognized as a finalist for the America's Climate Leadership Award. Institutionally, OSI participated in the Environmental Rules Review Committee, joined multiple committees developing the MI Healthy Climate Plan for the [[actors/state-of-michigan|State of Michigan]], and served on UM's [[actors/presidents-commission-on-carbon-neutrality|Presidents Commission on Carbon Neutrality]] — demonstrating engagement at city, state, and university levels ([[sources/annual-reports/a2zero-year1]]).
