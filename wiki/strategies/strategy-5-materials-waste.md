Strategy 5 of the A2Zero plan calls for changing the way Ann Arbor uses, reuses, and disposes of materials ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). This strategy is one of seven overarching strategies that together are projected to eliminate 2.1 million metric tons of CO2e annually by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

Strategy 5 focuses on changing Ann Arbor's relationship with materials purchasing, use, and disposal, with six specific actions identified to achieve its goals. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) Combined, the six actions under this strategy reduce baseline community-wide greenhouse gas emissions by 0.3% at a total cost of just over $45,000,000. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) Calculations in this strategy focus only on greenhouse gas savings from reducing material disposal; upstream emissions from extracting and creating goods are not yet included in calculations. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The A2Zero plan's resource reduction topic covers waste reduction, recycling, organics, plant-based diets, local food production, circular economy, sustainable purchasing, construction materials, and community engagement as ideas considered for the Materials & Waste strategy ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Key proposed actions include creating a Zero Waste Plan, expanding composting access, reducing single-use plastics, renovating the Materials Recovery Facility, and scaling circular economy infrastructure such as repair centers and tool libraries ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
