Sub-Timelines as Filtered Views of the Blackboard

Sub-timelines are not separate files you maintain. They are the blackboard filtered by topic, location, strategy, actor, or funding type. The enabling mechanism is thorough multi-dimensional tagging on every quad during ingest.

One Quad — Multiple Dimensional Tags (Ingest Output)

When the LLM ingests "DOE awards $10M grant for Bryant geothermal in September 2024", it emits this quad. Every relevant dimension is tagged — this single event is then retrievable from the geothermal sub-timeline, the Bryant sub-timeline, the federal-grant sub-timeline, AND the Strategy 1 sub-timeline simultaneously.

The ingest rule that makes this work: When the LLM emits a quad, it must tag every relevant dimension — not just the most obvious one. A Bryant geothermal grant is tagged with geothermal, bryant, federal-grant, strategy-1, strategy-6, actors/osi, and actors/us-doe. This single quad then surfaces correctly in six different filtered sub-timelines. Conservative tagging (tag only the "primary" topic) is the failure mode — it creates sub-timelines with artificial gaps where events were tagged too narrowly.
Dark Matter Detection — Scoped to a Sub-Timeline

Gap detection can run on the full blackboard or on any filtered sub-timeline. "What are the dark matter gaps specifically in the geothermal story?" produces targeted interview questions, not a generic list of all gaps.

⬛ Dark Matter — Geothermal Sub-Timeline (filter: keywords ∋ "geothermal")

"Has the Stantec citywide geothermal feasibility study been completed? Where are the results published?"
