Ann Arbor is the Michigan city that is the geographic scope of the A2Zero carbon neutrality initiative, with community-wide greenhouse gas emissions of 2.1 million metric tons as of 2018. (source: cap-2020) The average annual temperature in Ann Arbor has already increased nearly 1°F since the 1900s, and temperatures are expected to rise by 3–7°F more by 2050. (source: cap-2020) Average annual precipitation has increased by 44% since the 1950s, and the total amount of rain falling during extreme events has increased by 37% since 1981. (source: cap-2020)
