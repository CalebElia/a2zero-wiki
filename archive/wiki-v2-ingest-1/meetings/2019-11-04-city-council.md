At the November 4, 2019 City Council meeting, the council passed a resolution declaring a climate emergency and supporting a public engagement process to outline how the entire Ann Arbor community could achieve carbon neutrality by 2030. (source: cap-2020) The council requested that the City Administrator develop a draft 2030 Carbon Neutral Ann Arbor Plan to be presented no later than March 31, 2020. (source: cap-2020) The council also directed the City Administrator to seek collaboration with the University of Michigan and the President's Commission on Carbon Neutrality (PCCN). (source: cap-2020)
