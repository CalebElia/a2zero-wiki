Ann Arbor Public Schools was one of 67 organizations that signed up as a partner of A2Zero as of early March 2020. (source: cap-2020) The City sent 11,000 backpack fliers to all K-5th grade students in Ann Arbor Public Schools as part of A2Zero outreach efforts. (source: cap-2020)

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
Ann Arbor Public Schools is listed as a collaborator on the Neighborhood and Youth Ambassador Program, the Neighborhood Asset and Needs Mapping initiative, and the Emergency Preparedness Kits Program under Strategy 6. (source: cap-2020) The plan assumes AAPS will partner with the City on hosting emergency preparedness events. (source: cap-2020)

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
Ann Arbor Public Schools is listed as a collaborator and project co-designer for the Sustaining Ann Arbor Together Grant Program. (source: cap-2020)

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
Ann Arbor Public Schools (AAPS) was identified in the A2Zero planning process as a potential site for community solar development on school property. (source: cap-2020) AAPS was also identified as a partner for incorporating plant-based diet programs, food planning and purchasing, composting at schools, and school curriculum on environmental science. (source: cap-2020)
