The Office of Sustainability and Innovations designed the A2Zero planning process around five pillars: public engagement, technical advisory committees, partner support, staff expertise, and peer learning and literature review. (source: cap-2020) The office hosted 68 public events between November 13, 2019 and March 12, 2020 as part of the public engagement pillar. (source: cap-2020) The office also created the A2Zero website, a listserv, a meeting-in-a-box toolkit, and hired a communications firm to brand the city's carbon neutrality work. (source: cap-2020)

The Office of Sustainability and Innovations is the party responsible for implementing multiple Strategy 6 actions, including the Resilience Hubs, Neighborhood and Youth Ambassador Programs, Neighborhood Asset and Needs Mapping, and Emergency Preparedness Kits Program. (source: cap-2020) OSI also serves as a supporting collaborator on the Local Tree Canopy Preservation and Enhancement initiative and the Environmental Sensors Monitoring Network. (source: cap-2020)

The Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI) is the party responsible for implementing all four actions under the Other Actions strategy, including the Equity Program, Sustaining Ann Arbor Together Grant Program, Internal Carbon Price, and Greenhouse Gas Emissions Offsets. (source: cap-2020) OSI is committed to ensuring climate protection and mitigation work improves the lives of Ann Arbor residents who have been historically underrepresented and under-resourced. (source: cap-2020)

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI) was created in FY19 to actualize the climate goals created and reaffirmed by City Council. (source: cap-2020) OSI developed a 5-year work plan aimed at helping the city achieve a 25% reduction in community-wide greenhouse gas emissions by 2025, based on 2000 levels. (source: cap-2020) Funding for public engagement related to the carbon neutrality planning process was available in the FY2020 OSI budget. (source: cap-2020)
