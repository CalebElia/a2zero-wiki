The Ann Arbor City Council unanimously declared a climate emergency and directed the City to immediately begin crafting an aggressive and living strategy to achieve community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030. (source: cap-2020)

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
Ann Arbor City Council unanimously adopted a Climate Emergency Declaration on November 4th, 2019, committing the city to achieving community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030. (source: cap-2020) The Council also committed to charting a path for how the entire Ann Arbor community could achieve this goal. (source: cap-2020)

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
Ann Arbor City Council passed Resolution 19-2103 in November 2019, directing the City to create a living plan for achieving community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030. (source: cap-2020) The Council called for the plan to be developed with public input and presented by Earth Day 2020. (source: cap-2020)
