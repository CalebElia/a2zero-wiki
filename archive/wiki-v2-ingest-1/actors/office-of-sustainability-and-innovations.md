The Office of Sustainability and Innovations is the City of Ann Arbor government office responsible for launching A2Zero, the city's carbon neutrality planning and implementation initiative. (source: cap-2020) The office initiated the A2Zero work following the passage of City Council Resolution 19-2103 in November 2019. (source: cap-2020)

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The Office of Sustainability and Innovations initiated A2Zero on November 13th, 2019 with a plan completion deadline of mid-March 2020. (source: cap-2020) The office mobilized dozens of community partners and volunteers, thousands of members of the public, dozens of technical advisors, and peer communities to provide input into the Plan within a four-month period. (source: cap-2020) The office designed the A2Zero planning process around five pillars: public engagement, technical advisory committees, partner support, staff expertise, and peer learning and literature review. (source: cap-2020)

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The Office of Sustainability and Innovations (OSI) is the party responsible for implementing all four Strategy 1 CAP actions, including Community Choice Aggregation, the Onsite Renewables Bulk Buy program, the Community Solar Program, and the Landfill Solar Project. (source: cap-2020) OSI's Solarize Coordinator specifically leads the Onsite Renewable Energy Generation with Battery Storage program with support from the broader OSI team. (source: cap-2020) OSI also intends to integrate energy efficiency and electrification support into the bulk buy program so that each household or business receives a package of support rather than multiple separate programmatic offerings. (source: cap-2020)
