The City of Ann Arbor committed to developing a 24MW utility-scale solar project with DTE Energy on the City-owned landfill and in Pittsfield Township, intended to offset roughly 80% of Ann Arbor municipal energy usage. (source: cap-2020) The project incorporates ground-mounted solar, a pilot of solar panels on a retention pond, and a portion of clean energy for Pittsfield Township to offset their municipal operations. (source: cap-2020) Full operation was targeted by end of 2023, with feasibility and technical assessments and an RFP release planned for 2020. (source: cap-2020) The initiative is projected to reduce GHG emissions by 1%, equivalent to 23,000 metric tons of CO2e, at an estimated 10-year staffing support cost of $80,000, with final project costs to be determined based on grid upgrade requirements. (source: cap-2020)
