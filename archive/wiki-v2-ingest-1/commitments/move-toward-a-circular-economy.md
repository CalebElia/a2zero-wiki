This action focuses on moving towards a circular economy, including actions to promote resource reduction, material reuse, and regeneration, and by working with peer municipalities to create tools for transparency, traceability, and calculation of embedded greenhouse gas emissions for different materials, goods, and services. (source: cap-2020) Indicators of success include promoting repair centers for bicycles and appliances, supporting toy, book, and tool libraries, and supporting thrift stores and reuse centers along with curbside textile collection. (source: cap-2020) The City noted there is no existing City-coordinated textile collection program; curbside collection of textiles would be contracted with a private collector to provide weekly curbside collection from single-family homes initially, with potential future expansion to multi-family properties. (source: cap-2020) The estimated total cost over 10 years is $270,000; greenhouse gas reduction potential has not been calculated. (source: cap-2020)
