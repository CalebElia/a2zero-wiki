Ann Arbor committed to working with partners and community organizations to educate residents on emergency preparedness and to assemble and distribute emergency preparedness kits. (source: cap-2020) The action prioritizes low-income residents who may face cost, language, or other barriers to building emergency preparedness kits on their own. (source: cap-2020) The goal is for 1,000 residents to have emergency preparedness kits by 2030 as a result of this action. (source: cap-2020) The estimated 10-year cost is $350,000, and greenhouse gas reduction potential was not calculated for this action. (source: cap-2020)
