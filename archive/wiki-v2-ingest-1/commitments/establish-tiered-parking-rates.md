type: commitment

This commitment establishes a sliding parking fee structure that increases the cost to park while offering lower rates for low-income residents, residents with disabilities, and electric vehicle drivers, including deploying smart metering and dynamic pricing. (source: cap-2020) Excess on-street parking in the DDA will be removed and replaced with infrastructure for pedestrians, bicyclists, transit, and street activities, and all parking minimums will be eliminated with low parking maximums set through the plan review process. (source: cap-2020) A full-scale launch of tiered rates was planned for 2022, with a pilot of dynamic parking rates in the DDA planned for 2021. (source: cap-2020) The 10-year cost is estimated at $4,500,000, and the greenhouse gas reduction potential is captured in other actions and not independently quantified. (source: cap-2020)
