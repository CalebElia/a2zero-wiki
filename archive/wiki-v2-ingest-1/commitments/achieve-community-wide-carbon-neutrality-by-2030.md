Ann Arbor committed to achieving carbon neutrality community-wide by the year 2030, following the unanimous adoption of the Climate Emergency Declaration on November 4th, 2019. (source: cap-2020) To achieve this goal, the entire Ann Arbor community must eliminate 2.1 million metric tons of carbon dioxide equivalent emissions annually, which represents the community's total greenhouse gas emissions as measured in 2018. (source: cap-2020) The seven overarching strategies and 44 accompanying actions outlined in the A2Zero Plan are projected to reduce emissions by 2.1 million metric tons of CO2e and cost just over $1 billion additional dollars over ten years. (source: cap-2020)

Ann Arbor City Council passed Resolution 19-2103 in November 2019 committing the city to achieving community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030. (source: cap-2020) Community-wide greenhouse gas emissions were 2.1 million metric tons as of 2018, meaning the plan must reduce emissions by 2.1 million metric tons over ten years. (source: cap-2020) The commitment is structured around seven overarching strategies and 44 specific actions. (source: cap-2020)
