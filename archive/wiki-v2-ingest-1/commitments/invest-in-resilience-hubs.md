Ann Arbor committed to investing in resilience hubs — community-serving facilities augmented to support residents and coordinate resource distribution before, during, or after a natural hazard event. (source: cap-2020) The plan calls for five resilience hubs to be equitably distributed across the city, with the first hub targeted for launch in 2022 and subsequent hubs designed and launched through 2023 and beyond. (source: cap-2020) The goal is for every Ward within the City to have a functioning, community-designed and -operated resilience hub by 2030. (source: cap-2020) The estimated 10-year cost is $5,025,000, and the action is projected to contribute a 0.1% reduction (approximately 2,500 MTCO²e) in community-wide greenhouse gas emissions. (source: cap-2020)
