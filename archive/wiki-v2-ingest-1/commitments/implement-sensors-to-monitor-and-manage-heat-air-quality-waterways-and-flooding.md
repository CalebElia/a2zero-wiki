title: Implement Sensors to Monitor and Manage Heat, Air Quality, Waterways, and Flooding
type: commitment
---

Ann Arbor committed to collecting data using sensors and drones to proactively understand risks and mitigate vulnerabilities related to heat, air quality, waterways, and flooding. (source: cap-2020) The timeline calls for completing a Smart City Strategy in 2020, piloting sensors with the University of Michigan and other partners in 2021, and installing an interconnected network of sensors and monitors throughout the city by 2024. (source: cap-2020) The Ann Arbor Information Technology Unit is the party responsible for implementation, with collaborators including the University of Michigan and the Huron River Watershed Council. (source: cap-2020) The estimated 10-year cost is $300,000. (source: cap-2020)
