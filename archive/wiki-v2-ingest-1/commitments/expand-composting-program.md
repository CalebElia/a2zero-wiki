This action focuses on moving to year-round composting, diverting food and yard waste from the waste stream to reduce methane emissions and produce valuable compost for local use. (source: cap-2020) The Solid Waste Resource Management Plan estimates that increasing to year-round composting would put collection at 1,000–2,784 tons, and increasing food collection from food-related businesses would add another 1,000–2,400 tons per year. (source: cap-2020) The goal is that by 2022, year-round and commercial composting will have led to over 5,000 tons of yard and food waste diverted from the waste stream annually. (source: cap-2020) The estimated total cost over 10 years, including staffing, hard, and soft costs, is $26,000,000, with a greenhouse gas reduction potential of 0.02% of community-wide emissions (400 metric tons CO2e). (source: cap-2020)
