The City of Ann Arbor committed to creating a community solar program in partnership with DTE Energy, allowing residents and businesses to purchase solar panels from an existing solar farm and receive on-bill credits. (source: cap-2020) The program targets those who face barriers to installing solar, including renters, low-income residents, and those with shaded or obstructed roofs, with a goal of 11MW of community solar established locally by 2030. (source: cap-2020) State enabling legislation was required, with a local pilot program launch planned for 2022 and expansion in 2024. (source: cap-2020) The initiative is projected to reduce GHG emissions by 0.5%, equivalent to 11,500 metric tons of CO2e, at an estimated 10-year cost of $205,000 for staffing, hard, and soft costs. (source: cap-2020)
