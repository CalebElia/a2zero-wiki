The City proposes at least a 10x increase in park and ride spots from the current 1,300 available free spaces to reduce in-city traffic. (source: cap-2020) The vision is to displace 25% of commuter trips into the City through significantly increased park and ride infrastructure seamlessly connected to transit. (source: cap-2020) The 10-year cost is estimated at $170,000,000, excluding land purchase costs, with a greenhouse gas reduction potential of 28,400 metric tons CO2 equivalent, representing 1.3% of community-wide emissions. (source: cap-2020) New park and ride lots were planned to begin construction in 2023. (source: cap-2020)
