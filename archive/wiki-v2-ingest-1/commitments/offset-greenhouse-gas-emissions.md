The City committed to purchasing carbon offsets to cover greenhouse gas emissions that cannot be locally reduced due to lack of technically or financially feasible solutions, with offsets used only to close the gap between local actions and the 2030 carbon neutrality goal. (source: cap-2020) The first offset purchase was planned for 2028, with the amount of offsets purchased expected to decline over time as more local actions are undertaken. (source: cap-2020) The greenhouse gas reduction potential is 13.6% of community-wide emissions, equivalent to 298,500 metric tons of CO2e, assuming University of Michigan participates in all programs. (source: cap-2020) The estimated 10-year cost is $9,440,000, based on two years of purchasing offsets and staffing support to set up the purchase. (source: cap-2020)
