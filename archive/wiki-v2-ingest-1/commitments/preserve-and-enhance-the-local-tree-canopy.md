Ann Arbor committed to preserving and enhancing the local tree canopy by conducting an analysis and inventory of existing canopy and updating the master plan to incentivize shade tree and vegetation planting for private projects. (source: cap-2020) The goal is to plant 10,000 new trees on city property and in the right of way and 10,000 new trees on private property by 2030, assuming 1,000 trees planted per year in each category. (source: cap-2020) The plan notes that tree canopies in low-income communities and communities of color tend to be smaller, and calls for equitable enhancement of canopy in underserved neighborhoods to be prioritized. (source: cap-2020) The estimated 10-year cost is $1,160,000, and the action is projected to reduce community-wide emissions by 0.02% (approximately 450 MTCO²e). (source: cap-2020)
