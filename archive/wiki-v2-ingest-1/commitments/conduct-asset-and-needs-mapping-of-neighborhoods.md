Ann Arbor committed to working with neighborhoods to systematically identify assets and needs in order to create plans that prioritize and address community needs. (source: cap-2020) The action involves identifying safe gathering points and activating physical spaces as shelters during natural disasters and power outages. (source: cap-2020) The goal is for every neighborhood in Ann Arbor to have an asset map and know how to activate it in the case of an emergency by 2025. (source: cap-2020) The estimated 10-year cost is $300,000. (source: cap-2020)
