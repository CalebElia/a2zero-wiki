The City of Ann Arbor committed to implementing a Community Choice Aggregation (CCA) program allowing local government to procure 100% new renewable energy on behalf of residents, businesses, and municipal accounts from an alternative supplier. (source: cap-2020) The program is structured as an opt-out model intended to achieve economies of scale and cost parity with existing electricity rates. (source: cap-2020) CCA program launch was targeted for 2027, contingent on state enabling legislation passing by 2025 and a local public vote in 2026. (source: cap-2020) The initiative is projected to reduce community-wide GHG emissions by 21%, equivalent to 462,100 metric tons of CO2e, at an estimated 10-year cost of $3,245,000. (source: cap-2020)
