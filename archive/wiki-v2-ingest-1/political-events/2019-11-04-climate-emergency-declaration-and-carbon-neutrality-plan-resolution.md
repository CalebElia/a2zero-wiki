On November 4, 2019, the Ann Arbor City Council passed a resolution declaring a climate emergency and committing to take action as a result of that declaration. (source: cap-2020) The resolution directed the City Administrator to develop a draft 2030 Carbon Neutral Ann Arbor Plan to be presented to Council no later than March 31, 2020, in support of its presentation on Earth Day 2020. (source: cap-2020) The resolution also directed the City Administrator to design and execute a community engagement process and to seek collaboration with the University of Michigan and the President's Commission on Carbon Neutrality (PCCN). (source: cap-2020) The resolution was sponsored by Mayor Taylor and Councilmembers Bannister, Smith, Ramlawi, Grand, Ackerman, Eaton, Nelson, and Griswold. (source: cap-2020)
