type: initiative

The Emergency Preparedness Kits Program involves working with partners and community organizations to educate Ann Arborites on emergency preparedness and to assemble and distribute emergency preparedness kits and plans. (source: cap-2020) The program prioritizes low-income residents who may face cost, language, or other barriers to building kits independently, and plans to integrate emergency preparedness modules into the Neighborhood and Youth Ambassador programs by 2022. (source: cap-2020) Collaborators include the Red Cross, Meals on Wheels, Neighborhood Associations, and Ann Arbor Public Schools. (source: cap-2020) The goal is for 1,000 residents to have emergency preparedness kits by 2030, at an estimated 10-year cost of $350,000. (source: cap-2020)
