title: Community Choice Aggregation (CCA)
type: initiative
---

Community Choice Aggregation is a program allowing Ann Arbor to procure 100% new renewable energy on behalf of residents, businesses, and municipal accounts from an alternative supplier while retaining transmission and distribution services from existing utility providers. (source: cap-2020) The program is designed as an opt-out structure to achieve economies of scale and maintain close to cost parity with existing electricity rates, with the University of Michigan identified as a potential participant. (source: cap-2020) Implementation requires state enabling legislation, with the CCA program launch targeted for 2027 following a local public vote in 2026. (source: cap-2020) The Office of Sustainability and Innovations is the party responsible for implementation, collaborating with the state legislature, other Michigan municipalities, MI-MAUI, and Michigan nonprofits focused on energy choice. (source: cap-2020)
