The Environmental Sensors Monitoring Network initiative focuses on deploying sensors and drones throughout Ann Arbor to collect real-time data on heat, air quality, waterways, and flooding in order to proactively manage risks. (source: cap-2020) The Ann Arbor Information Technology Unit is the party responsible for implementation, with collaborators including the Office of Sustainability and Innovations, Emergency Management, the University of Michigan, the Huron River Watershed Council, and Downtown Development. (source: cap-2020) The timeline calls for completing a Smart City Strategy in 2020, piloting sensors with the University of Michigan in 2021, and installing an interconnected network of sensors citywide by 2024. (source: cap-2020) The estimated 10-year cost is $300,000. (source: cap-2020)
