The Circular Economy Strategy aims to create a model where goods and materials are recovered, reused, and recycled, working with peer municipalities to develop tools for transparency, traceability, and embedded greenhouse gas emissions calculation. (source: cap-2020) The strategy supports repair centers, toy and tool libraries, thrift stores, and curbside textile collection contracted with a private collector for weekly single-family home pickup with potential future expansion to multi-family properties. (source: cap-2020) The Circular Economy strategy was planned to be finalized and integrated into other planning initiatives by 2022. (source: cap-2020) The 10-year cost estimate is $270,000; greenhouse gas reduction potential has not been calculated. (source: cap-2020)
