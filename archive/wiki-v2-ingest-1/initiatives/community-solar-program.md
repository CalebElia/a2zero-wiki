The Community Solar Program allows residents and businesses to purchase solar panels from an existing solar farm and receive an on-bill credit as if those panels were on their own roof, targeting those who face barriers to installing solar directly. (source: cap-2020) The City of Ann Arbor planned to develop the program in partnership with DTE Energy, with a local pilot launch planned for 2022 following state enabling legislation in 2021. (source: cap-2020) The goal is 11MW of community solar established locally by 2030, with the landfill solar site planned to open for community subscriptions in 2026. (source: cap-2020) The Office of Sustainability and Innovations is responsible for implementation, with the State of Michigan and partner organizations as collaborators. (source: cap-2020)
