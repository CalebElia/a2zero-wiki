The Landfill Solar Project is a utility-scale solar installation being developed by the City of Ann Arbor in partnership with DTE Energy on the City-owned landfill and in Pittsfield Township, targeting 24MW of installed solar capacity. (source: cap-2020) The project is intended to offset roughly 80% of Ann Arbor municipal energy usage and includes a pilot of solar panels installed on a retention pond, as well as a portion of clean energy for Pittsfield Township. (source: cap-2020) Co-benefits include creating habitat for local pollinators, and if demand exists and enabling legislation passes, the project may be integrated into a community solar program. (source: cap-2020) Feasibility and technical assessments were planned for 2020, with full operation targeted by end of 2023. (source: cap-2020)
