type: initiative

The Resilience Hubs initiative aims to establish five community-serving facilities across Ann Arbor that support residents and coordinate resource distribution before, during, or after natural hazard events. (source: cap-2020) The hubs are designed to operate at the nexus of climate mitigation, climate adaptation, and equity, using a bottom-up approach centered on co-development and community leadership. (source: cap-2020) The first hubs are to be placed in underserved neighborhoods, with the long-term goal of at least one hub in each Ward and ideally one in every frontline community by 2030. (source: cap-2020) The Office of Sustainability and Innovations is the party responsible for implementation, with support from Emergency Management and a wide range of community collaborators. (source: cap-2020)
