The Expand Composting Program aims to move Ann Arbor to year-round composting, diverting food and yard waste from the waste stream to reduce methane emissions and produce locally usable compost. (source: cap-2020) The Department of Public Works is the party responsible for implementation, with the Office of Sustainability and Innovations as a collaborator. (source: cap-2020) The program also includes creating markets for imperfect fruits and vegetables to ensure they do not enter the waste stream, which could benefit low-income residents through more affordable produce access. (source: cap-2020) The 10-year cost estimate is $26,000,000, with a projected greenhouse gas reduction of 400 metric tons CO2e (0.02% of community-wide emissions). (source: cap-2020)
