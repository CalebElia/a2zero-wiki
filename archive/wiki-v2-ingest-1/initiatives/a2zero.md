A2Zero is the City of Ann Arbor's carbon neutrality planning and implementation initiative, launched by the Office of Sustainability and Innovations following City Council Resolution 19-2103 in November 2019. (source: cap-2020) From the outset, A2Zero was designed to be iterative, transparent, failure-positive, and grounded in justice and equity, with extensive and broad public input and engagement. (source: cap-2020) The initiative is structured around seven overarching strategies and 44 specific actions determined through public input, technical advisory committees, peer communities, and staff expertise. (source: cap-2020) A2Zero's unifying vision is: 'Together, creating and implementing a just transition to carbon neutrality, community-wide, by the year 2030.' (source: cap-2020)
