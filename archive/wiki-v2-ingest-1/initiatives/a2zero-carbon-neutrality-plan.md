The A2Zero Living Carbon Neutrality Plan was created with input from thousands of Ann Arborites over the course of an intensive four-month planning process. (source: cap-2020) The plan centers around six core strategies and one catch-all strategy, with 44 actions identified to fully implement them. (source: cap-2020) Combined, the seven strategies and 44 actions are projected to reduce emissions by 2.1 million metric tons of CO2e and cost just over $1 billion additional dollars over ten years. (source: cap-2020) The plan is considered 'living' because many conditions are expected to change during implementation, and it focuses on eliminating emissions primarily from natural gas, electricity, gasoline, coal, and diesel used by residents, visitors, students, and businesses in Ann Arbor. (source: cap-2020)
