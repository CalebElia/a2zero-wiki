Community Choice Aggregation (CCA) is a procurement mechanism allowing local governments to purchase power on behalf of their residents and businesses from an alternative supplier while retaining transmission and distribution services from existing utilities. (source: cap-2020) In Ann Arbor's context, CCA requires state enabling legislation before the city can implement the program, representing a key barrier to deployment. (source: cap-2020) The opt-out structure is intended to achieve economies of scale leading to close to or actual cost parity with existing electricity costs, making it accessible to the entire community including renters and those unable to install their own renewable energy systems. (source: cap-2020)
