The 2030 Carbon Neutral Ann Arbor Plan was initiated by a City Council resolution passed on November 4, 2019, which directed the City Administration to develop a draft plan for how the Ann Arbor community could achieve carbon neutrality by approximately 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The resolution described the 2030 target as 'both ambitious and achievable' and called for a public engagement process beginning immediately ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Funding for public engagement was noted as available in the FY2020 Office of Sustainability and Innovations budget ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). City Council also directed collaboration with the University of Michigan's President's Commission on Carbon Neutrality to create and realize the plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
