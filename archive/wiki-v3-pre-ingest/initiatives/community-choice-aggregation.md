Community Choice Aggregation (CCA) is an opt-out program allowing the City of Ann Arbor to procure 100% new renewable energy on behalf of residents, businesses, and municipal accounts from an alternative supplier while retaining transmission and distribution services from the existing utility. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) State legislation enabling CCA must be passed before the program can launch; the plan targets a 2025 legislative milestone and a 2027 program launch. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The initiative is projected to reduce community-wide GHG emissions by 21%, equivalent to 462,100 MTCO₂e, at an estimated 10-year cost of $3,245,000. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) If the University of Michigan participates, an additional 321,900 MTCO₂e could be reduced, bringing the total to approximately 784,000 MTCO₂e (~36% of community-wide emissions). ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])
