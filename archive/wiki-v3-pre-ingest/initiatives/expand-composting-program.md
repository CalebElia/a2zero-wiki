The Expand Composting Program focuses on moving to year-round composting, diverting food and yard waste from the waste stream to reduce methane emissions and produce locally usable compost. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The Solid Waste Resource Management Plan estimates that increasing to year-round composting would put collection at 1,000–2,784 tons, with an additional 1,000–2,400 tons per year from food-related businesses. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The program has a 10-year cost of $26,000,000 and a greenhouse gas reduction potential of approximately 400 metric tons of CO₂ equivalent, representing 0.02% of community-wide emissions. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) Through a new market for imperfect produce, low-income residents could benefit from access to more affordable fruits and vegetables. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])
