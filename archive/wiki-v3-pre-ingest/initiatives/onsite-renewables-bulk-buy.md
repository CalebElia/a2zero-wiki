type: initiative

The Onsite Renewables Bulk Buy program enables residents and businesses to procure solar, geothermal, and energy storage systems at discounted prices (5–15%) through economies of scale, targeting 78MW of installed local renewable capacity by 2030. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) At least one-quarter of installations are expected to include battery storage to enhance community resilience. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The initiative is projected to reduce GHG emissions by 81,000 MTCO₂e (3.7% of community-wide emissions) at a 10-year cost of $605,000, including $540,000 for staffing and $65,000 for community resident stipends. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) Benefits flow primarily to property owners, though nonprofit organizations and public institutions may also participate. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])
