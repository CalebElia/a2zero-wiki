The A2Zero Living Carbon Neutrality Plan is Ann Arbor's community-wide strategy to achieve carbon neutrality by 2030, developed following a unanimous City Council climate emergency declaration. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The plan involves the City of Ann Arbor and 60+ partner organizations working with community members and stakeholders across land use, energy, transportation, materials, and community resilience. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The plan is described as 'living,' meaning it is intended to evolve over time as implementation progresses. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The A2Zero Living Carbon Neutrality Plan outlines a path to achieve community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030, built around six core strategies and one catch-all strategy, encompassing 44 discrete actions ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan was developed with input from thousands of Ann Arborites over a four-month planning process following the November 2019 Climate Emergency Declaration ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Combined, the seven strategies and 44 actions are projected to reduce emissions by 2.1 million metric tons of CO2e and cost just over $1 billion in additional dollars over ten years ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan is considered 'living' because conditions are expected to change during implementation, and it targets the 2.1 million metric tons of CO2e emitted community-wide in 2018 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

A2Zero is Ann Arbor's community-wide carbon neutrality planning and implementation initiative, launched by the [[actors/office-of-sustainability-and-innovations|Office of Sustainability and Innovations]] following City Council Resolution 19-2103 in November 2019 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The initiative was designed to be iterative, transparent, failure-positive, and grounded in justice and equity, with extensive public input and engagement ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). As of 2018, community-wide greenhouse gas emissions totaled 2.1 million metric tons, establishing the baseline reduction target for 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The initiative integrates climate adaptation, climate mitigation, and equity as its three core components ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The A2Zero plan is a community-wide initiative with the unifying vision of creating and implementing a just transition to carbon neutrality by the year 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan is structured around five main strategies: powering the electrical grid with 100% clean and renewable energy, reducing vehicle miles traveled by at least 40%, switching appliances and vehicles from fossil fuels to electric, improving energy efficiency of homes and businesses, and changing material consumption habits ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Specific tools and supports include appliance rebates, energy vendor recommendations, financial assistance for up-front efficiency costs, and community sharing initiatives such as toy shares and tool sheds ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
