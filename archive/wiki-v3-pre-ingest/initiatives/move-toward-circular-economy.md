The Move Toward a Circular Economy initiative promotes resource reduction, material reuse, and regeneration, and aims to work with peer municipalities to create tools for transparency, traceability, and calculation of embedded greenhouse gas emissions for materials, goods, and services. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) Key actions include promoting repair centers for bicycles and appliances, supporting toy and tool libraries, supporting thrift stores and reuse centers, and contracting for curbside textile collection from single-family homes. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) The 10-year cost is estimated at $270,000; greenhouse gas reduction potential has not been calculated. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) Lower-income residents may benefit from being able to buy repurposed and refurbished items. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])
