The University of Michigan is incorporated into the A2Zero plan's 'win scenario,' which assumes that the University mirrors the actions of the City in pursuing carbon neutrality by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Under the win scenario, UM electricity emissions are projected to drop to near zero between 2026 and 2027 alongside other electricity categories ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]] was one of 67 organizations that signed up as an A2Zero partner organization by early March 2020 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The University of Michigan President's Commission on Carbon Neutrality was also listed as a separate partner entity in the A2Zero partner network ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The [[actors/university-of-michigan|University of Michigan]] is a collaborator on both the Sustaining Ann Arbor Together Grant Program and the Offset Greenhouse Gas Emissions initiative. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]) University participation in all A2Zero programs reduces the required community-wide offset purchases from approximately 925,000 MTCO2e annually to approximately 300,000 MTCO2e annually. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The University of Michigan convened the President's Commission on Carbon Neutrality to work with community and regional partners toward shared carbon neutrality goals, with Ann Arbor city staff included in the commission ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Ann Arbor City Council directed the City Administrator to seek collaboration with the University of Michigan and the PCCN in creating and realizing the 2030 Carbon Neutral Ann Arbor Plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
