The Ann Arbor City Council unanimously declared a climate emergency and directed the City to immediately begin crafting an aggressive and living strategy to achieve community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030. ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]])

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
Ann Arbor City Council unanimously adopted the Climate Emergency Declaration on November 4, 2019, committing the community to achieving carbon neutrality by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The Council also directed the creation of the [[initiatives/a2zero-carbon-neutrality-plan|A2Zero Carbon Neutrality Plan]], which was developed with input from thousands of Ann Arborites over a four-month planning process ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The [[actors/ann-arbor-city-council|Ann Arbor City Council]] passed Resolution 19-2103 in November 2019, directing the City to develop a living plan for community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The Council called for the plan to be developed with broad public input and presented by Earth Day 2020 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).

<!-- source: cap-2020 -->
The Ann Arbor City Council adopted the Climate Action Plan in 2012, committing to reduce community-wide greenhouse gas emissions by 8% by 2015, 25% by 2025, and 90% by 2050 relative to a year 2000 baseline ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). On November 4, 2019, the Council passed a resolution declaring a climate emergency and directing the City Administration to develop the 2030 Carbon Neutral Ann Arbor Plan ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The resolution was sponsored by Mayor Taylor and eight councilmembers: Bannister, Smith, Ramlawi, Grand, Ackerman, Eaton, Nelson, and Griswold ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
