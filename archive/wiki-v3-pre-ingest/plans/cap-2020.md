title: Ann Arbor's Living Carbon Neutrality Plan

The A²ZERO Living Carbon Neutrality Plan is Ann Arbor's comprehensive roadmap to achieve community-wide carbon neutrality by 2030, targeting the elimination of 2.1 million metric tons of CO2-equivalent emissions—the city's full greenhouse gas inventory as of 2018—through seven overarching strategies and 44 specific actions at a projected total cost of just over $1 billion ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan was initiated following Ann Arbor City Council's unanimous adoption of a Climate Emergency Declaration on November 4, 2019, which directed the City to develop an aggressive and living strategy for community-wide carbon neutrality ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). It was developed by the Office of Sustainability and Innovations through the A²ZERO initiative over an intensive four-month planning process that incorporated input from thousands of Ann Arbor residents, with the goal of presenting the plan by Earth Day 2020 ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). The plan is explicitly designed to be living and iterative, grounded in justice and equity, and supported by over 60 partner organizations alongside City staff, with modeling indicating a minimum of 15 full-time equivalent employees needed for implementation ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Its seven strategies span renewable grid transition, building and vehicle electrification, energy efficiency, vehicle miles traveled reduction, materials and waste, community resilience, and broader engagement and equity programs, with each action accompanied by cost estimates, projected emissions reductions, and identified co-benefits ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]). Implementation is guided by an A²ZERO Investment Plan, Prioritization Framework, and Governance Model, and the plan accounts for the University of Michigan's significant share of community emissions while acknowledging that UM's own carbon neutrality plan was still in development at the time of publication ([[sources/cap/cap-2020|cap-2020]]).
