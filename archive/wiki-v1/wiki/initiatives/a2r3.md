## What it is

A reusable takeout container program. US-made, USDA-approved, microwave-,
freezer-, and dishwasher-safe containers are available at participating
restaurants; customers return them for cleaning and reuse. Managed through
Live Zero Waste. Implements the circular economy principle at the point of
food service.

## Year 1 (a2zero-year1)

A precursor reusable container pilot ran at four restaurants during the
Green Fare event — a single-event test rather than an ongoing program.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

A2R3 launched as a formal ongoing program at three named restaurants:
Zingerman's Next Door Café, Ginger Deli, and El Harissa Market Café, with
additional businesses to be added continuously. This fulfills the Year 1
commitment to expand the container pilot — see
[[../commitments/reusable-container-expansion|reusable container commitment]]
(note: that commitment page does not yet exist as a file; see strategy-5
page for Year 2 status).

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Maintained at four local restaurants (up from three at launch) in
partnership with Live Zero Waste. An EPA grant application was submitted to
scale A2R3 and upgrade the drop-off station. Partnership with Perpetual
began to design a broader reusable landscape for Ann Arbor. Washtenaw
County provided a $4,000 waste reduction sponsorship.

## Status as of Year 3

Active at four restaurants. EPA grant pending for scale-up. No usage
or waste-diversion numbers yet reported.
