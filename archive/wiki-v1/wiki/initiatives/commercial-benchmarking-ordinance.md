## What it is

Michigan's first commercial benchmarking ordinance, passed in Year 2,
requires large commercial and multifamily buildings in Ann Arbor to track
and publicly report energy and water performance annually. First reporting
deadline was June 1, 2022. Expected to generate average energy savings of
2–3% per year through transparency and awareness effects.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

Passed this year. Described as a landmark — no prior Michigan municipality
had a comparable requirement. The ordinance positions Ann Arbor as a model
for state-level adoption, and the city was actively working with stakeholders
to advance the 2021 international model building code statewide.

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Described as "Implemented" in Year 3, confirming the ordinance moved from
enactment to active enforcement. Still the first in Michigan. No compliance
rate or energy savings data reported in Year 3 — this gap persists.

## Status as of Year 3

Active and implemented. First in Michigan. Compliance/savings data not yet
publicly reported in the annual report series.
