## What it is

A city-owned microgrid project targeting Veterans Memorial Park and Fire
Station 3. A microgrid is a localized group of electricity sources and
loads that can operate independently from the main grid — providing
resilience during outages while reducing grid demand during normal operation.
This site would serve as both critical public infrastructure backup and a
model for future city-owned microgrids.

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

A $70K design grant was awarded to design the microgrid at Veterans Memorial
Park and Fire Station 3. A separate FEMA BRIC (Building Resilient
Infrastructure and Communities) grant was submitted to fund construction.
Year 5 priority: "Launch City's first microgrid and advance biodigester at
water recovery facility" — see [[../commitments/y5-microgrid-launch-biodigester|commitment]].

Note: the Veterans Park geothermal workstream from Year 3 (NREL technical
assistance) appears to have evolved or been superseded by this microgrid
initiative at the same site.

## Status as of Year 4

Design phase. $70K design grant awarded. FEMA BRIC construction grant
submitted (outcome pending). Not yet built.
