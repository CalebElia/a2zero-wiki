## What it is

A proposal to create a community-owned energy utility that would provide
electricity from local solar and battery storage systems installed on homes
and businesses throughout Ann Arbor. As conceptualized, it would deliver
100% clean, locally built, affordable electricity — positioned as an
alternative or complement to traditional utility municipalization.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

A proposal for the SEU was developed and a public engagement process opened
(a2zero.gov/a2seu). The project won the project-of-the-year award from the
Great Lakes Renewable Energy Association. Year 3 priorities list continued
investigation of both the SEU and traditional municipalization as pathways
to 100% renewable energy — see [[../commitments/renewable-energy-pathways|commitment]].

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Two major advances: an Energy Options Analysis was commissioned to determine
the financial viability of the SEU, traditional municipalization, and other
pathways to 100% renewable. A DOE grant was also submitted to formally
create the SEU; decisions expected mid-2024. The city also released a joint
RFP with the University of Michigan for 1MW on City facilities and up to
20MW at UM sites — a potential structural partnership for the SEU model.

Year 4 priority: bring a 100% renewable energy strategy to Council for
formal consideration by year's end.

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

The 100% Renewable Energy Pathways study results were published publicly.
In May 2024, City Council authorized a Phase II Municipalization Study.
The SEU is going before voters in the November 2024 election — meaning
residents will decide whether to formally create it. Year 5 priorities
list "Support education, engagement, and potential launch of SEU" — the
word "potential" indicates the outcome is not yet determined.

The DOE grant outcome (pending from Year 3) was not mentioned. The
Year 3 joint RFP with UM for 1–20MW also received no Year 4 update.

## Year 5 (a2zero-year5)

**November 5, 2024: Ann Arbor voters authorized the SEU with 79% of the
vote.** Work immediately began. The SEU is expected to begin offering service
approximately 24 months after the vote (late 2026). $5M secured to fund
foundational tools, structures, and preliminary staffing. SEU waitlist
launched at a2gov.org/a2seu; biweekly updates sent to waitlist subscribers.

OSI piloted "SEU open houses" with residents — a peer-to-peer engagement
model for neighbors to share information about the SEU. A toolkit is being
developed for replication.

Year 6 priority: "Stand up the Sustainable Energy Utility, getting
significantly closer to the SEU formally providing its first service
offerings."

## Status as of Year 5

Authorized by voters (79%, November 2024). Implementation underway.
$5M secured. Expected service launch: ~2026. Waitlist open.
Highest-stakes milestone in A2ZERO history — now advancing to execution.
