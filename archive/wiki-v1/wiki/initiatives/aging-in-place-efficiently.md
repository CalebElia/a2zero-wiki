## What it is

A program serving older, income-qualified residents through combined energy
efficiency, mobility, and home safety improvements. Designed to address
intersecting needs — carbon reduction and livability — for a population
that often cannot easily navigate standalone energy programs.

## Year 1 (a2zero-year1)

Program was in co-design phase: analysis and feedback sessions with
community partners, service providers, and program participants to shape
the model.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

The program moved from co-design to active service delivery, serving 19
older, income-qualified residents (a2zero-year2, Strategy 3). The Year 3
priority is to fundraise to expand — see
[[../commitments/fundraise-aipe-and-bryant|fundraising commitment]].

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Served 17 more participants with energy efficiency and home safety retrofits,
saving $45,800 in utility costs and reducing 113 metric tons of carbon
(a2zero-year3, Strategy 3). A program to confirm participants' legal home
status was created to help stabilize housing — an equity-deepening addition.

The pilot program has **closed** as of Year 3. Ongoing grant funding is
being sought to restart or scale it. Year 4 priority is "Securing funding
to scale the Aging in Place Efficiently program" — meaning the closure is
a pause for refunding, not a permanent end.

## Status as of Year 3

**Pilot closed; seeking funding.** Total served across Years 1–3: ~36
participants (19 in Year 2, 17 in Year 3; Year 1 was co-design only).
$45,800 in utility savings and 113 MT CO2e reduction documented for
Year 3 cohort alone.

Active and delivering services. Small cohort (19 residents) suggests the
program is still in limited pilot mode. No cost-per-household or energy
savings figures were reported — watch for these in Year 3 as indicators
of whether the program is scaling efficiently.
