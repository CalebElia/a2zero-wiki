## What it is

A city program to plant 10,000 trees as green infrastructure to improve
climate resilience, with explicit attention to underserved neighborhoods.

## Year 1 (a2zero-year1)

Launched this year. Over 1,000 trees planted toward the 10,000 goal (10% of
target in year one, if pace is treated literally — worth checking in later
years whether the pace holds, accelerates, or stalls). Grant funding was
specifically secured for planting in underserved neighborhoods, suggesting
an equity-weighted rollout rather than even distribution.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

Cumulative trees distributed since spring 2021 reached almost 2,500 through
FreeTree Giveaways and Community Tree Plantings (a2zero-year2, Strategy 6).
Two FreeTree Giveaways were held in Year 2 (fall and spring). Funding was
being investigated for additional Community Tree Plantings in fall 2022.
A tree maintenance and care app was developed to support recipients. Year 3
target: 1,500 more trees — see [[../commitments/ten-thousand-trees-1500-more|commitment]].

Note: the Dive Deeper section of Year 2 says "almost 2,500 trees...since
spring 2021" — this is a cumulative figure across both Year 1 and Year 2.
Year 1 recorded "over 1,000" trees; the difference (~1,500) is the Year 2
contribution. See [[../contradictions/city-facility-audit-count|note on
report cumulative vs. annual framing]] for the general pattern.

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Cumulative trees reached approximately 5,750 — Year 3 added roughly 3,250
trees, more than double the 1,500 committed and well above all prior-year
paces. The program was presented at the 2023 Partners in Community Forestry
conference and ArborCon 2023. A USDA Forest Service Inflation Reduction Act
grant proposal was submitted to fund a canopy assessment, private property
planting, a NAP management plan, and feasibility study for a city/county
tree nursery.

Year 4 targets the full 10,000-tree milestone (requiring ~4,250 more trees)
plus a survival study — the first explicit plan to measure tree mortality,
which was flagged as a data gap in this page's original notes.

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

Cumulative trees reached 8,903 — just 1,097 short of the milestone. A plan
for a survival study was developed; launch planned for early Year 5. A $1M
USDA Forest Service grant was won for urban tree canopy analysis, expanded
planting in underserved areas, and feasibility study for a city/county tree
nursery. The 10,000 goal is now a Year 5 priority. Year 4 did not quite
close the gap despite adding ~3,153 trees.

## Year 5 (a2zero-year5)

**10,000 trees milestone reached in September 2024 — years earlier than
planned.** Now at 11,000+ cumulative (2,250 additional trees distributed in
Year 5). Survival study underway. Program continues distributing trees.

$1M American Forests grant received for tree equity initiatives: updating
the community tree canopy assessment, piloting private property planting and
maintenance programs, ecologically-driven stewardship in parks, and feasibility
study for a city/county tree nursery and agricultural demonstration site.

## Status as of Year 5

**Milestone achieved.** 11,000+ trees cumulative. Survival study active.
$1M American Forests grant in progress. Program continuing beyond the
original 10,000-tree goal.

## Status as of Year 1

On pace if 1,000/year is sustained for 10 years, but no explicit multi-year
target timeline was stated in this report — flag as an assumption, not a
confirmed plan. Watch for an explicit completion-year target in later
reports.

## Things to verify in later years

- Cumulative tree count (is it reported as annual or running total?)
- Whether "underserved neighborhoods" funding is itemized separately or
  folded into the general count
- Any mortality/survival rate data (newly planted urban trees often have
  significant first-year die-off — absence of this data would be notable)
