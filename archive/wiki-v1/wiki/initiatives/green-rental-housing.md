## What it is

An expansion of Ann Arbor's existing rental inspection program, adding
sustainability standards — including appliance electrification — designed
to improve safety, comfort, health, and efficiency in rental units citywide.
Targets both carbon reduction and tenant equity (lower energy costs,
standardized quality expectations) while providing resources for landlords.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

Draft developed following a task force process that included tenants,
landlords, engineers, architects, and low-income representatives. Model
cities were examined; the city participated in regional cohorts. The draft
was in review at end of Year 2, with an expectation of proceeding to Council
in Year 3.

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Not mentioned in the Year 3 report — a notable absence given it was a
Year 3 priority. Year 4 re-lists "Finalizing a green rental housing initiative
and formally launching the program." This is now the third year this
commitment has appeared in priorities without resolution.

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

"Achieved significant progress on advancing the Green Rental Housing
program, with plans to bring the program to City Council for consideration
in summer/fall 2024." This is the most concrete progress reported in any
year — the program was in draft in Year 2, went unreported in Year 3, and
now has a Council timeline for Year 4. Year 5 re-lists creating the program
as a priority, suggesting it had not yet passed Council before the Year 4
report was published. This is now the fourth year it has appeared as a
priority without enactment.

## Status as of Year 4

Pre-enactment. Significant progress made; Council consideration planned
