## What it is

An initiative to study and design district geothermal loop systems —
shared underground heat exchange infrastructure that can serve multiple
buildings from a single installation. Two separate sites are in
planning: the Bryant neighborhood and Veterans Park.

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Two workstreams launched:

**Bryant geothermal:** Won a $580,000 U.S. Department of Energy Community
Geothermal Planning Grant to design a district geothermal loop in the Bryant
neighborhood. Also won a DOE planning grant specifically to design the system.
This is part of the broader goal of making Bryant America's first carbon-neutral
neighborhood — see [[../topics/bryant-neighborhood-decarbonization|Bryant Neighborhood Decarbonization]].

**Veterans Park geothermal:** Secured NREL Clean Energy to Communities
technical assistance to identify data needs and draft an RFP for a geothermal
design around Veterans Park. A separate NREL energy modeling course was
also completed by OSI staff to leverage NREL tools for clean energy planning.

Also referenced in Strategy 1: "Supported geothermal studies and analysis
at two future Ann Arbor Housing Commission (AAHC) sites" — indicating the
technology is being considered for affordable housing as well.

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

**Bryant geothermal:** Active design. The system now encompasses 262
households, County Mental Health building, Bryant Elementary, Bryant
Community Center, and City public works facilities. This is a substantially
larger scope than initially described. A $1.3M contract with Community
Action Network was established specifically for the Bryant decarbonization
project. Year 5 priority: win a federal grant to implement a portion of
the Bryant district geothermal system.

**City-wide geothermal:** A city-wide networked geothermal study was
initiated in Year 4 — this goes beyond the two original pilot sites and
signals a potential larger deployment strategy.

**Veterans Park/Fire Station 3 microgrid:** A separate but adjacent
initiative — a $70K design grant was awarded and a FEMA BRIC grant
submitted for a microgrid (not geothermal) at Veterans Memorial Park
and Fire Station 3. This appears to be an evolution from the Year 3
Veterans Park geothermal idea toward a microgrid focus. See
[[../initiatives/microgrid|Microgrid (Veterans Park / Fire Station 3)]].

## Year 5 (a2zero-year5)

**$10M DOE grant won** for Bryant neighborhood geothermal deployment — "OSI
and a team of collaborators successfully won $10,000,000 to support the
deployment of a networked geothermal system in the Bryant neighborhood."
System projected to reduce resident energy costs by 77% and carbon emissions
by 75%. **However: funding is currently on hold** due to federal policy action.
The City is pursuing resolution. This is the most significant external risk
in the A2ZERO portfolio as of the Year 5 report.

**Geothermal installed at Bryant Community Center** — a major physical
milestone. The center is now equipped with the system, taking a "HUGE step"
toward being the City's first living, learning laboratory of carbon neutrality.

Geothermal integrated into new AAHC affordable housing on Catherine Street —
expanding the technology to affordable housing.

City-wide geothermal feasibility study initiated with Stantec (18-month
timeline). Year 6 priority: advance the study and develop a deployment strategy.

## Status as of Year 5

Bryant neighborhood: $10M DOE grant won (on hold due to federal funding
freeze; City disputing). Geothermal installed at Bryant Community Center.
City-wide study underway with Stantec (18 months). Construction of
neighborhood loop not yet begun.
