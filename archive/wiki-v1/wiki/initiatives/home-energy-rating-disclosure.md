## What it is

A proposed program requiring disclosure of home energy ratings — likely at
point of sale or rental — to give buyers and renters visibility into
building performance. Complements the commercial benchmarking ordinance
(which covers large buildings) by extending transparency to the residential
sector.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

Draft advanced. Council consideration expected in Year 3. See
[[../commitments/home-energy-rating-finalization|finalization commitment]].

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Fully drafted as a policy for home sales at point of listing. Council
introduction targeted for summer 2023. Year 4 priority is to finalize and
formally launch. This is the second consecutive year it has been listed as
a Year-N priority — watch Year 4 for actual enactment.

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

Formally launched. Two assessors were hired to complete Home Energy Scores
for residents. The City became a Department of Energy Home Energy Score
Partner. This fulfills the commitment that had been pending since Year 2.

## Status as of Year 4

Launched. Active. Two assessors hired. DOE Home Energy Score Partner.
Fulfills [[../commitments/y4-home-energy-rating-launch|Year 3→4 commitment]].
