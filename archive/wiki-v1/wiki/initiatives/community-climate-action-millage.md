## What it is

A 20-year voter-approved property tax millage dedicated to funding local
climate action in Ann Arbor. Passed by voters in November 2023. Ann Arbor
became the 8th community in the United States to tax itself specifically
to fund local climate work. The millage creates the most structurally
significant and durable funding mechanism in A2ZERO's history.

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Voters approved the millage in November. OSI helped organize a UMMA session
on the millage, voting, and democracy as part of the lead-up. Year 4
programming funded by the millage includes a series of residential rebates
and incentive programs — see [[../commitments/y4-residential-rebates-launch|commitment]].

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

First year of millage-funded programming. $4M+ authorized for residential
rebates — a 3-year contract with Elevate to design and launch a Home Energy
Rebate program (launching summer 2024). The city also set a Year 5 target
of raising 1:1 matching grants to double the effective millage capital.
The Sustaining Ann Arbor Together grant program was formalized with $80K
granted to community organizations. Year 5 priority: "Continue grant
fundraising with a goal of 1:1 match of the Community Climate Action
Millage."

The millage has NOT yet unlocked the three longest-stalled commitments
(Green Rental Housing, Aging in Place Efficiently, LMI Solar) — though GRH
is closest to Council adoption.

## Status as of Year 4

Active. First $4M+ deployed (rebates). Year 5 goal: 1:1 matching grants.
Key question: will it unlock GRH, AIP, and LMI Solar? Track through Year 5.
