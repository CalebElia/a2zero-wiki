## What it is

A City-led process to establish a new heating franchise — a regulatory
mechanism to ensure all Ann Arborites have access to safe, healthy, clean,
and affordable heating and cooling options. This is structurally distinct
from (but related to) the Sustainable Energy Utility: the franchise governs
who can provide heating service and on what terms, while the SEU would be
a new provider. The franchise process could restrict or phase out fossil
gas distribution in the long term.

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

OSI launched the initiative with a new public website, community listening
sessions, a request for ideas, and a public comment email
(franchisefeedback@a2gov.org). Still in the public engagement and
information-gathering phase at end of Year 3.

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

Continued work on the City process. No final agreement yet.

## Year 5 (a2zero-year5)

**Signed on Earth Day, April 22, 2025.** The Ann Arbor–DTE heating franchise
is the first in the country to include climate commitments. It includes:
- An Agreement Regarding Climate Action (ARCA) committing both parties to
  cooperate in support of A2ZERO and DTE's climate goals
- A 2-year work plan specifying concrete actions DTE and Ann Arbor will
  take to advance climate action

This closes a multi-year effort that began with public engagement in Year 3.
It is the most structurally significant agreement between the City and DTE
in A2ZERO's history — placing climate obligations directly in the regulatory
framework governing gas distribution.

## Status as of Year 5

**Signed.** Earth Day 2025. First climate-commitment heating franchise in
the country. 2-year ARCA work plan active.
