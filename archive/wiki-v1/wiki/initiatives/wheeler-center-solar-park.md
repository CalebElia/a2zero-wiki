## What it is

A 20MW solar installation on the former Ann Arbor landfill and surrounding
areas, commonly called the Wheeler Center Solar Park. It is the site of
DTE's first community solar offering, secured via a utility settlement in
Year 2. The scale (20MW) would make it the largest single solar project in
the A2ZERO portfolio by a wide margin.

## Year 1 (a2zero-year1)

Feasibility study conducted for a "24MW solar installation on a capped
landfill." An RFP was issued for rooftop solar at thirteen municipal
facilities — this is a separate but parallel initiative. The 24MW figure
in Year 1 vs. the 20MW figure in Year 2 may represent a scope refinement
during design; see [[../contradictions/wheeler-center-mw-discrepancy|potential
discrepancy note]] if this is flagged during Year 3 ingest.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

Final design for the 20MW solar field was completed. Critically, the city
secured a community solar offering tied to this site through utility case
U-20713 settlement with DTE — making this the first community solar
program in DTE's service territory. This simultaneously fulfills the
[[../commitments/community-solar-pilot|community solar pilot commitment]]
made in Year 1. The Year 3 priority is to break ground.

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Still in progress — "Continued working on a project to create a 20MW solar
installation on the City's capped landfill, which, when complete, would be
one of the largest landfill solar projects in the nation." No groundbreaking
in Year 3. Year 4 carries the groundbreaking commitment forward for the
second consecutive year — see [[../commitments/y4-wheeler-center-groundbreaking|commitment]].

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

No groundbreaking occurred. The city is "continuing to strategize (and
adjusting plan)" — suggesting a design or financing pivot rather than just
construction delay. In Year 4 a $5M federal award was secured to advance
solar at and around the former landfill site, the largest single infusion
of capital for this project to date. Year 5 priority carries the project
forward with language about making Bryant CC carbon neutral and achieving
"solar at every viable city facility or have a plan." The Wheeler Center
park is implicitly part of that ambition but no groundbreaking timeline
was set.

## Status as of Year 4

Pre-construction; fourth year without breaking ground. Design complete
(since Year 2). Strategy is being reconsidered. $5M award secured.
Community solar offering established via U-20713 settlement (Year 2).
See [[../contradictions/wheeler-center-mw-discrepancy|24 vs 20MW discrepancy]].
