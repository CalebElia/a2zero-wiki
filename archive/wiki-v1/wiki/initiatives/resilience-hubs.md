## What it is

A network of community resilience hubs — facilities equipped with solar
and battery backup capable of providing power and services during grid
outages or climate emergencies. Sited in community anchor institutions to
maximize accessibility, particularly for residents without home backup power.

## Year 1 (a2zero-year1)

Northside Community Center hub opened, featuring solar-powered battery
storage capable of 3 days of backup power. Funding was secured for a
second hub.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

The Northside hub remained operational — it was activated during a real
5-day power outage in August 2021, demonstrating real-world function.
Second and third hubs were designed: Bryant Community Center and Ann Arbor
Senior Center. Both are targeted for implementation in Year 3 — see
[[../commitments/resilience-hubs-2-and-3|commitment]]. A resilience
assessment was also conducted with Avalon Housing to support the resilience
of affordable housing providers.

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Hub 2 (Bryant Community Center) was completed. The Northside hub was
activated a second time during a February 2023 ice storm (5-day emergency
shelter). Hub 3 (Ann Arbor Senior Center) was not completed — Year 4 now
targets hubs 3 AND 4 together. An EPA grant was applied for to formally
launch a county-wide resilience network and expand the hub concept beyond
the City.

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

Hub 3 and Hub 4 sites identified: Peace Neighborhood Center and AAHC's
Green Baxter Court community center. Note: the Year 2 plan designated Ann
Arbor Senior Center as Hub 3 — in Year 4 it instead received a battery
backup system, and Peace Neighborhood Center was named as the actual Hub 3
site. Initial engagement and planning underway at both new sites; neither
is built yet.

Bryant Community Center (Hub 2) was upgraded to 3-day off-grid capacity
(solar + battery). The $1M EPA Environmental Justice Government-to-Government
grant was won to launch a Model Regional Resilience Network and expand hubs
county-wide. A mobile nanogrid was purchased for education and community use.

## Year 5 (a2zero-year5)

**Green Baxter Court (Hub 4):** RFP sent for installation of solar and
battery storage. Advancing toward construction.

**Peace Neighborhood Center (Hub 3):** LED lighting retrofit and HVAC
controls scheduled; OSI supported prep for future solar and energy storage;
sustainability integrated into STEAM summer programming.

**EPA $1M Regional Resilience Network grant terminated** by federal
administration. City is actively disputing the termination. This grant was
meant to expand hubs county-wide.

Food distribution at resilience hubs continued throughout Year 5.

County Resilience Network meetings co-hosted with Washtenaw County Resilience
Office — the county-wide network is advancing despite federal funding loss.

## Status as of Year 5

Hub 1 (Northside): operational. Hub 2 (Bryant): operational + geothermal
installed. Hub 3 (Peace Neighborhood Center): prepping; not yet operational.
Hub 4 (Green Baxter Court): RFP for solar/storage sent; not yet built.
$1M EPA expansion grant terminated (City disputing).
