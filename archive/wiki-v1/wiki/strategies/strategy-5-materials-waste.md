## Goal

Change how the community uses, reuses, and disposes of materials.

## Year 1 (a2zero-year1)

The smallest strategy by reported activity. The city piloted a reusable
container program with four restaurants during the Green Fare event, aimed
at cutting food-container waste — see
[[../initiatives/reusable-container-pilot|Reusable Container Pilot]]. The
Public Works Department also co-published a circular economy paper in the
journal *Sustainability*.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

Year 2 was a substantial expansion of this strategy relative to Year 1's
two-item report. The reusable container pilot scaled into a formal named
program: A2ZERO Reduce, Reuse, Return (A2R3), offering returnable takeout
containers at three named restaurants (Zingerman's Next Door Café, Ginger
Deli, El Harissa Market Café), with additional businesses to be added
continuously — see [[../initiatives/a2r3|A2R3 Program]]. This fulfills
the Year 1 commitment to expand the container pilot.

Year-round residential composting launched, and Saturday downtown
commercial recycling service was set up to begin July 1, 2022. A
sustainable food purchasing policy was adopted by City Council on March
21, 2022, and implementation work began.

The city made heavy investments in circular economy infrastructure: a
circular economy working group, a GIS map of 100+ circular businesses, a
city circular economy website, participation in Michigan's NextCycle I2P3
challenge, partnership with ICLEI on circular economy metrics, academic
and interview-based research, and promotional materials and videos in
development.

An A2ZERO Waste Challenge (Resident Race to Zero) reached 829 participating
households across multiple zero-waste week events held three times per year.
A student-focused zero-waste challenge is planned for Year 3.

**Prior-year commitment check:**
- Reusable container pilot expansion → fulfilled (A2R3 launched as a
  formal program with named restaurant partners)

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Year 3 was OSI's self-described "word of the year" for circularity, with
the most activity this strategy has seen. A sustainable food framework rough
draft was developed with a working group of local business owners and City
staff, and the city is working to implement it — fulfilling the Year 2
commitment partially. Food events included a Plant-Based Challenge (~200
participants, 300+ attendees, 8 restaurants), a Local Food Festival (17
businesses/nonprofits, 300+ residents), and a virtual Holiday cooking
demonstration.

The A2R3 returnable container program was maintained at four restaurants
(up from three at Year 2 launch) in partnership with Live Zero Waste. An
EPA grant was applied for to scale A2R3 and upgrade the drop-off station.
The university student zero-waste challenge launched, fulfilling the Year 2
commitment.

Major circular economy infrastructure built this year: three co-design
working sessions with residents, a circular economy strategy co-design
process underway (but strategy not yet finalized — carried to Year 4),
updated GIS map, two new video series episodes, a decluttering/moving
database, three neighborhood swap days with a replication guide, and a
waste-diversion collaboration with The Ecology Center across five areas.

The Greenbelt reached 7,600 permanently protected acres — the first
mention of Greenbelt in a Strategy 5 context, with a focus on sustainable
agriculture and land protection as part of the circular economy.

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

Year 4 significantly expanded the circular economy programming through
multi-year contracts. A 3-year contract with ZeroWaste.org launched for
broad CE engagement. A 3-year contract with VegMichigan launched for
plant-forward food — 50+ events, 2,600 attendees, 529 residents completing
a 30-day plant-based pledge (93% maintained changes afterward). The Ann
Arbor Area Sustainable Food Business Coalition (SFBC) launched to coordinate
the private sector. A circular economy planning initiative launched with
a resident advisory commission, targeting a CE development strategy within
12–18 months — the closest the plan has come to producing the strategy
committed since Year 2.

The A2R3 container program continues, with a scale-up strategy in development.
Neighborhood Swap Days reused 6,800+ items. Move-in/move-out materials were
diverted at UM. "More Life, Less Stuff" social marketing campaign launched.
A second electric refuse truck was purchased (Public Works). A city code
change enabled curbside placement of household items for neighbor pickup.

## Year 5 (a2zero-year5)

**10,000 Trees milestone achieved!** Reached September 2024 — "years
earlier than planned." Now at 11,000+ cumulative. This milestone moves from
Strategy 5 to its own celebration. See [[../initiatives/10000-trees-initiative|10,000 Trees Initiative]].

Circular economy: Grant funding received to create a formal circular economy
action plan. RFP released for firms to engage businesses in action planning
(RFP 25-23). Resident Advisor Committee co-designed and conducted a
community survey. First draft of Ann Arbor Sustainable Food Framework
completed. Sustainable Food Business Coalition (40+ businesses) completed
first full year. City-wide returnable container program explored and grant
submitted — but the grant was **terminated by the federal administration**.

5 Neighborhood Swap Days kept 10,000+ items out of landfill (up from 6,800
items in Year 4). 3 Electronics Collection events (6,000+ pounds per event).
New municipal wood waste recycling program launched with Urban Ashes — first
in Michigan. Move Out Ann Arbor (MOAA) coalition's inaugural year diverted
34 tons of material during UM student move-out.

VegMichigan reached 1,700 Ann Arbor residents; 400 tried plant-based eating
for the first time via the 30-day pledge. A2ZERO Food Waste Challenge: 540
residents. 3rd Annual Local Food Fest with hundreds in attendance. A2ZERO
Community Cookbook in progress (70+ residents; publishing late summer 2025).
EPA grant received for small food retailer refrigerant management program
(75+ businesses; 13,074 MT CO2e reduction goal). Public Works hired a new
worker for commercial recycling expansion; compost expansion strategy underway.
