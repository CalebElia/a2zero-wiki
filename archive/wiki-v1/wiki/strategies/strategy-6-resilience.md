## Goal

Enhance the resilience of Ann Arbor's people and physical places against
climate impacts.

## Year 1 (a2zero-year1)

The flagship item this year was the 10,000 Trees Initiative, launched with
over 1,000 trees planted in Year 1 alone, plus dedicated grant funding for
planting in underserved neighborhoods specifically — see
[[../initiatives/10000-trees-initiative|10,000 Trees Initiative]].

The city also stood up a community Ambassadors program: 22 members trained
in Year 1, who went on to design and implement seven sustainability
projects in their own neighborhoods, supported by city-developed
educational materials.

On physical infrastructure, the city opened a resilience hub at Northside
Community Center featuring solar-powered battery storage capable of
3 days of backup power, and secured funding for a second resilience hub.

The city also began a research collaboration with the American Society of
Adaptation Professionals studying climate-driven migration into the Great
Lakes region.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

Year 2 continued the strong pace from Year 1 across the Ambassadors
program, trees initiative, and resilience hub infrastructure. The Ambassadors
program completed cohorts 2 and 3 (both in Year 2), bringing total trained
Ambassadors to over 50 — year 1 committed to cohort 2 alone, so this
represents ahead-of-schedule delivery — see
[[../commitments/ambassadors-cohort-2|Ambassadors Cohort 2 commitment]].

The 10,000 Trees Initiative distributed almost 2,500 trees cumulatively
since spring 2021 (through FreeTree Giveaways and Community Tree Plantings),
with two FreeTree Giveaways held in Year 2 — see
[[../initiatives/10000-trees-initiative|10,000 Trees Initiative]]. Funding
was being investigated for additional Community Tree Plantings in fall 2022.
A tree maintenance app was developed to support recipients. Year 3 target is
1,500 more trees.

On physical infrastructure: two AQMesh air quality monitors were installed
(Kerrytown Farmers Market and Bryant Community Center), with two more
ordered for Year 3. The Northside Community Center resilience hub (launched
Year 1) remained active; a second and third hub were designed at Bryant
Community Center and Ann Arbor Senior Center for Year 3 implementation — see
[[../initiatives/resilience-hubs|Resilience Hubs]].

Other Year 2 resilience work: a resilience assessment with Avalon Housing;
Master Rain Gardener training for residents; emergency preparedness shelter
staffed during a 5-day power outage in August 2021; 200+ emergency
preparedness kits deployed; a soil carbon calculator developed with U-M
students; and support for No Mow May.

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Hub 2 (Bryant Community Center) was completed — a major milestone. Hub 3
(Ann Arbor Senior Center, designed in Year 2) was not completed in Year 3;
Year 4 now targets hubs 3 AND 4 together. The Northside hub was activated
again during a February 2023 ice storm (5-day emergency shelter), demonstrating
the network's real-world value a second time.

The 10,000 Trees program reached approximately 5,750 cumulative trees —
adding roughly 3,250 in Year 3, more than double the 1,500 committed. A
USDA Forest Service Urban Forestry grant proposal was submitted to fund
canopy assessment, private property planting, and a feasibility study for
a city/county tree nursery. The program was presented at two national
forestry conferences. Year 4 targets the full 10,000-tree milestone plus
a survival study — see [[../commitments/y4-10000-trees-survival-study|commitment]].

Air quality monitoring: two additional monitors installed (total now 4 —
the two ordered in Year 2 were the ones installed). A data visualization
platform launched at a2gov.org/airdata. Year 4 still targets four more
monitors. The Pollinator-Aware Yard Care program (successor to No Mow May)
launched with 250+ registered participants. Over a dozen community organizations
explored creating a resilience network; an EPA grant was applied for to
formally launch it and expand hubs county-wide.

The Ambassador program grew to 85 members — up from 50+ in Year 2. The
next cohort is expected to push total past 100. Applications were open at
time of publication.

## Year 4 (a2zero-year4)

Trees: 8,903 cumulative — 97 short of the 10,000 milestone. A survival
study plan was developed; launch planned for early Year 5. A $1M USFS grant
won for canopy analysis, underserved planting, and tree nursery feasibility
— see [[../initiatives/10000-trees-initiative|10,000 Trees Initiative]].

Resilience hubs: Bryant Community Center formally established as Hub 2 (a
re-confirmation of Year 3 completion). Hub 3 and 4 sites newly identified
as Peace Neighborhood Center and AAHC's Green Baxter Court (replacing the
earlier Year 2 plan for Ann Arbor Senior Center as Hub 3 — the Senior
Center instead received a battery backup system). A $1M EPA Environmental
Justice Government-to-Government grant won to start a Model Regional
Resilience Network and expand hubs county-wide.

Two battery-backup Solarize bulk buys piloted: 37 battery backup systems
installed, saving residents $101,650 upfront. Bryant Community Center hub
upgraded to 3-day off-grid capacity (solar + battery). A mobile nanogrid
was purchased for education and backup power. A FEMA BRIC grant submitted
for a Fire Station 3 / Veterans Memorial Park microgrid.

Ambassadors: 5th cohort graduated at 99 members; 6th cohort launched,
marking the 100th Ambassador trained. New initiative: Green Energy Neighbors
program launched with Ambassadors. Nourishing Neighborhood pilot launched
in the Darlington Neighborhood. Science Communication Training held for
Ambassadors (with UM R.E.L.A.T.E.).

Air quality: six more monitors ordered (deployment fall 2024). Pollinator-
Aware Yard Care program: 396 registered participants.

## Year 5 (a2zero-year5)

**Ambassadors: 118 trained** (6th cohort graduated). 7th cohort underway;
graduation slated June 2025 (expected 148 total). 10 recruitment events held.
Youth Now Climate Action created in partnership with Leslie Science and
Nature Center and Neutral Zone — a group of high school students from Ann
Arbor Public Schools focused on environmental problem-solving.

**Trees: 10,000 milestone reached September 2024!** Now 11,000+ cumulative
(2,250 additional trees distributed). Survival study underway. $1M American
Forests grant received for tree equity initiatives, canopy assessment, and
city/county tree nursery feasibility.

**Air quality monitors: 8 more installed = 10 total** in the city network
(up from 2 installed by Year 3 end, 6 ordered in Year 4). Live data at
City dashboard. Pollinator-Aware Yard Care: 450+ registered (50 more).

**Resilience hubs — Hubs 3 and 4 advancing:**
- Green Baxter Court: RFP sent for solar and battery storage installation
- Peace Neighborhood Center: LED lighting retrofit + HVAC controls scheduled;
  prep for future solar/storage underway

**$1M EPA EJ grant for Regional Resilience Network — TERMINATED** by
federal administration. City is actively disputing.

Emergency preparedness: household preparedness seminar at WCC (30 kits);
Ambassador project at Avalon Housing (20 kits); ward party raffles (5 family
kits); plans to scale the workshop model with more community partners.
Sesame Solar mobile nanogrid arrived — providing education and backup power
at city events.

Washtenaw County Resilience Network meetings co-hosted. OSI accepted into
Center for Heat Resilient Communities 2025 cohort — but that funding was
also terminated; cohort collaborations continuing independently.

## Status as of Year 1

Strong first-year showing — this strategy has the most concrete, measurable
outputs (1,000+ trees, 22 trained ambassadors, 1 operational resilience
hub) of any strategy in the report. Good candidate to watch for whether
Year 2 sustains this pace.
