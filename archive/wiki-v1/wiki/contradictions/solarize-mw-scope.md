## Conflicting figures

The Year 5 report contains three different solar MW numbers that cannot be
reconciled without additional scope clarification:

**5.4MW** — "Our Solarize program reached 5.4MW of solar installed through
the program" (a2zero-year5, Strategy 1). This is the narrowest scope:
residential solar installed through the Solarize bulk-buy program only.

**6.5MW** — Dashboard figure: "Over 6.5 MW of solar has been installed in
Ann Arbor since the adoption of the A2ZERO Plan" (a2zero-year5, Dashboard).
This appears to be broader than Solarize alone — likely includes city facility
solar and commercial installations.

**11.88MW** — Dashboard figure: "The dashboard reports 11.88 MW of new solar
capacity installed" (a2zero-year5, Dashboard). This is substantially larger
and may include University of Michigan installations, county-wide solar, or
use a different geographic/entity scope than the other two figures.

## Why it matters

The A2ZERO plan's 78MW renewable energy goal requires tracking progress
accurately. If "11.88MW" includes solar outside Ann Arbor or outside the
city's direct programs, it would overstate progress toward the 78MW goal.
At 5.4–6.5MW, the city is at 7–8% of its 78MW target after 5 years. At
11.88MW, it would be at 15%. The difference matters for Year 6 planning.

## Best-guess explanation

- 5.4MW = Solarize residential only
- 6.5MW = Solarize + city facilities + some commercial (Ann Arbor-based)
- 11.88MW = All solar in Ann Arbor + UM campus + possibly surrounding area

The dashboard likely tracks all solar capacity within a broader boundary.
Resolution requires seeing the dashboard's data sources and geographic scope.
