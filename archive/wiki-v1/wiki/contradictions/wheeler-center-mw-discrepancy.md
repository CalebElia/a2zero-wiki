## Conflicting claims

**Year 1 (a2zero-year1, Strategy 1):** "conducted studies on the feasibility
and cost of a 24MW solar installation on a capped landfill" — and issued
an RFP for rooftop solar at thirteen municipal facilities as a parallel track.

**Year 2 (a2zero-year2, Strategy 1):** "Final design for a 20MW solar field
on our capped landfill...completed." No mention of 24MW.

**Years 3–4:** Consistently refer to "20MW solar installation" or "20MW solar
field." Year 4 adds a $5M award for the project.

## The discrepancy

The Year 1 feasibility study evaluated 24MW; the Year 2 final design settled
on 20MW. Neither report explains the 4MW difference. Possibilities:
- The feasibility study tested 24MW as an upper bound; 20MW was chosen as
  the optimized design based on interconnection limits, cost, or site capacity
- A phase of the project was deferred or removed after Year 1
- The two figures refer to slightly different site footprints (landfill only
  vs. landfill + surrounding areas)

## Why it matters

The project is confirmed real and active with $5M in federal funding secured
(Year 4). The 20MW figure has been consistent across three years, suggesting
it is the authoritative project size. The Year 1 24MW was likely a feasibility
scenario that was right-sized during design. Track Year 5 — if a final design
or construction contract is announced with a specific MW capacity, that will
be the definitive number.
