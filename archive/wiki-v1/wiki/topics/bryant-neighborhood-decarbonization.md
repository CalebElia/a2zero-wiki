## What it is

A neighborhood-scale decarbonization initiative in Ann Arbor's Bryant
neighborhood, run in partnership with Community Action Network (CAN). Cited
in A2ZERO Year 1 as part of Strategy 3 (building efficiency).

## Year 1 (a2zero-year1)

Described as newly launched in Year 1: "a decarbonization initiative with
the Bryant neighborhood and Community Action Network, to co-create a whole
home health, safety, comfort, and decarbonization assessment." No
quantitative output reported yet (no homes-completed count, no dollar
figures) — this year's report frames it as a co-design phase.

## Year 2 (a2zero-year2)

Year 2 significantly elevated the ambition framing for Bryant: the Year 2
report describes the goal as creating "the first fully decarbonized existing
neighborhood in America" — in Bryant — in collaboration with Bryant
residents, Community Action Network, Elevate, and the University of Michigan
(a2zero-year2, Strategy 7). This is a step up from Year 1's "co-create a
whole-home assessment" framing.

Concrete Year 2 activities: a whole-home decarbonization assessment was
developed and piloted in Bryant; a federal grant of $25,000 from the EPA
and $75,000 from the McKnight Foundation were secured specifically for
Bryant work; and $25,000 from Solar Moonshot was won to install solar on
2 Bryant homes. An air quality monitor was installed at Bryant Community
Center. A second resilience hub was designed for Bryant Community Center
for Year 3 implementation.

Year 3 priorities: continue growing momentum; fundraise for Bryant
decarbonization — see [[../commitments/fundraise-aipe-and-bryant|commitment]].

## Year 3 (a2zero-year3)

Bryant received significant funding in Year 3: $500K MI-HOPE grant (via
Community Action Network) helping 19 households with health, safety, and
quality of life improvements; $180K Energy Efficiency and Conservation Block
Grant to make Bryant Community Center a carbon-negative building and living
demonstration site; $580K DOE Community Geothermal Planning Grant to design
a district geothermal loop in the neighborhood. Hub 2 (Bryant Community
Center resilience hub) was completed.

The goal framing remains "America's first carbon neutral neighborhood." Year 4
commitment adds specificity: "securing funding to help 20 homes become fully
carbon neutral" — the first time a homes-completed count has been given as a
Bryant target. Bryant Community Center carbon-negative building is also a Year
4 target. See [[../commitments/y4-bryant-20-homes-carbon-neutral|commitment]].
